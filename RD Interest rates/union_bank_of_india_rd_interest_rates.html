How to Calculate RD in Union Bank of India?

The interest on Union Bank Recurring Deposit is compounded at each quarter.

This formula used for calculation of the maturity value is -

A= P x [(1+R/n)^(nt)]

Here,

  • A = Maturity Amount
  • P = Recurring Deposit installment
  • n = Number of times the interest is compounded.
  • R = Rate of interest
  • t is the Tenure.

Mr. Roy decides to deposit Rs. 1,000 in a cumulative RD account for a period of 1 year at an interest rate of 6.75%. According to Union Bank's applicable interest rates. How to Open a Recurring Deposit with Union Bank of India?

Existing customers of Union Bank can simply go for the online method - Internet Banking. Just log into the internet banking account and open an e-RD account directly linked to the Savings Account. Types of Union Bank RD Schemes

Union Bank of India provides different RD schemes to cater to the varied needs of customers. These are:

1. Union Bank Cumulative Deposit:

Union Bank Cumulative Deposit RD scheme is for individuals with a low-risk appetite and are looking to invest small amounts at monthly intervals. This is a cumulative deposit scheme and is specifically designed to cater to people starting from the grass-root level. This scheme is ideal for individuals and small depositors.

Moreover, the following people can also opt to open an RD and enjoy the benefits of Union Bank of India RD interest rates -

  • An individual who is a resident of India and holds a savings account with Union Bank.
  • Two or more individuals with a joint account.
  • A Hindu Undivided Family.
  • Minors under the guardianship of their parents.
  • Any Institution, Club or Society, etc.

People falling under the above mentioned category can choose to open the Union Bank Cumulative Deposit scheme. All they need is a minimum deposit amount of Rs. 50 to start their savings. They can deposit any amount per month in multiples of Rs. 50 to open the account under this scheme.

Features of Cumulative Deposit:

  • The minimum deposit amount starts from as low as Rs. 50 per month in rural/semi-urban branches.
  • Tenure ranges from 6 months to 120 months (10 years). One can choose from any tenure between the same.
  • One cannot change the number of installments; however, it can be converted into a term deposit feature by the depositor later.
  • Interest rates are compounded quarterly.
  • Loan facility is available against the cumulative deposit.
  • The final amount is disbursed a month after the last installment of the deposit.

2. Union Bank of India Union Identity Plus:

Under this scheme, one gets options like 'Step-up' and 'Step-down' with the help of these options, you can increase or decrease the amount of the monthly deposit. This scheme is ideal for individuals, proprietorships, partnership, etc.

Features of Union Identity Plus:

  • One needs to choose the start amount to open the RD account which ranges from a minimum of Rs. 100 and a maximum of Rs. 1 lakh.
  • The tenure of the deposit ranges from 6 months to 60 months (multiples of 1 month).
  • Interest on Union Identity Plus RD scheme is paid annually at fixed intervals, and are compounded on a daily product basis.
  • It provides the account holder with great options of 'Step-up' or 'Step-down' that it administers the monthly collection.

Step-up option:

In step-up option, as and when suggests, one can increase the monthly installment amount. One can increase it up to 10 times the base amount. For instance, if the chosen base amount is Rs. 500, then you can increase it up to Rs. 5000.

Step-down option:

If you have stepped-up the installment amount, you might want to decrease it. This is where the step-down option comes into play.

3. Union Bank of India - Term Wise Recurring Deposit Interest Rates:

The interest rates on Term Wise Recurring Deposit offered by Union Bank are mentioned below. Union Bank has shorter tenures starting from 6 months to 1 year. The interest rate is 4.8% for RDs with less than 2 crore deposits for tenure ranging from 181 days to 264 days. For 265 days to 364 days, the interest rate is 5.0%.

4. Union Bank Medium and Long Term Recurring Deposit Rates:

The medium and long-term RD interest rates offered by Union Bank of India are mentioned below. RD tenure ranges from more than 12 months to 10 years. The rate of interest offered by the bank for this type of RD scheme is 6.3%. For RDs with a deposit of more than 2 crore, the interest rate offered is 5.0%. For RDs of more than 2 crores, you need to contact the nearest Union Bank branch.

Note: The interest rates mentioned above are applicable for deposits less than Rs. 2 crore. For RDs with deposit of more than 2 crores, you need to contact the nearest branch.

Union Bank RD Eligibility

Individuals, minors (under the guardianship), and Hindu Undivided Families (HUF) can open an RD account with UBI. NRIs can also open an RD account through NRE and NRO accounts.

Union Bank provides RD account opening for minors as well, to supervise their finances under the guardianship of their parents.

Documents Required

Individuals require these documents to open a Union Bank RD account:

  • Identity proof such as Aadhaar, PAN card, Passport, driving license, voter card, etc.
  • Address proof such as passport, electric bill, telephone bill, etc.

Tax Exemptions

Investment in Union Bank of India RDs is not tax deductible under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act of 1961. Furthermore, the Union Bank of India RD interest income is taxed at the investor's marginal tax rate. Furthermore, if the yearly interest earned through Union Bank RDs exceeds Rs. 40,000, individuals need to pay TDS at the rate of 10%. In addition, interest income is liable to TDS. The Union of India Bank deducts TDS based on whether or not the investor has furnished their PAN card details.

Loan Against the Union Bank RD

The Union Bank of India offers the option of borrowing against recurring deposits. As a result, depositors are able to meet their financial liquidity requirements. It also saves the hassle of applying for a new loan.

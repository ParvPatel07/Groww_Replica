Bank of India RD Interest Rates

Bank of India offers Recurring Deposit schemes to help its customers save regularly and earn interest on their savings. BoI Recurring Deposit is a popular investment option for individuals looking for a safe and convenient way to build a corpus over time. The scheme offers flexible tenures and attractive interest rates. The minimum deposit amount for a BoI RD is Rs. 500 per month, and the tenure ranges from 6 months to 10 years. Senior citizens are eligible for an additional interest rate on their RD deposits.

Features of BOI RD Interest Rates

  • The minimum deposit amount needed to open an RD account in Bank of India is Rs. 500 (in urban and metro locales).
  • The minimum tenure is 6 months and the maximum tenure is 120 months.
  • The maximum interest rates offered by the bank to general citizens is 6.80% and 7.30% to senior citizens.
  • The interest is compounded quarterly.
  • Nomination facility is available as per the existing rules at the time of opening the account.
  • Bank of India provides a repatriation facility to NRIs.
  • Loan facility is available against the deposit amount.

How to Calculate BOI RD Interest?

The interest on the Bank of India Recurring Deposit is compounded at each quarter.

This formula is used for calculation of the maturity value is -

A= P x [(1+R/n)^(nt)]

Here,

  • A = Maturity Amount
  • P = Recurring Deposit installment
  • n = Number of times the interest is compounded.
  • R = Rate of interest
  • t is the Tenure.

For example, Mr. B, who lives in Delhi and is a retired person of age, decides to open an RD account with the Bank of India with a deposit of Rs 1000 for 5 years. The applicable interest rate is 7.00% p.a. In this case, the total interest amount at the end of the maturity period will be Rs. 2249 and his total maturity amount will be Rs 62249.

How to Open an RD with the Bank of India?

Bank of India's existing customers can simply open a RD account with an online method - Internet Banking. All they need is an active internet banking account and they can open an account online directly linked to the Savings Account. They need to select the appropriate scheme, fill in the details and submit the documents. In case of new customers, they need to open a Savings account first and then apply for the RD. For offline method, one needs to visit the nearest branch and do the formalities.

Types of Bank of India RD Account

Recurring Term Scheme: This system allows depositors, primarily those with fixed incomes, to save by depositing a preset sum of money every month for a set period of time. Quarterly compound interest is paid on deposits. The higher the interest rate, the longer the time.

Star Flexi-Recurring Deposit Scheme: This is a one-of-a-kind RD scheme that allows the depositor to choose the amount of the monthly installment. It is ideal for people who have variable incomes. The minimum monthly installment amount is Rs. 100 in rural and semi-urban branches, and Rs. 500 in urban and metro branches. The account's duration can range from 1 year to 10 years in 3-month increments only.

Who is Eligible to Open an RD Account with BOI?

The list for types of eligible individuals who can open a recurring deposit in BOI are mentioned below -

  • Individuals and Hindu Undivided Family
  • A joint account held by two individuals.
  • Minor account handled by legal guardians of an under-aged individual.
  • Differently-abled individuals.

Documents Required for BOI Recurring Deposit Account Opening

The following documents (any 1) are required for address proof -

  • Electricity bill
  • Telephone bill
  • Passport
  • Bank Statement
  • Certificate/ID card issued

The following documents (any 1) are required for identity proof -

  • Passport
  • Voter ID card
  • PAN card
  • Government ID card
  • Driving License
  • The Senior Citizen ID card
  • Ration Card

Bank of India RD Premature Withdrawal Rules

If the account is closed within the first three months of opening, the bank will not provide any interest and the principal amount will be refunded to the source. If the deposit amount is withdrawn before maturity, a penalty is levied by the bank as per the norms and the accrued interest will be reduced for the last quarter as a result. Partial withdrawal of deposits is not allowed.

Tax Exemption

Due to the pandemic, TDS has been decreased from 10% to 7.5% (effective from May 2020 to March 2021). If your yearly interest is less than the total exemption limit, you can file Form 15G or Form 15H to request that the bank not deduct TDS at the source.

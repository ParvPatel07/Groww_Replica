Features of BOB RD Interest Rates 2025

  • One can apply for a loan and avail of 90% of the RD amount.
  • Any family member or spouse of the investor can be nominated to receive the final amount.
  • The deposit tenure ranges from as low as 6 months to as long as 120 months.
  • Interest will be compounded quarterly and will be paid at the time of opening the RD accounts.
  • One can make minimum deposits in multiples of Rs 100.
  • One can go to any Bank of Baroda branch to avail of the facilities.
  • Quarterly and half-yearly RD facilities are also available under other schemes.

Calculation of BOB RD Interest?

The BOB RD interest is calculated with the formula: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt), where 'A' is the final amount, 'P' is the principal amount, 'r' is the annual interest rate, 'n' represents the number of times that interest has been compounded and 't' represents the tenure.

Bank of Baroda Recurring Deposit Scheme Eligibility Criteria

  • To open an RD account, one must be a citizen of India or a HUF (Hindu Undivided Family).
  • An NRI can also opt for BOB RD account through NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary Account) and NRE (Non-Resident External Account) accounts.
  • A person can also open a RD account opened in the bank of Baroda gives that his/her parents will supervise the finances under their guardianship.

Bank of Baroda RD Tax Benefits

Bank of Baroda and all other recurring deposits are taxable under the Income Tax Act 1961. The money you invest in a BOB recurring will get taxed as per the income tax slabs. However, if your income is less than the taxable amount, given that it should be less than Rs 60,000 per year (Rs 50,000 prior to FY 2019-20). Tax is TDS on your RD interest as per Section 194A of Income Tax Act.

Terms and Conditions for Submitting Form 15G

  • One must be an individual or HUF.
  • Only Resident Indians can apply.
  • Age should be less than 60 years.
  • The total interest from all bank deposits must be zero.
  • The total income from interests or the total income from interests in the previous year should be less than the minimum amount exempted from income tax for that year. Which is, Rs 2.5 lakhs for the 2023-24 (AY 2024-25).

Premature Withdrawal Facility of Bank of Baroda RD

Bank of Baroda provides the facility of premature withdrawal on their recurring deposits. There will be a 1% deduction on the applicable interest rate according to the RD scheme chosen in case the depositor decides to withdraw the deposit amount before the fixed tenure. A partial withdrawal facility is not available.

Nomination

Beneficiaries for Bank of Baroda RDs can be nominated by depositors.

Recurring Deposit Schemes Offered by Bank of Baroda

Bank of Baroda offers various Recurring Deposit schemes in which one can make an investment, viz:

  • Yatha Shakti Jama Yojana (Flexible recurring deposit scheme)

    Under this scheme, the bank pays interest on a half-yearly basis. The account can be opened with an amount as low as Rs. 100 (minimum deposit) while the maximum deposit can be three times the core installment (maximum Rs. 50,000 per month). The tenure ranges from 12 months to 120 months (10 years). 90% of the outstanding amount or the RD amount can be availed as a loan or overdraft facility. A 1% penalty from the applicable rate shall be levied in case of premature withdrawal.

  • Baroda Holiday Saving Recurring Deposits

    This minimum monthly contribution is Rs 100 under this scheme and the minimum/maximum tenure is 12 months. Interest accumulated in the account is paid on a quarterly compounding interval. 5% of the applicable rate is charged as a penalty in case of premature withdrawal.

  • Regular Income cum Recurring Deposit

    The minimum deposit under this RD scheme is Rs. 1000 and has a tenure range between 12 months to 120 months. Interest earned on the deposit is paid on a half-yearly basis and compounded quarterly. Under this scheme, no penalty is charged for premature withdrawal of deposits up to Rs. 5 lakhs given that the amount was with the bank for 12 months.

How to Open an RD Account at BOB?

Opening a recurring deposit account with the Bank of Baroda is a hassle-free process. One can open an RD account with BOB both in an online and offline way.

Online method - This method can only be used if someone already has an active BOB internet banking account. To open an RD account, all you need to do is log in to net banking and select the option to open a new recurring deposit account. Once you select that, you need to fill the enrollment amount and select the tenure. Also, one needs to select the account to link to the RD account i.e. current account or savings account.

Offline method - To apply for the Bank of Baroda Recurring Deposit Scheme offline, you have to fill up the application form. You can obtain this form either from your nearest BOB branch or access it online. Meet the eligibility and provide necessary documents along with the application form for RD account opening at the bank.

While account opening for all other RDs can be done through a unified application form, to open your Flexible RD account with BOB, you will be required to fill the Yatha Shadi Jama Yojana application form.

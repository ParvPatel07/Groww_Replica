Central Bank of India RD Interest Rates

Central Bank of India offers its customers a recurring deposit scheme that allows them to save regularly and earn interest on their deposits. The Cent Recurring Deposit scheme is a popular choice for individuals looking for a flexible and convenient way to save for their financial goals. The scheme offers attractive interest rates and flexible tenures ranging from 6 months to 10 years. Customers can open an RD account with a minimum deposit amount of Rs. 100 per month. Senior citizens are eligible for an additional interest rate on their RD deposits.

How to Calculate Central Bank of India RD Interest?

The interest on Central Bank of India RD is calculated based on the principal amount, interest rate, and tenure. The interest is compounded annually. You can use an online Central Bank of India RD calculator to estimate the maturity amount.

The formula for calculating the maturity value is:

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Here,

  • A = maturity amount
  • P = recurring deposit amount
  • r = annual interest rate
  • n = number of times the interest is compounded annually (which is 1 for annual compounding)
  • t = tenure in years

For example, Mr. Ashok, who lives in Madhya Pradesh and is below 60 years of age, opens an RD account with the Central Bank of India with a deposit of Rs 5000 for 5 years and the applicable interest rate for him is 6.50% p.a. Hence, upon maturity, the total amount he will get is Rs. 3,46,885, and his interest earned is Rs. 46,885.

If you are a senior citizen, the applicable interest rate would be 7.00% and your maturity amount would be Rs. 3,53,840.

You can take the help of the online Central Bank of India RD calculator. The process is very simple, you just have to fill in the basic details of your RD account such as the amount, tenure, and rate of interest.

How to Open an RD with the Central Bank of India?

Central Bank of India's existing customers can open an account online with the help of Internet Banking. All they need is an active internet banking account and they can open an account online directly linked to the Savings Account. They need to select the appropriate scheme, fill in the details and submit the documents. In case of new customers, they need to open a Savings account first and then apply for the RD. For offline method, one needs to visit the nearest branch and do the formalities.

Types of Central Bank of India RD Schemes

Central Bank of India offers 2 types of recurring deposit schemes to cater to different people with varying needs.

1. Recurring Deposit Scheme: This scheme is specifically designed for people with regular income. One of the primary reasons which make this scheme stand out is any minor above 10 years of age who can read and write can open an account. The minimum monthly deposit is Rs. 100. There is no limit on the maximum deposit amount. However, if the yearly interest exceeds Rs. 10,000, moreover, it offers additional rates of interest which are as followed:

  • Central Bank of India RD Rates
  • Senior Citizens: Extra 0.5% p.a.
  • Staff/Ex-staff Senior Citizens: 1.5% p.a.
  • Staff/Ex-staff: Extra 1.0% p.a.

2. Cent Swashakti Flexi Recurring Deposit Scheme: This RD account can be opened with a minimum deposit amount of Rs. 100. Minors below 10 years of age can apply jointly with their guardians for this scheme. The maximum monthly deposit is Rs. 1 lakh. There are no penalties for irregular payments. The maximum duration of the tenure starts from 6 months to 10 years. Also, the bank doesn't charge any penalty for delayed installment or irregular payments.

3. Cent Lakhpati: It is a new scheme by the Central Bank of India under which one can earn an interest of 6.0% p.a. on the deposited amount. The tenure of this scheme ranges from 1 year to 10 years.

4. Cent Millanaire: Under this RD scheme, one can enjoy attractive interest rates for a period of up to 10 years.

Note: TDS will be applicable to whatsoever interest you earn.

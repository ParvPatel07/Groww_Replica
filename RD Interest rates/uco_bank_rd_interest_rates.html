UCO Bank Recurring Deposit (RD) Interest Rates

UCO Bank offers recurring deposits within its UCO Swdhan RD Scheme to its customers at an attractive interest rate of 5.50% on the deposited amount. However, senior citizens can avail a 0.50% additional interest rate over regular interest rates. One of the most preferred investment methods offered by people is Recurring deposits in UCO Bank. How to Open an RD Account with UCO Bank?

The account opening process of a Recurring Deposit account is easy and can be done using offline as well as online methods.

For the online method, individuals will have to open a Savings account with the bank in order to use net banking to apply for an RD account online. If you already have a Savings account with UCO bank, you can log in to your account by using your net banking username and password and open an e-RD right away. If you are not an existing account holder, you need to open one first. Once your account is active, you can log in to net banking and open an e-RD.

For the offline method, one needs to visit the nearest UCO Bank branch and submit the duly filled account opening form along with the KYC documents. The bank will verify the same and inform you when the account is active.

Eligibility Criteria for UCO Bank Recurring Deposit

The following are the criterias that can open a recurring deposit account with UCO Bank -

  • Resident Indian.
  • Two or more individuals can open an account jointly, each holding similar rights over the account.
  • Minors above the age of 10 years, represented by their legal guardians.
  • Institutions that are registered and joint companies.

In the case of jointly held accounts, each holder is the beneficiary for the other. It means, in case of the demise of one account holder, the other will be the beneficiary.

Tax Charged on UCO Bank Recurring Deposit

If the interest earned on all your RDs across all branches of UCO bank in a year exceeds Rs. 10,000, the bank deducts a 10% TDS. Moreover, if you don't have your IT PAN updated in the UCO bank records, the bank must deduct twice the TDS rate, i.e. 20%. Tax is levied as per the Income Tax (IT) rules and regulations at the time of opening the account.

Apart from TDS, you would need to add the interest income on the UCO bank RDs and other RDs as well, to your taxable income for that year. The income tax is payable based on your tax bracket. You need to be spontaneous and proactive and submit your Form 15G/ Form 15H to avoid TDS deduction on the earned income. You can also pay advance tax on advance tax payment date (15th Sep, 15th Dec, 15th Mar, and 31st March).

UCO bank issues Form 16A to its customers providing details of the deducted tax on RD interest, deposited by it with government treasury.

UCO Bank RD Premature Withdrawal

An individual can access their RD money if they need funds prematurely. However, in that case, the bank levies a penalty on the UCO Bank RD interest. The penalty is 1% less than the rate of the following - applicable interest rate on the date of redemption or the interest rate according to the period for which the deposit was held by the bank.

Types of UCO RDs

In the financial system, there are numerous deposit plans. They are classified into the following categories based on their maturity dates, deposits and tenures:

Short Term RD

The length of your deposit under this sort of UCO Bank Recurring Deposit ranges from 6 months to 2 years. This type of RD is most suited for people who want to build the habit of saving.

Medium Term RD

When you don't want to commit for a long time and aren't sure how much value you'll gain from RD, a medium-term RD is an excellent option. This type of RD is offered by the bank for a period of 3 to 5 years and offers higher interest compared to short-term RDs.

Long Term RD

When you want to receive a return on your investment but do not have the lump sum cash to invest in a Fixed Deposit, a long-term RD is the ideal option. The maximum tenure for a long-term RD is 10 years and it offers the highest interest rates. This type of RD is ideal for long-term financial goals such as retirement or a down payment on a house.

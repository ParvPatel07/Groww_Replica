PNB RD Interest Rates 2025

PNB, short for Punjab National Bank is a banking and financial service bank owned by the Government of India. Founded in 1894, the bank has grown leaps and bounds and has its headquarter in New Delhi. A PNB recurring deposit is an ideal saving avenue for people. One can open a PNB RD account by depositing an amount from Rs. 1000. The detailed interest rates are given down the page. PNB recurring deposits are categorised as short-term, medium-term and long-term.

Features of PNB Recurring Deposit Scheme

  • The minimum amount needed to open a PNB RD account is as low as Rs. 100.
  • The tenure ranges from 180 days to 10 years.
  • PNB offers loan against the deposited amount of 90% interest rate as per regular interest rates.
  • The maximum deposit offered by the bank is for a tenure ranging from 3 years to 10 years. The rates are 5.25% for regular citizens and 5.75% for senior citizens.
  • PNB offers a facility of loan against the deposit amount.
  • TDS deductions depend on the income slab to which the applicant belongs.
  • PNB also offers a nomination facility so that you can nominate somebody to collect the matured amount in case of the account holder's demise.

How to Apply for a PNB RD Account?

There are two methods to apply for an RD account at PNB:

  1. Online (Net Banking)
  2. Offline (Visiting the Branch)

Online: This is for existing customers of Punjab National Bank, they can use their net banking username and password to log in to their online account and open an e-RD right away.

Offline: Those who are not PNB account holders, will have to open a PNB bank account first by visiting the nearest PNB branch. Once the account is active, one can open an e-RD by logging in to their net banking.

How to Calculate PNB RD Account Interest Rate Investment?

The formula used to calculate the total amount accumulated upon maturity is: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Here:

  • 'A' is the final amount
  • 'P' is the principal
  • 'r' is the annual interest rate
  • 'n' is the number of times that interest has been compounded.
  • 't' is the tenure.

Suppose, Mr. Ravi opens an RD account with a regular deposit of Rs 500 on a 6.1% for 24 months or 2 years. The applicable PNB recurring deposit interest rate on the same is 6.1%, so the total deposit amount would stand to Rs. 12000 which is less than Rs 1 Crore. Interest on the same would be Rs 813 according to the formula.

Apart from getting the highest interest rates, RD in PNB is also eligible for tax benefits under the Income Tax Act of 1961. The money you invest in recurring deposit schemes is taxable under the Income Tax (TDS) at Source (TDS) of 10% is applicable on interest earned (provided, it is more than Rs. 10,000/year). For saving TDS on your RD profit, you can either fill form 15G or form 15H.

PNB RD Interest Rate Deciding Factors

The RD maturity amount you get at the end is based on the following parameters. Alternatively, one can also use the PNB RD interest calculator to check the answer yourself on their deposit.

  • Deposit Amount: The total amount deposited by a customer in their account. The deposit amount for PNB RD starts from as low as Rs 100.
  • PNB Interest Rates: Punjab National Bank offers a comparatively higher rate of interest on recurring deposits with longer tenure and lower interest rates for an account opened for a shorter period. The highest interest rate offered by the bank is 5.70% for the deposit amount.
  • Deposit Tenure: As mentioned in the previous point, low interest rates are offered for short tenure RDs and vice-versa.

Eligibility Criteria to Open an RD Account

To open a PNB RD account, you must fulfil these eligibility requirements:

  • Individuals, more than 10 years of age, can open an RD account by furnishing valid age proof.
  • Minors below 10 years of age are allowed to operate an RD account under their parent's/guardianship.
  • Even the municipalities, panchayats, and other quasi-government bodies can also have access to RD in PNB.
  • HUF's, charitable trusts, proprietorships can also open recurring deposit accounts.

Documents Required

To open a Punjab National Bank RD account, you are required to submit any of the following documents mentioned below:

(A) Identity proof

  • A voter ID card or Aadhaar card
  • PAN card
  • Government ID card
  • Birth card
  • A senior citizen ID card

(B) Address proof

  • Passport
  • Telephone or electricity bill
  • Bank account statements with a cheque
  • Any certificate or ID card issued by the post office

Components of the PNB RD

  • Premature Withdrawal Rules on PNB RD

As per the bank rules, the bank levies a penalty of 1% if the deposited amount is withdrawn before the maturity period. This penalty is also applicable on cases the deposits are irregular or discontinued.

  • Tax Benefits

PNB RDs are not eligible for tax breaks. The interest income earned from RDs is taxable according to the individual's income tax slab rate. Furthermore, if the annual interest income reaches INR 40,000, PNB deducts a 10% TDS (INR 50,000 for senior citizens). The 10% TDS rate is only applied when PAN data are given; otherwise, the bank deducts TDS at 20%.

  • Loan Facility

PNB allows investors to borrow against their recurring deposits. This will assist them in meeting their liquidity needs. Furthermore, it eliminates the need to apply for a new loan.

Types of Recurring Deposit Offered by PNB

PNB Recurring Deposit (e-RD): This RD is available to PNB internet banking users only. This e-RD account has eligibility criteria and premature withdrawal penalties. Once it is matured, the amount is credited to the same account through which the term deposit was made.

PNB Recurring Deposit Scheme: This RD account can be opened by any individual who has attained 10 years of age or older, as well as by parents/guardians on behalf of minors below 10 years of age with their parents/guardians as the legal guardian of a guardian. Not only individuals but partnership firms, Hindu Undivided Families (HUF), clubs, associations, societies, proprietorships can also open the RD account. A PNB recurring deposit account can be opened with an Amount as low as Rs. 100 onwards multiple.

PNB Special Recurring Deposit Product: Any government, private institution, or corporate body can open a PNB Special Recurring Deposit account. Any institution having a minimum of 10 employees or 75% of eligible employees whichever is lower is not eligible to participate in this scheme.

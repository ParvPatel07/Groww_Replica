Axis Bank Recurring Deposit (RD) Interest Rates

Axis Bank provides the facility of opening a Recurring Deposit and earning profits on the deposited money in the form of interest. The shortest tenure for Axis Bank RD is 6 months and the maximum tenure is 10 years (120 months). The Axis Bank RD interest rates range from 6.05% p.a. to 6.90% p.a. Senior citizens are entitled to get a preferential interest rate of 0.50%.

Features of Axis RD Interest Rates

  • The minimum deposit amount needed to open an RD account at Axis Bank is Rs. 500
  • The tenure ranges from a minimum of 6 months to a maximum of 120 months (10 years)
  • Axis Bank offers interest rates between 6.05% p.a. and 6.90% p.a. for the general citizens
  • The maximum amount can be deposited in multiples of Rs. 10
  • Premature withdrawal is allowed with a nominal penalty as per the bank norms
  • Tax Deduction at Source (TDS) on the interest income is applicable subject to the existing rules as at the time of opening the account
  • Loan facility up to 90% of the RD amount is provided
  • Partial withdrawal is not allowed on Axis Bank RD account

How to Calculate Axis Bank RD Interest?

The RD interest rates offered on Axis Bank recurring deposit is compounded each quarter.

The formula used for calculation of the maturity value is:

A= P x [ (1+R/n)^ (nt) ]

Here,

  • A is the Maturity Amount.
  • P is the Recurring Deposit
  • n is the number of times the interest is compounded.
  • R is the Rate of Interest
  • t is the Tenure.

Here is an example of the same,

Rahul deposits Rs. 500 each month for 6 months. The rate of interest applicable on his Axis Bank Recurring Deposit account is 6.5% (for regular RDs). The sum at maturity would be -

A = 500 * [ (1+6.5/4)^ (4*0.5) ]

A = 500 * [ (1+0.01625)^ (2) ]

...Rs. 3,032

Hence, the interest income from the Recurring Deposit is Rs. 123.

Important Note: The interest in RD with Axis Bank is calculated quarterly.

How to Apply for a New Axis Bank RD Account?

Individuals can apply for an Axis Bank Recurring Deposit:

  • Offline: This is the process.

Gather all of the paperwork needed to open an Axis Bank RD account and visit a nearby bank to request the application procedure. One of the Axis Bank employees will assist you throughout the application process. It is recommended that photocopies of documents be brought with you.

  • Via Internet Banking: This is the process.
  1. Navigate to Axis Bank's official website.
  2. Hover your cursor over the "Login" button at the top of the page. Choose "Login" from the "Personal" section of Internet Banking.
  3. Select "Create Recurring Deposit."
  4. Fill out the form that displays the depositor's and nominee's information, if applicable.
  5. Once confirmed, the deposit amount will be taken from the customer's savings account, and a receipt will be issued to the customer through email (for those who have registered for e-statement) or physically via post.
  • Via Mobile Banking: Here's the process.
  1. Access the bank's mobile application, Axis Mobile.
  2. Select the Open RD option.
  3. Upon confirmation, the selected amount will be deducted from the linked savings account and an RD account will be created promptly.

If you have registered for an e-statement, a confirmation email will be sent to your registered email; otherwise, a physical statement will be mailed to you.

Eligibility Criteria for Axis Bank RD

  • The applicant should be an Indian resident or a HUF (Hindu Undivided Family).
  • NRIs can opt for an RD account through NRO and NRE accounts.
  • In case of a minor, he/she can open an Axis Bank RD account under parental guardianship to supervise your finances.

Documents Required to Open an RD Account with Axis Bank

  • Individuals would require submitting these documents to open an Axis Bank RD account:
  • Documents that serve as identity proof such as Aadhaar card, PAN card, Passport, driving license, ration card, etc.
  • Documents that serve as an address proof, namely, passport, electricity bill, telephone bill, etc.

Taxation of Axis Bank Recurring Deposit

Under the guidelines laid down by the Income Tax Act 1961, all Recurring Deposits are liable for taxation. The proceeds earned by an individual would incur a TDS at the rate of 10% if the accrued interest exceeds Rs. 10,000 in a financial year. However, individuals who would not be able to furnish PAN will incur TDS at the rate of 20%. However, individuals can present their earnings against TDS by submitting a declaration under Section 80C. They can do this by filling 15G or 15H Form, whichever is applicable in their case.

Premature Withdrawal Facility and Rules on Axis Bank RD

Individuals can withdraw prematurely from their Axis Bank RD account but a penalty equivalent to 1% of the applicable interest has to be paid by them. Such a feature would help individuals to meet their financial crunch at a time of need. However, if they are closed within 7 days of opening, no interest is applicable. Moreover, if there is a delay of the monthly payments, the bank will charge Rs. 50 per Rs. 1000 a month.

Penalties

Customers who opened the recurring deposit after August 9, 2016, must pay a penalty of Rs. 10 for every Rs. 1000 in late payments. Existing clients who open an account after the calendar month has passed must pay a penalty of the current business's penal interest rate + Rs. 10 for the delay.

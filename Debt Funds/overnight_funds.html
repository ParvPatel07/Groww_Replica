Overnight Funds

Overnight Funds are a category of debt mutual funds that invest in debt and money market instruments with a maturity of one day. These funds are considered one of the safest investment options in the debt fund category due to the extremely short maturity profile of the underlying securities.

They are suitable for investors who are looking for a safe and highly liquid option to park their funds for a very short period, typically overnight, and earn returns comparable to overnight interest rates.

What are Overnight Mutual Funds?

Overnight Mutual Funds are debt funds that invest in instruments with a maturity of just one day. These instruments typically include overnight reverse repo, overnight index swaps, and other overnight money market instruments. The extremely short maturity profile minimizes both interest rate risk and credit risk, making these funds highly stable.

Features of Overnight Funds

Key features of Overnight Funds include:

  • Highest Liquidity: These funds offer the highest level of liquidity among all debt funds. Investors can redeem their investments on any business day and typically receive funds the next business day.
  • Lowest Risk: Due to the one-day maturity of underlying instruments, these funds have minimal interest rate risk and credit risk.
  • Stable Returns: Returns in Overnight Funds are closely linked to prevailing overnight interest rates and are generally very stable.
  • Suitable for Very Short-Term Parking: They are ideal for parking surplus funds for a day or a few days.

How Does an Overnight Mutual Fund Work?

An Overnight Mutual Fund works by investing in debt and money market instruments that mature in one day. The fund manager invests in instruments like overnight reverse repos where banks borrow from the RBI by pledging government securities and repay the next day. The interest earned from these overnight investments is accumulated in the fund and reflected in the Net Asset Value (NAV).

The portfolio is reset every day with new overnight instruments. The NAV is declared on a daily basis, including weekends and holidays.

How Should You Invest in Overnight Mutual Funds?

Investing in Overnight Funds is straightforward and can be done through a lump sum. Consider the following steps:

  1. Identify Your Very Short-Term Need: Overnight Funds are suitable for parking funds for just a day or a few days, where safety and liquidity are paramount.
  2. Choose a Fund: Research and select an Overnight Fund. Performance differences among Overnight Funds are usually minimal due to the nature of underlying assets.
  3. Complete KYC: Ensure your Know Your Customer (KYC) process is completed as required by SEBI.
  4. Invest: You can invest online through a mutual fund platform, a broker, or directly through the Asset Management Company (AMC) website.

Why Should You Invest in Overnight Funds?

Investing in Overnight Funds can be suitable for investors who:

  • Need to park large sums of money for a very short duration (overnight or a few days).
  • Prioritize the highest level of safety and liquidity for their funds.
  • Are looking for returns that are typically better than keeping money idle in a savings account for a very short period.

Risks Associated with Overnight Funds

Overnight Funds are considered to have the lowest risk among debt funds. However, they are still subject to minimal risks:

  • Interest Rate Risk: Due to the one-day maturity, interest rate risk is almost negligible.
  • Credit Risk: The risk of default is minimal as they primarily invest in instruments like reverse repos with the RBI or other highly-rated entities.
  • Liquidity Risk: While highly liquid, in unprecedented market-wide liquidity crises, there could be theoretical challenges, though highly unlikely.

Taxation of Overnight Funds

The taxation of Overnight Funds is similar to other debt funds:

  • Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG): If you hold the fund units for less than 3 years, the gains are treated as STCG and are added to your total income and taxed as per your income tax slab.
  • Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG): If you hold the fund units for 3 years or more, the gains are treated as LTCG and are taxed at 20% with indexation benefits.

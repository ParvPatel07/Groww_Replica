What is Low Duration Mutual Funds?

Low Duration Funds Invest In money market Instruments and debt securities so that the Macaulay Duration of the fund Is in between six and twelve months. These funds are ideally suited to low-risk Investors with a one-year Investment horizon.

These funds are also considered to be an alternative to liquid funds and ultra-short duration funds due to their longer Macaulay duration, but they do not Include any stock market or equity Instruments and thus are generally safer funds to Invest In.

Features of Low-Duration Funds

Some of the salient features of low-duration funds are as follows:

  • Open-ended fund: It is possible for the fund to be open-ended or close-ended. Investors can enter and exit open-ended funds anytime until the fund matures.
  • Moderate returns: These funds are for Investors who want a consistent source of income from their Investment, In addition to those with a shorter Investment horizon and a reduced risk profile.
  • Shorter lock-in: Short-term funds will be more liquid than longer-duration funds.
  • Low risk: Low duration Mutual Funds also pose low risk.
  • Easy to manage: Low duration funds do not necessitate ongoing fund management. The requirement for active management is substantially reduced once the Instruments to be Invested In are chosen based on values, Income generation and risk factors.

How Does a Low Duration Mutual Fund Work

The fund manager of a low-duration fund selects debt securities and money market Instruments according to the Investment objective of the fund, ensuring that the Macaulay duration is between 6 and 12 months.

Why Should You Invest in a Low Duration Fund?

The main benefits of Investing in low-duration funds are:

Moderate Risks

These funds have a moderate level of interest rate risk because they typically do not hold securities with maturities of more than 1.5 years.

Higher Returns

Low-duration funds typically outperform liquid funds because they can take on more credit and duration risk. These funds also tend to outperform other short-duration funds since they can earn bigger capital gains by holding longer-maturity bonds.

Taxation Rules of Low-Duration Mutual Funds

Being a debt fund, the taxation rules for a low-duration fund are as follows:

Capital Gains Tax

Before looking at the tax rates, here is a quick understanding of the 'holding period':

The holding period Is the amount of time an Investor holds the security. In other words, it is the time between buying and re-investing the units of the fund.

If the holding period Is less than three years, then the gains you earn are termed Short Term Capital Gains or STCG. For low-duration funds, STCG Is added to your taxable Income and taxed according to the Applicable Income tax slab.

If the holding period Is more than three years, then the gains earned by you are termed Long-term Capital Gains or LTCG. For low-duration funds, LTCG Is taxed at 20% with Indexation benefits.

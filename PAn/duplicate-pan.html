When you request the duplicate PAN card, the details remain the same as the original PAN card, with the same details of photo, signature, and PAN number. What is a Duplicate PAN Card?

A duplicate PAN card is a reprinted PAN card that is issued to you by the department if a PAN holder ever loses the card, it is misplaced, or damaged. The request for issuing duplicate cards can be made online or offline. The details on the duplicate PAN card remain the same as the original PAN card. If you have lost or misplaced a duplicate PAN card, you can have it re-issued by the Department.

How to Apply for a Duplicate PAN Card?

Obtaining a duplicate PAN includes a supporting procedure. The application can be submitted online via official websites (TIN-NSDL & UTIITSL) or offline by visiting NSDL or PAN service center. Online duplicate card application is on a duplicate account form and through steps.

Let's take a look at the steps for a duplicate PAN Card:

  • Visit the NSDL website, choose the application type "Change or correction in an existing PAN" from Request of PAN and specify the changes as re-issue (loss/missing). If you have lost, and lost, fire, forgotten, or stolen, you should request a duplicate PAN card.
  • Follow the instructions and submit it as shown.
  • Fill out the required personal details and mention the loss and details you entered on the previous page. Make a note of the acknowledgement number generated for the application. You need to provide the application info.
  • Attach the documents for proof such as "Personal Details" and scan the required docs of information for your PAN application.
  • Pay the application fee as per the guidelines.
  • Physically send the signed application, proof of payment, and documents (from the generated printout after the print), along with a passport-size photo, to the NSDL/UTIITSL address mentioned in the PAN application process.
  • Once the application is approved, a duplicate PAN card will be issued. PAN will be delivered to your address as per the application.

Documents Required to Apply for Duplicate PAN Card

Traditionally, the following documents are needed to apply for a duplicate PAN card:

  • Self-attested identity documents include: a photo ID, such as Voter's card, Aadhaar card, etc.
  • Address proof, such as a utility bill, passport, driving license, Aadhaar card, etc.
  • Proof of date of birth, such as a birth certificate, passport, matriculation certificate, etc.

How to Check Duplicate PAN Card Application Status?

You are able to track the status of the online application for a duplicate PAN card by following the steps outlined below:

  • Step 1: Go to the TIN-NSDL website to check the status of duplicate PAN cards.
  • Step 2: Select "PAN New/Change Request" from the Application Type" drop-down box.
  • Step 3: Enter the 15-digit acknowledgement number and security code.
  • Step 4: Press the Submit option.
  • Step 5: The status of the duplicate PAN card application will be displayed on the following screen. If you choose the application status for the duplicate pan, you can see the status, and track and view online.

How to Download Duplicate PAN Card Online?

To download missing PAN cards online, follow these step-by-step instructions.

  • Step 1: Go to the TIN-NSDL website first.
  • Step 2: Enter the acknowledgement number you obtained after completing the online duplicate PAN card application.
  • Step 3: Click the Generate OTP button to complete the process.
  • Step 4: Enter the details of your PAN as well as verify and call process. After that, click "Validate."
  • Step 5: To obtain your e-PAN card, select the Download PDF option.
  • If you can't recall your application acknowledgement number, you can still download the document with your Aadhaar card.

How to Surrender the Duplicate PAN?

It is illegal to carry more than one PAN card. A penalty of Rs. 10,000 is imposed on anyone who holds more than one PAN card. As a result, if you have more than one PAN card, you must surrender the duplicate PAN card immediately by carefully following the steps below:

  • Send a letter to the Assessing Officer requesting the return of the duplicate PAN card. Provide all essential and mandatory information in the letter.
  • Mention both the number of PAN cards owned and the one you want to surrender.
  • Submit the letter to the evaluating office, and you will be issued an acknowledgement of surrender. The acknowledgement number also works as verification that the PAN card you surrendered has been revoked.

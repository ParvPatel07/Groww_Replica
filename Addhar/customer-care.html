To address any Aadhaar-related queries, the government is connecting customer programs via helpline numbers in all regions with Aadhaar. Dedicated teams have established for smooth resolution of all issues.

The UIDAI has established a number of arrangements to make it easier for residents to access such services. People may thus register their problems or doubts by calling the Aadhaar card customer service phone number. These are available both online and offline, and are available for free.

In connection with the Aadhaar card, the UIDAI has a customer support system. Users can submit a complaint or make a grievance by phone, email, or by post. UIDAI also offers regional helpline numbers. Aside from these numbers, most of the organization's regional offices have a grievance redressal officer.

Online Through the Resident Portal

A complaint can be filed using the UIDAI's Public Grievance Portal (https://pgportal.gov.in/). The Centralized Public Grievance Redress and Monitoring System is the main arm of this website (CPGRAMS).

Please keep in mind, you must be a registered user to raise a complaint on this portal. If you are a registered user, follow these steps to complete the User Login process:

  1. Go to https://pgportal.gov.in/
  2. Enter Username/Registered Mobile Phone Number/Email Address.
  3. Please enter your security code.
  4. Click the "Login" option.

Once a new user, EID, or a registered user, you must complete the Registration / Sign-up Form.

After you've registered, you may file a complaint using the following procedure:

  1. Go to https://pgportal.gov.in/
  2. Then select "Login" to the public grievance site.

Grievance Redressal Process

Following submission of your grievance, the following steps are taken to remedy your issue:

  • Grievance is received and registered in the system.
  • Following approval from the Deputy Director/Regional Office, the problem is investigated and forwarded to the appropriate Regional Office or Concerned Section at the Headquarters.
  • The issue will be addressed online by the relevant Regional or Concerned Section.
  • If action is required on spot, the Regional Office or Concerned Section will provide details and perform action.
  • The Deputy Director examines all final actions.

The status may be updated, available through the Public Grievance Portal.

  • Directorate of Public Grievances (DPG),
  • Administrator of Central Public Grievances Department (DARPG),
  • Post Office,
  • The President's Secretariat,
  • The Prime Minister's Office,
  • The Home Minister's Office.

Aadhaar Card Customer Care Grievance through Post

Postal complaints can be directed through UIDAI Office or Headquarters. The contacts are investigated and then forwarded to the appropriate Regional Office/Concerned Section by the Deputy Director.

The Concerned Section or Regional Office then redresses the issue electronically, unless requested.

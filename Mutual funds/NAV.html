What is NAV?

NAV (Net Asset Value) denotes the market value per share of a particular mutual fund. It is calculated by deducting the liabilities from total net assets divided by the number of shares. One needs to gather the market value of a portfolio and also identify the current market fund unit number to determine the price of a fund unit.

Most of the time, the stock or mutual fund trading deals with its NAV, and as NAV increases the asset under-funds grows. NAV is the price at which the units of a mutual fund are bought or sold, and it is higher the better.

The value of an asset is most commonly used in case of open-ended funds. With these investments, the interest and the date of purchase and redemption are not fixed.

Why NAV matters: It helps investors with certain goals choose which one is a higher return investment portfolio by providing a reference value. Also, this lets you know where to start, making a financial bet. Let’s understand it in formulas:

What is the Net Asset Value Formula?

The calculation of the net asset value is quite easy to get through. One can also do this by using the NAV formula below:

NAV = (Total Asset – Total Liabilities) / Total Outstanding Shares

What is the Net Value of Assets for Mutual Funds?

Mutual funds, unlike stocks, are not traded in real-time. Instead, they are calculated based on the trading method and the market closing price of the underlying assets. The NAV is calculated as follows:

  • Assets: The assets of a mutual fund include the combined market value of a particular fund’s investments, like securities, cash, cash equivalents and other accrued income. The closing or last price of the value of such securities included in the portfolio, plus receivables, plus accrued income, plus the carrying of capital in the form of total asset and cash and cash equivalents, are all included under assets.
  • Liabilities: Apart from these, mutual funding may also forgo liabilities which can include short-term or long-term debts, payment pending to trading organizations and other various kinds that may be bank or auditor’s fees.

Liabilities can also include various accrued expenses including custodian, unit holder, operating expenses, distribution, management expenses, etc.

After total asset calculation and total funds, the quantum of the above mentioned liabilities and assets at the end of a period is actually taken into account.

How is the Net Value of an Asset Relevant for Investors?

Most investors prefer to check the net asset value to be aware as the price it usually shows. However, to know the actual returns, the investors also need to check the growth of a company and include dividend and bonus options in the calculation. When the calculation is done, the net asset value of a mutual fund is calculated on a per-share basis.

For example, if you have to represent a company’s working capital, value, and the net value of an asset, you quote a company’s total monetary worth.

Therefore, net value of an asset is necessary to make proper decisions about the funds in which investors choose to invest in.

How is the Net Value of an Asset Calculated?

  • Daily Net Valuation of Assets: Mutual fund investment companies evaluate the total worth of their portfolios daily. By the stock market’s close at 3:30 p.m., the market appraises the day with the close price of the previous day.
  • General Calculation of the Net Value of Assets: The general net value of assets is the price of all assets they are going to sell given by the cumulative cost of individual assets minus the liabilities. The market value of a particular asset is used to find the NAV and is then divided by the outstanding shares.

Role of NAV in the Performance of a Fund

Investors should not only consider the NAV while selecting a mutual fund to invest in. They should track the market value of the fund, the performance of the fund manager, and the fund’s portfolio. However, the net asset value does help in understanding the growth of the fund and the returns generated over time.

Real Estate Funds – Advantages & Features

Investing in real estate has been the most-acclaimed way of generating wealth around the world. However, the prices of real estate properties are high enough to deter most investors. If you are interested in the real estate sector and believe that this sector will undergo growth in the near future and want to invest in it, then Real Estate Mutual Funds in India are ideal for you. Here, we will talk about these funds, along with their features and the benefits they offer.

What are Real Estate Funds?

Real Estate Funds are sector funds that invest in securities of companies from the real estate sector. In other words, these funds provide the capital to the real estate company to develop a property. If the sector grows, then the fund makes good returns. A real estate fund might invest in real estate companies or REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts) based on the investment objective of the fund among other factors.

Who should invest in Real Estate Funds?

A real estate fund offers the investors an opportunity to earn good returns by investing in the growing real estate sector. However, it does come with its own risks and investors must research well before investing. Usually, investors without sufficient funds to purchase a property opt for real estate mutual funds. It is important to note that this investment requires a horizon of at least three to five years. These funds are not recommended to short-term investors. Also, you need to be aware of how the real estate sector functions to benefit from this investment.

Advantages of Investing in Real Estate Mutual Funds

  • Flexibility: You don't need a huge amount to invest in real estate funds. You can invest with a lower amount and diversify your portfolio.
  • Alternative to equity property: These funds allow you to participate in the real estate sector's growth without directly owning property.
  • Diversification: These funds are less volatile and are less affected by market cycles as they are spread across multiple properties and sectors.
  • Liquidity: Unlike direct real estate property, these funds are very liquid by allowing quick selling in property value times. This allows you to switch your allocation along the way as highly liquid assets also help you liquidate your position when the market is high.
  • Stability: When you invest in property, if there is a downturn in prices in your locality for any reason, then you can suffer losses. However, with real estate funds, the diversification in allocation and sectoral funds ensures multiple sources of returns across different areas.
  • Protection against inflation: When an economy goes through inflation, the price of property rises, and the sector increases too. This, in turn, helps investors shield the value of their assets at a macro level. Real estate funds hedge your generation against inflation.

Risks associated with investing in Real Estate Funds

  • Market Risk: These assets are highly dependent on the demand for properties (rental as well as outright purchase). If the market falls, the value of the fund will also go down and give lower returns. However, in a sluggish market, the fund's returns are also affected.
  • Interest Rate Risk: The interest rate cycle affects the real estate market since that is a direct impact on borrowing costs.

Features of Real Estate Funds

  • Investment Horizon: These funds are ideal for investors with a long-term investment horizon, as the real estate sector takes time to grow and provide returns.
  • Returns: Real estate funds can provide attractive returns, but they are subject to market risks and fluctuations.
  • Convenience: Real estate funds allow investors to participate in the growth of the real estate sector without the hassles of direct ownership.

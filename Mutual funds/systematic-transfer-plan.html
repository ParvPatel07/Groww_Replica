STP – Systematic Transfer Plan

What is a Systematic Transfer Plan?

A systematic transfer plan (STP) allows investors to shift their financial resources from one scheme to the other, especially based on varying market situations. These plans are especially rewarding, enabling investors to gain market advantages by shifting their assets between equity and debt funds. The gradual transfer of funds helps in reducing the risk of market volatility and ensures better returns in the long run.

STP is an automated way of moving a fixed amount of money from one mutual fund scheme to another, at fixed intervals. An investor is automatically allocated units based on the NAV on the date of transfer.

Suppose the investor has a lump sum amount. He can initially invest in a debt fund, because debt funds are less volatile and the returns are relatively stable. The investor can then transfer a fixed amount periodically to an equity fund, thus benefiting from rupee cost averaging and compounding.

Besides balancing the investor's portfolio, STPs also offer flexibility, because an investor can choose the amount, intervals and the schemes operated by a single asset management company, allowing him to diversify between multiple schemes offered by several fund houses.

Types of Systematic Transfer Plans

A client generally receives principal plus a bit of pre-existing interest in three types:

  • Fixed STP: Under this plan, the investor transfers only a fixed sum, that needs to be transferred on determined intervals as selected at the time of investment. The investor can choose the amount and the frequency of transfer, making it a flexible option for those who want to manage their investments actively.
  • Capital Appreciation STP: In this plan, only the profit or capital appreciation earned in one mutual fund is to be transferred towards a new mutual fund in another category.
  • Flexible STP: Under this plan, the investor can transfer any amount, depending on the market situation and his own financial goals. This plan allows the investor to transfer more when the market is down and less when the market is up, thus maximizing returns.

Features of a Systematic Transfer Plan

  • STP allows investors to transfer a fixed or variable amount from one scheme to another through systematic transfer goals (fixed interval).
  • STP can be started with a minimum investment amount, generally a minimum investment of Rs. 12,000 is eligible for STP in most mutual funds.
  • STP helps in balancing the investor's portfolio by shifting funds from debt to equity or vice versa, depending on the market situation and the investor's risk appetite.
  • STP can be stopped or modified at any time, giving the investor complete control over his investments.
  • STP is tax efficient, as the investor can plan his withdrawals in a way that minimizes tax liability.
  • Systematic transferring instances from a liquid fund to an equity fund does not attract any charges under usual load.

Benefits of a Systematic Transfer Plan

  • Higher returns: An STP allows an investor to earn higher returns on your investments by shifting to a more profitable venue during market swings, allowing smaller denominations to be shifted towards the market when situations brought about are in the investor's advantage.
  • Stability: STP reduces the high degree of volatility in the market, investors can transfer their funds with a low risk profile, allowing their investment channels such as debt funds and money market instruments. This allows an STP to offer the dual advantage of higher returns and lower risk, making it an ideal investment option for conservative investors.
  • Rupee Cost Averaging: One of the biggest advantages while investing in Mutual Funds via STP, (shifting investments from a debt to their average costs, as the investor is able to buy more units when the NAV is low and fewer units when the NAV is high). This helps in averaging out the cost of investment over a period of time, thus reducing the impact of market volatility on the investor's portfolio. Overall, rupee cost averaging reduces the risk of market fluctuations, thereby reducing the overall risk on the individual account.

The systematic transfer plan also serves as a perfect option to invest in relation of equity and debt instruments, to provide an optimal balance between risk and reward. It also provides an option to invest in a lump sum and transfer it to an equity fund periodically, to avoid market volatility.

STP is suitable for the aggressive investor. Apart from balanced plans to debt products, the product spectrum also includes, short-term, long-term capital gains and the option to transfer between two schemes, provided both schemes are under the same fund house. Long-term capital gains are eligible for indexation benefit, while short-term capital gains are taxed as per the investor's tax slab.

Who Should Invest in A Systematic Transfer Plan?

STP is ideal for investors who have a lump sum amount and want to invest in equity funds but are risk-averse and want to avoid market volatility. By investing in a debt fund and transferring a fixed amount to an equity fund, investors can benefit from rupee cost averaging and compounding. STP is also suitable for investors who want to balance their portfolio by shifting funds between debt and equity schemes, depending on the market situation and their risk appetite. It is also suitable for investors who want to minimize tax liability and maximize returns.

Things to Remember when Investing with a Systematic Transfer Plan

  • STP is subject to market risks and does not guarantee returns. It is best to have a long-term horizon, risk mitigation strategies, and realistic expectations while investing in STP.

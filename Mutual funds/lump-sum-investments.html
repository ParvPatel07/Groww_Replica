Lump Sum Investments- How is it different from an SIP?

This page helps you primarily manage a large chunk of money, in financial terms, in regards to the investment of a substantial sum of money at a go versus periodic investing over time via multiple systematic plans.

What are lump sum investments and lump sum payments?

In mutual funds, a lump sum investment means you invest the entire amount at once, as opposed to SIPs where you invest smaller amounts periodically. Lump sum is suitable for investors who have a large investable surplus and want to invest it in one go.

How is a lump sum investment different from SIP?

SIP or Systematic Investment Plan, lets you, as an investor, invest a fixed amount at regular intervals. The following points will help you understand the difference between SIP and lump sum investments:

  • Mode of Investment: This is the main mode, as it says you invest money in a mutual fund via systematic periodic investments (like SIP) or by investing a large sum at a go (lump sum). SIPs are ideal for those who want to invest smaller amounts regularly, while lump sum is suitable for those who have a large investable surplus.
  • Potential Rewards: SIPs are safe as an investment instrument. Therefore, it is best initially for the beginner, who has not yet inculcated a market and maturity sense of the same.
  • In contrast, a lump sum investment is usually profitable for the bigger player. They have been in the field for a long time and have enough experience in the market. Therefore, they also have a low-risk tolerance, while beginners should avoid investing in lump sum.
  • Discipline of Investment: SIPs instill a certain level of investment discipline in investors. Therefore, the time period of return is otherwise also different. However, in the case of a lump sum payment, the amount lock-in period is dependent on period of plans. Since this is no periodic investment, the amount is expected to be deposited and stays locked in.

Which will give better returns – lumpsum or SIP?

This answer is not quite as dependent on what is better, but due to the fact that the investment plan, as mentioned above, depends on market conditions and investor goals. SIPs help average out market volatility, while lump sum can benefit from a rising market if timed well. There is no one-size-fits-all answer.

Moreover, investment is an completely market-oriented. The market forces decide the benefits to be extracted from the invested. While the money remains invested, the market plays a huge return in turn.

Therefore, it is always advisable to consult a financial advisor and check the amount of investment, either from sum investment or SIP, before investing. The market is always subject to risk. Therefore, it is advisable to compare the returns and risks before investing.

Advantages of lump sum investment over SIP

  • Simplicity: Lump sum investments are more streamlined as compared to SIPs because in the former case, you invest and wait for your investment grow, whereas with SIPs you need to make regular contributions every month.
  • Minimum charges: Lump sum investments have lower transaction costs than SIPs. Therefore, they also have minimum charges, as compared to periodic investments via SIPs. This is because there are fewer transactions and lower maintenance costs.
  • Appreciation of capital: As mentioned earlier, Lump sum investments are meant for a longer duration, they have enough time to grow in the market. Simply put, the returns related to the lump sum investment get appreciated in the long run.

Factors to consider before investing in lump sum investment

Lump sum investments take large amounts at once; hence most investors often find it difficult to let go of the amount. Nevertheless, it becomes easier when you have considered certain factors beforehand for easy steering.

Patience

Patience is the key to handling any situation. This is what remarkably distinguishes the investors from the traditional traders. Investing and then waiting for money to grow over a period of time requires patience and perseverance

The temptation to exit from the scheme is quite high, but only patience will help you sail through the downtrend of the market condition.

Market valuation

Attaining a perfect market valuation is an arduous task overall; yet you can get hold of the same if you look into the past records closely. Before investing, you can look out for the P/E ratio of various market indices.

Potential returns

The prime aim of lump sum investment returns. Hence, it becomes vital to assess the returns beforehand, so that you get an overview of the scheme you are opting for. It is important to know the features of your investment plans and also understand the relevant returns that your scheme is enabled to provide you.

Liquidity expectations

The lump sum investment should also be able to meet your liquidity requirements. However, there should not be any considerable loss in amount. A lesser loss implies a better investment plan.

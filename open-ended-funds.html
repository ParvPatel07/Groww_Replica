What are Open Ended Mutual Funds?

7 min read  |  21 March 2025

Mutual funds can be categorized based on their structure into three types: open-ended, closed-ended, and interval funds. Open-ended funds are the most popular mutual fund schemes in India, offering high liquidity and flexibility to investors.

Here, we will explore Open-Ended Mutual Funds and talk about the different types of open-ended funds in India along with their benefits and a lot more.

Open-Ended Funds Meaning

In an open-ended plan, you can subscribe to and redeem units at any time from the fund house at NAV, without any lock-in period. These funds are always open for investment and redemption, and the fund size keeps changing as investors buy and sell units.

Open-ended funds are managed by professional fund managers and are ideal for investors seeking liquidity and flexibility. The NAV is updated daily, and you can invest or withdraw at any time.

Advantages of Open-Ended Mutual Funds

  • Highly Liquid: You can redeem units at any time on any working day, providing maximum liquidity.
  • Performance over Different Market Cycles: Investors can evaluate a fund's performance over different market cycles, as the fund is always open.
  • Availability of SIP: You can start a Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) in open-ended funds, making it easier to invest regularly.

Disadvantages of Open-Ended Mutual Funds

  • High Volatility: NAV fluctuates daily based on the market value of the underlying assets.
  • Huge Inflows and Withdrawals: Large inflows or outflows can impact the fund's performance.
  • Cash Flow Risks: The fund manager must maintain liquidity to meet redemption requests, which can affect returns.

Who Should Invest in Open-Ended Mutual Funds?

Open-ended funds are ideal for investors who want liquidity, flexibility, and the ability to invest or withdraw at any time. They are suitable for both new and experienced investors with varying risk appetites and investment horizons.

Taxation

Taxation of open-ended funds depends on the asset allocation. Equity-oriented funds (65% or more in equity) are taxed as equity funds, while others are taxed as debt funds. Check the scheme's offer document for details.

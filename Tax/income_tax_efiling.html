Income Tax e-Filing

According to Section 139(1) of India, any individual whose total income in the last year exceeds the tax exemption limit is required by law to file Income Tax Returns, or ITRs. E-filing is the process through which an individual files their income tax returns online.

As a taxpayer, you can seek expert assistance or e-file your own taxes by registering on the income tax department's or Fincept's website. Those who submit their tax returns through an online method do not have to send the tax documents to the government. This method helps save paper. Also, those who e-file their taxes on time are given a grace period of 10-20 days to file the income tax returns manually.

How to File ITR Online - ITR Filing Online Process

Here is a step-by-step guide for filing ITR online:

Step 1: Calculate Income and Tax

The first step while e-filing income tax is to calculate the income and applicable tax with respect to the tax provisions.

The income tax calculation should be done by considering income from every source and computed after claiming the allowed deductions.

Step 2: TDS Certificates and Form 26AS

The next crucial step is to summarize the TDS amount from the TDS certificates received. Note a Form 26AS can be used to compute the summary of TDS and tax paid during a certain financial year.

Step 3: Select the Suitable Income Tax Form

In step 3, a taxpayer is required to select the ITR form before filing the returns. Note as a taxpayer, you can file an income tax return form only when you have a Permanent Account Number, which is shortened to PAN.

The income tax rules are created within the framework of the Income Tax Act and are not permitted to supersede its scope. The Finance Act is passed every year by the Parliament of India. The Finance Act changes the Income Tax Act of 1961.

Step 4: Download ITR Utility from Income Tax Portal

Visit the official Income Tax portal at www.incometax.gov.in and click on 'Downloads'. Next, select the Assessment Year, ITR form, and the utility that you want.

Step 5: Fill Out the Downloaded File

Once you download the offline utility, ensure to fill out the required details that would include your income, tax payable, refund receivable, etc.

Step 6: Validate the Information Entered

After filling out the form, click on the 'Validate' button to confirm all the filled in details.

Step 7: Convert the File to XML Format

After validating, use the 'Generate XML' option of the file to convert the file into XML format.

Step 8: Upload the XML File on the Official Income Tax Filing Portal

Log in to the official income tax e-filing portal. Click 'e-File' option and then click on 'Income Tax Return'.

Choose the Assessment Year, ITR form, and Submission mode as 'Upload XML'. Attach the XML file from your computer and click 'Submit'.

Rules for eFiling in Income Tax

Filing Only with Aadhaar: You can use Aadhaar to file your income tax return when you don't have a Permanent Account Number. Furthermore, when applying for a new PAN, you must present your Aadhaar Card.

Taxpayers who file their returns using their Aadhaar card will be assigned a PAN after their demographic data is obtained from the Unique Identification Authority of India, according to the new income tax guidelines issued.

The new rule was implemented by the Government of India in order to improve tax compliance while making the tax filing process simpler for taxpayers. As a result, individuals are required to link their PAN with Aadhaar before filing their taxes. However, you will be required to link your Aadhaar and PAN in order to file your taxes.

Benefits of E-Filing of Income Tax

The Government of India has made e-filing of income tax returns mandatory. This method is much simpler than the previous paper filing process.

The following are the benefits of submitting ITR filing or income taxes electronically:

  • Every year, the deadline for e-filing income tax returns is July 31st. If the user uploads it one or two months before the deadline, they will be able to accomplish the assignment much quickly and with less congestion, as servers tend to become overwhelmed as the deadline approaches.
  • This record may be useful if the taxpayer desires to conduct future business with any other organization that supports similar records.
  • If a taxpayer is unable to file income tax returns for the preceding year, he must incur partial interest for each extra day until payment is made. As a result, filing your income tax return early saves you from the additional tax.
  • Online filing of tax returns allows the taxpayer to retain a more effective record of all financial interactions with the Income Tax Department.

GST Filing - Types of GST Returns and GST Filing Due Dates

GST, or Goods and Services Tax, replaced the numerous different taxes which were levied by Central and State governments on different products or services. While it was enforced in 2017, the GST Act was passed in the year 2016.

This taxation system intends to streamline the numerous taxes levied on products and services, simplifying the process for taxpayers, their collectors, etc. Additionally, it also makes tax filing very easy since taxpayers have to file for just one tax.

What is GST Return and GST Return Filing

A GST return is a legal document containing GST invoices, receipts, payments, etc., of a certain period. It is a summary of all the GST related transactions of a business for a particular period. The GST return provides a comprehensive view of the business's tax liability and compliance status. It is used by the tax authorities to verify the accuracy of the tax paid and to ensure compliance with the GST laws. Businesses are required to file GST returns electronically through the official GST portal. The frequency of filing returns varies based on the type of business and its turnover. Businesses can also claim input tax credit for the GST paid on their inputs through the GST return. Overall, GST return filing is a crucial part of the GST regime and is essential for businesses to comply with the tax laws and avoid penalties.

The main parts of a GST return include:

  • Purchases
  • Sales
  • Output GST (on sales)
  • Input Tax Credit (GST paid on purchases)

All businesses registered under the Goods and Service Tax (GST) are liable to file GST returns monthly, quarterly, and annually, depending on the type of business and its turnover. It is mandatory to provide the respective details within the prescribed time limit to avoid penalties. Businesses need to maintain proper records of all GST related transactions, such as invoices, receipts, and payment details, to ensure accurate filing of returns. The GST framework also provides various online tools and resources to assist businesses in filing their returns and complying with the GST laws. Overall, timely and accurate filing of GST returns is crucial for businesses to avoid penalties and ensure smooth tax compliance.

For filing the GST returns, one has to file 2 Forms that may include the returns for the purchases made, returns for the supplies, monthly/quarterly returns, etc.

The government has made GST return filing in India compulsory for all such entities that carry a valid GST registration.

Online GST Filing Process

GST can be filed online using the software provided by the Goods and Services Tax Network. Below are the steps required to file GST return:

  • Step 1: Log in to the GST portal.
  • Step 2: Upon logging in, you will be required to enter the taxpayer's username and password. Once logged in, you will be directed to the dashboard, where you can access various services related to GST.
  • Step 3: Select the 'Services' tab and then click on 'Returns Dashboard' to proceed with the return filing process.
  • Step 4: Select the financial year and the return filing period (monthly or quarterly) for which you want to file the return.
  • Step 5: Select the type of return you want to file (e.g., GSTR-1, GSTR-3B) and click on 'Prepare Online' or 'Prepare Offline' based on your preference.
  • Step 6: Enter the required details in the respective tables of the return form, such as outward supplies, inward supplies, input tax credit, and tax liability.
  • Step 7: Verify the details entered and click on 'Submit' to proceed to the next step.
  • Step 8: Pay the tax liability, if any, through the available payment options (e.g., net banking, credit/debit card).
  • Step 9: File the return using DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) or EVC (Electronic Verification Code).
  • Step 10: Download the acknowledgment receipt for future reference.

Types of GST Returns and GST Return Filing Due Dates

There are quite a few types of GST returns which have their specific forms. These forms are used to file various types of information related to GST transactions, such as outward supplies, inward supplies, input tax credit, and tax liability. The GST framework provides various forms to cater to the diverse needs of businesses and ensure smooth tax compliance. Businesses are required to file the appropriate forms based on their type of business, turnover, and the nature of transactions. The GST portal provides online access to all the forms and allows businesses to file them electronically. It is essential for businesses to understand the purpose and requirements of each form to ensure accurate and timely filing of returns. The government has also introduced simplified forms for small taxpayers under the composition scheme to reduce their compliance burden. Overall, the availability of various forms and the online filing system have made GST compliance easier and more accessible for businesses.

These forms are listed below in the chart with the due dates for filing the GST returns:

Return Applicable Entrepreneurs GST Filing Dates
GSTR-1 Individuals liable to file returns for the outward supplies made during the month. 11th of next month
GSTR-2 Taxable recipients liable to file returns for the inward supplies received during the month. 15th of next month
GSTR-3B Individuals filing monthly returns, including details of outward and inward supplies, input tax credit, and tax liability. 20th of next month
GSTR-4 Individuals who have opted for the Composition Scheme and are required to file quarterly returns. 18th of the month following the quarter
GSTR-5 Individuals who are non-resident taxable persons and are required to file monthly returns. 20th of next month
GSTR-6 Input Service Distributors (ISD) who are required to file monthly returns. 13th of next month
GSTR-7 Individuals who are required to deduct TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) under GST. 10th of next month
GSTR-8 E-commerce operators who are required to collect TCS (Tax Collected at Source) under GST. 10th of next month
GSTR-9 Individuals who are required to file annual returns. 31st December of next financial year
GSTR-10 Individuals whose GST registration has been cancelled or surrendered and are required to file a final return. Within 3 months of cancellation or surrender order
GSTR-11 Individuals who have been issued a Unique Identity Number (UIN) and are required to file returns. 28th of the month following the month for which statement was filed.

The table above explains who the forms for GST filing are meant for, along with their submission date. It is also important to know the details to be filled in the forms.

GSTR-1

This form contains the below-mentioned purchase information of their outward supply of goods and services. The form contains the following details:

  • Name of business, period for which tax is being filed, and GST identification number (GSTIN).
  • Invoices and tax collection from previous month.
  • Advances received in lieu of supplies.
  • Revision on previous month's sales invoices.

GSTR-2

Taxable recipients have to provide the following details in this form during their GST filing:

  • Name of business, period for which tax is being filed, and GSTIN.
  • Invoices and collected tax in the previous month.
  • Advances received for order to be delivered in the future.
  • Revision of previous month's sales and invoices.

Taxpayers filing their return already have their details electronically updated along with forms GSTR-1 and 2. Taxpayers must check and verify the above forms as and when necessary, and also provide the details wherever required.

  • Input tax credit, liability, and cash ledger details.
  • CGST, SGST and IGST tax payment details.
  • Excess payment, refund claim or credit request.

GSTR-4

Businesses with a turnover up to Rs 1.5 Lakh can opt for Composition Scheme and go for GST filing with GSTR-4. It is important for such businesses to have their GSTIN registered under the scheme. The GSTR-4 form contains the following details:

  • Total value of supplies during the quarter.
  • Details of inward and outward supplies, including supplies from registered and unregistered persons.
  • Tax liability and tax payment details.
  • Input tax credit details, if any.

These businesses should file their GST returns quarterly. They do not have to file tax credit, they must also provide the following details:

  • The payment details.
  • Purchase information like invoices.

GSTR-5

NRIs must provide the following information while filing this form:

  • Taxpayer's name and address accompanied with return period and GSTIN.
  • Details of both outgoing and incoming supplies.
  • Input tax details accompanied with amendments of previous returns.
  • Service importing details along with amendments in the previous service.
  • Refunds from cash ledger, credit and debit details, closing stock, etc.

GSTR-6

Input Service Distributors must provide the following details when they are completing their GST filing process. Same details are already electronically updated via forms GSTR-1 and 5.

  • Taxpayer's name and address detail, return period, and GSTIN.
  • Input tax credit distribution details.
  • Details on the supplies received from registered suppliers.
  • Input tax credit amount in their present tax period.
  • Input tax credit received detail.
  • Credit and debit note details.
  • Do credit that has been received, reversed, and distributed under CGST, SGST and IGST.

GSTR-7

Taxpayers who have deducted tax at source must provide the following details in their form. The TDS liability is generated automatically. The Electronic Cash Ledger refunds will be Auto-generated.

  • Address details of Taxpayers to accompany their name and GSTIN along with the return period.
  • TDS amount, contract details, and necessary amendments.
  • Late filing fee and interest.

GSTR-8

E-commerce operators who are required to collect TCS (Tax Collected at Source) under GST. must provide the following details in their GST filing with form GSTR-8:

  • Personal details like name and address accompanied with return period and GSTIN.
  • Supply details of payments to registered individuals and necessary amendments to the previous tax payments.
  • Supply details to unregistered individuals.
  • TCS details.
  • Late filing details and delayed payment interest, accompanied by auto-populated tax liability.

GSTR-9

Filed by normal taxpayers, it contains both income and expenditure details. It is compiled and assorted with monthly returns. Filing this must accompany audited copies of annual accounts, and taxpayers can amend the previous declarations if necessary.

GSTR-10

To be filed on cancellation of GST, taxpayers must provide the following details while their GST filing with this form:

  • ARN or Application Reference Number.
  • GST registration cancellation date.
  • Cancellation order ID.
  • Calculation order date.
  • Closing stock details and payable tax.

GSTR-11

Every individual registered under GST with their UIN (Unique Identity Number) must file this form. This is used to claim a refund of excess taxes paid on inward supplies.

UINs should be accompanied by the return period and name of the government body.

  • Auto-generated inward supplies from suppliers who are registered under GST.

GSTN

GSTN, or Goods and Services Tax Network, is an online portal containing all the details of sellers and buyers registered under GST's registration. Businesses and taxpayers can access these details from the GSTN for filing their returns, knowing their credits, etc.

Companies have to file a total of 27 returns, one annual return and 2 monthly returns (once every 3 months) during a financial year. These contain information including details of inward and outward supplies indulged by an individual or a company in a financial year. This is primarily important if there is an amount of refund to be claimed.

How to Check GST Return Status?

The following ways can be used to track GST return status online:

Tracking through ARN

Here are the steps to check the status through the Application Reference Number (ARN):

  • Step 1: Open the official GST portal and log in.
  • Step 2: Click 'Services' -> 'Refunds' -> 'Track Refund Status'.
  • Step 3: Enter the ARN and click on 'Search'.
  • Step 4: Click on the search option to view the GST return application status.

Tracking through GST Return Filing Period

  • Step 1: Log in to the official GST portal.
  • Step 2: Click 'Services' -> 'Refunds' -> 'Track Refund Status'.
  • Step 3: Click on the GST Return Filing Period and enter the dates.
  • Step 4: Click on the Search option to view the GST return application status.

When Tracking Through the Status

Step 1: Navigate to the official GST portal and search taxpayer option.

Step 2: Under the GSTIN/UIN option, enter GSTIN ID and fill out the Captcha code. Click on the search. Note the Captcha Code will appear once you have entered the GSTIN ID.

Step 3: Upon filling out the details and submitting, the required details, such as company name, registration date, and GSTIN will be displayed on the screen.

How to Download GST Returns Online

Follow the steps listed below to download the GST returns online:

  • Step 1: Log in to the GST portal.
  • Step 2: Click on 'File GST Returns'.
  • Step 3: Select the financial year, quarter, and month and then click on the 'Search' option.
  • Step 4: Click on 'View GSTR-X' (where X is the return number) option.
  • Step 5: Click on the 'Download' option to download the GST return.

Penalty for Late Filing

Penalty in the form of late fees will be levied on taxpayers if they fail to file their GST returns on time. A total of Rs.100 (Rs.50 as CGST and Rs.50 as SGST) is charged per day, which can go as high as Rs.5000. However, this penalty is only applicable if the tax liability is higher than Rs. 1 Lakh. This is only applicable for businesses with a turnover of more than Rs. 1.5 Crore. For businesses with a turnover less than Rs. 1.5 Crore, the penalty is Rs. 50 per day (Rs. 25 as CGST and Rs. 25 as SGST).

The late fee for the annual return (GSTR-9) is Rs. 100 per day (Rs. 50 as CGST and Rs. 50 as SGST) for each Act, subject to a maximum of 0.25% of the turnover in the State or Union Territory.

Integrated Goods and Services Tax does not attract any late fee, although the taxpayer will be required to pay an interest of 18% per annum on the due tax sum.

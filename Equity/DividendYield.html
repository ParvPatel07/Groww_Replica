Dividend Yield Mutual Funds

Dividends are shares of profit assigned by wealthy or equity-holding shareholders. Many companies share a piece of their profits with their shareholders in the form of dividends. Dividend Yield Mutual Funds are equity mutual funds that invest in companies with a consistent track record of paying dividends. These companies are often well-established and financially stable, making dividend yield funds a popular choice for investors seeking regular income and capital appreciation. What are Dividend Yield Mutual Funds?

Dividend Yield Mutual Funds (dividend funds) invest in the shares and pay out dividends to their investors. These funds are chosen by the market price. Dividend yield is calculated as the ratio of the annual dividend per share to the market price per share. These funds invest in companies that have a consistent track record of paying dividends. The main objective of these funds is to provide regular income to investors along with capital appreciation. Dividend yield funds are suitable for investors who are looking for regular income and are willing to take moderate risk.

Features of a Dividend Yield Fund

  • Source of Income: Dividends are issued by a key criterion for selecting stocks for such funds, but you expect a consistent return on your investment. However, the actual company stock fund is paid out in addition to the possible dividends received.
  • Lower Risk: These funds are a regular choice for extremely reliable organizations that are well-known in the market and have a consistent track record of paying dividends. This makes dividend yield funds a relatively safer investment option. They focus a lot on large-cap companies, which are less risky than mid-cap or small-cap companies.

How Does a Dividend Yield Mutual Fund Work?

A dividend mutual fund invests in stocks of companies that pay out high dividends. Dividend mutual funds are actively managed, and the fund manager selects stocks based on the company's dividend track record, financial health, and growth potential. The fund manager may also invest in stocks that have the potential to pay high dividends in the future. Dividend yield funds are suitable for investors who are looking for regular income and are willing to take moderate risk. These funds are best suited for long-term investors who want to build wealth gradually while receiving a stable income.

How Should You Invest in a Dividend Yield Mutual Fund?

  • Online investment platforms

Why Should You Invest in a Dividend Yield Fund?

  • Regular Source of Income: These funds are best suited for investors who are looking for regular income and capital appreciation. They also aim for stability by investing in companies with a consistent dividend payout history.
  • Risk Mitigation: These funds focus on productivity and consistent dividends. Therefore, you are less vulnerable if markets fall sharply.

Taxation Rules of Dividend Yield Mutual Funds

As per the new rules, dividends received from mutual funds are taxable in the hands of investors from April 1, 2020, onwards, as per the investor's income tax slab. If you hold your mutual fund units for less than 12 months, short-term capital gains (STCG) tax at 15% is applicable. If you hold your units for more than 12 months, long-term capital gains (LTCG) tax at 10% is applicable on gains above Rs. 1 lakh, as per the rules below:

  • Long-Term Capital Gains – 10% after Rs. 1 lakh (no indexation benefit).
  • Short-Term Capital Gains – 15% (no indexation benefit).

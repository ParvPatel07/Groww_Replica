What are Focused Funds?

Focused funds invest in a limited number of stocks rather than a large variety of stocks. The primary purpose of focused mutual funds is to achieve better returns by focusing on high-conviction ideas. These funds are suitable for investors who are willing to take higher risks for potentially higher returns. Focused funds are managed by experienced fund managers who use various strategies to select the best stocks for the fund. Here is a detailed look at focused funds, their features, and how they work.

Features of a Focused Mutual Fund

  • Limited Investment in the Funds Portfolio: A focused mutual fund invests in a limited number (up to 30) of stocks.
  • Selection Process for Fund's Investments: The fund manager selects stocks based on technical, scientific, or thematic criteria, in order to manage a focused portfolio of high-conviction ideas.
  • No Restriction on Size of Fund's Investments: Focused funds can invest in companies of any size or category. They can own stocks that reward significant amount of returns, but also carry higher risk. These funds may invest in large-cap, mid-cap, or small-cap stocks, depending on the fund manager's strategy.

How Does a Focused Mutual Fund Work?

The portfolio of a focused mutual fund is constructed with a limited group of diversified stocks and follows a core strategy. Focused funds are managed by experienced fund managers who use various strategies to select the best stocks for the fund. The aim is to achieve better returns by focusing on high-conviction ideas. These funds are suitable for investors who are willing to take higher risks for potentially higher returns.

Why Should You Invest in a Focused Mutual Fund?

  • High Returns: Ideally, through the use of the right investments, they can achieve attractive returns. With focused mutual funds, investors can achieve higher returns by focusing on high-conviction ideas.
  • Diversification of Mutual Funds: Although focused funds are less diversified, they are better suited for investing in stocks that are expected to deliver superior returns. These funds are suitable for investors who are willing to take higher risks for potentially higher returns.
  • Exposure to Selected Stocks: Since focused funds invest only in a selected set (30 or fewer) companies, the fund manager selects a significant amount of their best ideas. This can lead to better returns if the selected stocks perform well. However, it also increases the risk if the selected stocks do not perform as expected.

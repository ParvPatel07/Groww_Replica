Large cap funds are ideal for investors looking for steady returns and lower risk.

What are Large Cap Funds?

Large Cap Mutual Funds are equity funds that invest a bigger proportion of their total assets in companies with a large market capitalization. These companies are mostly industry leaders and have a track record of stable performance, making large cap funds a relatively safer investment option.

Features of a Large Cap Fund

  • Blend of Return: These funds invest in the stocks of large, well-established companies, offering a blend of stability and growth.
  • Market Share-Driven Investments: The focus is on companies with a significant market share, which are less likely to be affected by market volatility.
  • Consistent Year Financial Growth: Large cap companies generally show consistent financial growth, making these funds ideal for conservative investors.

How Does a Large Cap Mutual Fund Work?

Large cap funds invest at least 80% of their assets in large capitalization listed companies. The remaining 20% may be in other equity, debt, or money market instruments. The fund manager selects stocks based on the company's market capitalization, financial performance, and growth potential.

How Should You Invest in a Large Cap Mutual Fund?

Investors can invest in large cap funds through SIPs or lump sum investments. It is advisable to invest for a longer duration to benefit from the compounding effect and to ride out market volatility.

Why Should You Invest in Large Cap Mutual Funds?

  • Lower Risk: These funds invest in large, stable companies, which are less likely to be affected by market volatility.
  • Higher Returns than Bank Deposits: Large cap funds generally offer higher returns than traditional bank deposits.
  • Diversification: These funds provide diversification by investing in a basket of large companies across sectors.

Taxation Rules of Large Cap Mutual Funds

  • Capital Gains Tax: Gains from large cap funds are subject to capital gains tax as per the prevailing tax laws.
  • Dividend Distribution Tax: Dividends received from these funds are taxed as per the investor's income tax slab.

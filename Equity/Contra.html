Contra Mutual Funds

Fund managers of contra funds adopt different investing styles to achieve the investment objective of the scheme. Of these, the contrarian style is the most interesting. These funds invest in stocks that are out of favour, expecting either a reversal in fortunes or a correction in the market valuation. With a long-term view, such funds typically offer value. What are Contra Funds?

A Contra Mutual Fund invests against the existing market trends and purchases stocks that are not performing well currently. The idea is to buy stocks at a lower price and sell them when the price rises. The fund manager identifies undervalued stocks and invests in them with the expectation that their value will increase in the future. Contra funds require a high-risk appetite and a long-term investment horizon. These funds are suitable for investors who are willing to take higher risks for potentially higher returns.

Features of a Contra Mutual Fund

  • Contra funds have a higher risk-reward ratio since they invest in companies that are seeking a market turn for potential. Fund managers strategically rebalance stocks to take complete benefit as soon as the best period for growth arrives.
  • Equity investment: These funds invest most of their assets in equity and equity-related products and equity derivatives. This allocation makes them suitable for investors with a long-term growth perspective.

How Does a Contra Mutual Fund Work?

How contra funds are different from other mutual funds: Contra fund operates significantly differently. It employs a strategy where the fund manager selects stocks, sectors, or asset classes against ongoing market sentiment. If the market is bullish, the fund manager may invest in undervalued stocks or sectors that are currently out of favour. If the market is bearish, the fund manager may invest in stocks or sectors that are expected to perform well in the long term based on a contrarian view.

Why Should You Invest in a Contra Mutual Fund?

  • Higher returns: Contra funds can potentially provide higher returns by investing in undervalued stocks.
  • Risk diversification: By investing in diverse, product categories involving riskier (out-of-favour) markets.
  • Contrarian approach: Contra funds may be a suitable choice for long-term investors looking for value and tax savings.
  • Capital appreciation: Contra funds can help diversify a portfolio, especially if the other assets in the portfolio are more traditional.

FAQs

  • Q1. Which Contra fund is leading?
    A contra fund is a fund that invests against the market trend to the opposite direction of the market. Contra funds invest in stocks that are undervalued or out of favour.
  • Q2. Who can invest in Contra Funds?
    Contra funds are suitable for long-term investors with a high-risk appetite who are looking for value and tax-saving benefits.
  • Q3. Are contra funds good investments?
    Contra mutual funds are best suited for high-risk investors. They invest in undervalued stocks and may deliver better returns if the market turns in their favour.
  • Q4. Are contra funds high risk investments?
    Yes, contra funds are generally considered high-risk investments and can provide higher long-term returns.

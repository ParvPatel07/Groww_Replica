ELSS Funds

Investors look out for investment opportunities that can help them generate wealth, get regular returns, and/or save taxes. ELSS funds are one such investment solution. ELSS stands for Equity Linked Savings Scheme, and these are diversified equity mutual funds that invest a majority of their corpus in equity and equity-related instruments. ELSS funds are popular among investors for the tax benefits under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act. Here is a detailed look at ELSS funds including how they function, features & benefits, and tax implications on ELSS mutual funds.

What are ELSS Funds

ELSS funds are among the fastest tax-saving mutual fund schemes of their class: simple and easy-to-understand instruments. ELSS mutual funds primarily invest their assets in equity and equity-related instruments and have a mandatory lock-in period of three years. If you are looking for tax-saving investment plans, many beginners turn towards ELSS to maximize tax benefits. If you want to build tax-free returns, these plans are ideal. The returns generated by these funds are subject to market risks, but the lock-in period ensures that investors stay invested for a minimum of three years. ELSS funds are also considered a long-term capital gains (from 1-3 years) and offer the benefit of equity returns and tax rebates as a bonus.

Features of ELSS Mutual Fund

  • A minimum lock-in of three (3) years is mandatory, coupled to a relatively high equity/equity-related instruments.
  • Tax deduction of up to Rs 1.5 lakh is available under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act.
  • ELSS funds invest at least 80% of their assets in equity and equity-related instruments (listed companies, financial, and sectors).
  • There is no maximum limit for investment in ELSS funds, but the tax deduction cap is Rs 1.5 lakh, investing that, you can invest as much as you want.
  • Taxation is as per the long-term capital gains tax (LTCG) on equity mutual funds.

How Does ELSS Funds Work?

ELSS is an open-ended equity fund with a diverse portfolio. These funds primarily invest in equity, tracker fund stocks. The lock-in period is three years, and you cannot redeem your investment before the period ends. The fund manager allocates the assets in equity and equity-related instruments, and the returns are subject to market performance. Investors can invest through SIPs, lump sum, or both. The minimum investment amount is usually Rs 500. The tax deduction cap is Rs 1.5 lakh. When a unit is purchased, the lock-in period starts from the date of investment. The tax deduction cap is Rs 1.5 lakh, investing that, you can invest as much as you want.

Why should you invest in ELSS Tax Saving Mutual Funds?

ELSS is ideal for investors who are looking to grow their wealth & cut tax outgo.

  • Compounding Returns: ELSS funds invest a major chunk of components ranging from small caps to large caps, and the returns are market-linked. This means, you achieve the power of compounding returns, and the longer you stay invested, the higher the returns you can expect.
  • Tax Benefits: ELSS funds are the only equity mutual funds that offer tax benefits under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act. You can claim a deduction of up to Rs 1.5 lakh per annum, and the returns are tax-free up to Rs 1 lakh per annum.
  • Short Lock-in Period: ELSS funds have a lock-in period of just three years, which is the shortest among all tax-saving investments under Section 80C.

Taxation Rules of ELSS Funds

Since ELSS funds are subject to capital gains, there is no way to evade taxation post gains. As a result, you claim deduction under Section 80C, but the returns are subject to LTCG tax. The tax rules are as follows:

  • Long-term capital gains (LTCG) of up to Rs 1 lakh in a financial year are tax-free. Gains above Rs 1 lakh are taxed at 10% without indexation.
  • Dividends received from ELSS funds are also taxable in the hands of the investor as per the applicable tax slab.

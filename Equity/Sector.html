What are Sector Mutual Funds

Sector Mutual Funds are equity schemes that invest in equities of a sector or a theme. These schemes can be utilities, technology, manufacturing, banks, etc. Sector funds are equity mutual funds that invest in companies belonging to a particular sector or industry. These funds are suitable for investors who are willing to take higher risk and want to benefit from the growth potential of a particular sector. These funds allow people to invest in the best-performing sectors of the economy.

Features of a Sector Fund

  • Expensive: These funds are relatively costly because most are more aggressive.
  • Greater Risk-Reward: Sector funds pay for their risk by making them a more densely diversified mutual fund. This also adds volatility, especially when the sector being invested in is not doing consistently.
  • Sectoral Exposure: Investing in a sector fund gives higher returns, provided the sector has consistently grown over time.
  • Diversification: Different sector funds are expected to perform differently depending on external factors, including the time of the economic cycle.
  • Low Diversification: Sector funds invest in their chosen construction mode, unlike other mutual funds. They invest virtually entirely in a single sector.

Types of Sector Funds

  • Banking Funds: which allow investors to invest in Indian banks or groups in the said sector, and across mutual funds.
  • Technology Funds: which are focused on investing in companies from the tech and start-up, energy, and manufacturing sectors.
  • Infrastructure Funds: which are focused on investing in companies that are part of the infrastructure sector, such as roads, bridges, and power plants.
  • Financial Funds: which invest primarily in companies from the financial industry like banking, insurance, and asset management companies.
  • Healthcare Funds: which cover companies and top-tiered medical institutions like pharmaceutical companies, path labs, etc.
  • Precious Metals Funds: which offer the investors exposure to precious metals like gold, platinum, silver, copper, and palladium.

How Does a Sector Mutual Fund Work?

Sectors funds invest in companies that operate in the fund's designated sector, which is a subset of the market. A sector fund is more risky than a diversified fund because all the stocks in the scheme pertain to a single sector.

Investors who enjoy the strong growth potential in a sector will occasionally witness volatility, and eventually, rewards, depending on the sector's performance. Sector funds are best suited for investors who are willing to take higher risk and want to benefit from the growth potential of a particular sector. These funds allow people to invest in the best-performing sectors of the economy.

Sector funds do provide some diversification through multiple holdings in a particular investment, however, if the sector underperforms, the entire portfolio can be affected. Sector funds are thus often suited for short-term tactical allocation or for investors who have a strong view on a particular sector's prospects.

How Should You Invest in a Sector Mutual Fund?

Sector funds are appropriate for investors who are willing to take on risks in order to invest in an unpredictable sector. Investors can invest in these mutual funds through the following ways: online and easy ways, some of them are:

  • Direct (Asset management companies' websites)
  • Through investment platforms
  • Through netbanking

Why Should You Invest in Sector Mutual Funds?

  • High Growth Potential: If the sector does well, investors can benefit from high returns.
  • High Volatility/High Risk: If the sector fails to grow over time, investing in sector funds might yield higher volatility.

Taxation Rules of Sector Mutual Funds

  • Long-term Capital Gains – 10% after Rs. 1 lakh (no indexation benefit).
  • Short-term Capital Gains – 15% (no indexation benefit).
  • Dividend Returns – According to the individual's tax slab.

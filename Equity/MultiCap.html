What are Multi Cap Funds?

As the name suggests, Multi Cap Funds invest their corpus in a portfolio of equity and equity-related assets of companies across large, mid, and small market capitalizations. This allows investors to benefit from the growth potential of mid and small cap companies, while also enjoying the stability of large cap companies. The fund manager actively manages the allocation to each segment based on market conditions and opportunities.

Features of a Multi Cap Fund

  • Management: The fund manager actively manages the portfolio to achieve the right balance of risk and return by investing in companies across different market capitalizations.
  • Diversification: By investing in a mix of large, mid, and small cap companies, these funds help reduce risk and capture growth opportunities across the market.
  • Flexible Allocation: The fund manager can adjust the allocation to each segment as per market conditions, aiming for the best long-term risk and return balance.

Types of Multi Cap Mutual Funds

  • Concentrated on Large Cap Stocks: These funds provide diversification alongside segments and occasionally explore mid and small caps.
  • Small Cap/Large Cycle Concentrated Funds: These funds capture growth from the small/midcap universe while maintaining a base of large-cap stocks.
  • Flexible/Opportunistic: These funds are less investment allocated, across market capitalizations, with a focus on finding stocks that can outperform benchmarks.

How Does a Multi Cap Mutual Fund Work?

Multi Cap funds invest in equity shares in large, mid, and small cap companies, using a long-term focus and a key element of the allocation strategy. The fund manager may shift the allocation between segments based on market outlook, opportunities, and risk assessment. This flexibility allows the fund to capture growth across the market while managing risk.

Why Should You Invest in a Multi Cap Fund?

  • Diversification: A multi cap portfolio allows the investor to invest in firms of various sizes and sectors. This method of diversification, especially in volatile times, is an excellent strategy to reduce risk and protect returns at any time, while generating reasonable returns.
  • Flexibility: The ability to allocate between large, mid, and small cap shares leads to a significant portfolio compared to fixed segment allocation schemes. So, large and mid-cap schemes become less rigid and it appears that the gains are not as heavily dependent on one sector, but are spread across the market.

Taxation Rules of Multi Cap Mutual Funds

  • Capital Gains Tax: When you redeem your Multi Cap fund units, the capital gains made are subject to Capital gains tax. The rates, like any other equity mutual fund, are as follows:
  • STCG: If units are held for a period of up to 1 year, the tax rate is 15%.
  • LTCG: If units are held for more than 1 year, the tax rate is 10% on gains above Rs. 1 lakh, and there are no taxes, but those that are taxed at 10% without indexation benefit.
  • Dividend Tax: All fund houses are mandated to deduct tax (DDT) at 10% before paying out dividends to unitholders.

FAQs

  • Q1. What is a Multi Cap mutual funds meaning?
    Multi Cap mutual funds are equity investment vehicles that invest across market capitalizations. Therefore, these portfolios consist of large-cap, mid-cap, and small-cap stocks.
  • Q2. How long should you invest in Multi Cap Mutual Funds?
    As with most equity mutual funds, Multi Cap funds are best for investors seeking returns over more than 5-6 years.
  • Q3. What is the risk of Multi Cap funds in India?
    Multi Cap funds are subject to market risk, with high concentration on large/mid/small caps. Like any funds with a high equity allocation, they are best suited for investors with a moderate to high risk appetite.
  • Q4. What are the advantages of Multi Cap funds?
    Multi Cap funds offer diversification, flexibility, and potential for better returns compared to funds focused on a single segment.

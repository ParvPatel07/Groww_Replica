Small Cap Mutual Funds

When investing in Small Cap Mutual Funds, it is important to understand the market capitalisation of companies, as small-cap funds primarily invest in companies ranked below the top 250 by market capitalisation. These companies are relatively smaller, have higher growth potential, but also come with higher risk due to market volatility. Small cap funds are suitable for investors with a high risk appetite and a long-term investment horizon, as they can help generate significant returns over time.

What is Small Cap Funds

Small-cap funds invest primarily in stocks of small market-cap companies with steady or significant outperformance of small-cap indices. Mostly these are in the sunrise and innovative sector, and can deliver high returns, albeit with higher volatility. Small cap funds are best suited for investors who can withstand market ups and downs, and are looking for wealth creation over a long period. Since companies in this sector are relatively new, they have the potential to deliver superior returns, but also carry higher risk.

Features of Small Cap Equity Funds

  • Long Term Investment: Small cap funds are suitable for investors with a long-term investment horizon.
  • High Volatility: Small cap stocks are highly sensitive to market movements. Therefore, the market timing should also be considered before investing in these funds.
  • Risk Tolerance: Investors with a high risk appetite can consider investing in small cap funds as they have the potential to deliver higher returns.

How Does a Small Cap Mutual Fund Work?

Small cap mutual funds invest at least 65% of their assets in small cap shares (as per SEBI's latest definition: companies ranked below 250 by market cap). The remaining assets may be invested in mid/large cap stocks, debt, or money market instruments. The fund manager selects stocks based on growth potential, sectoral trends, and company fundamentals. These funds are actively managed and require regular monitoring.

How Should You Invest in Small Cap Mutual Fund?

Being a volatile category, it is advisable to invest in small cap funds via a SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) to average out the market volatility. Investors should have a long-term horizon and review their portfolio regularly.

Why Should You Invest in Small Cap Fund?

  • High Returns: Small Cap Mutual Funds have the potential to provide significantly higher returns than mid-cap or large-cap mutual funds. This is due to the strong growth potential of small companies.
  • Low Market Value Buy: Most small cap mutual funds are typically available at a lower market valuation. This allows you to participate in the growth story of companies at an early stage, especially if market conditions are favourable.
  • Diversification Opportunity: You can diversify your portfolio by allocating a portion to Small Cap mutual funds. These funds have the potential to deliver superior returns as part of a diversified investment within the mutual fund basket. Risks, however, remain higher.

Taxation Rules of Small Cap Mutual Funds

  • Capital Gains Tax: Gains from small cap funds are subject to capital gains tax, and dividend distribution tax as per the prevailing tax laws.

Capital Gains Tax:

  • Capital gains are taxed at 15% when selling an investment held for less than 1 year (short-term capital gain), and at 10% (without indexation) for gains above ₹1 lakh if held for more than 1 year (long-term capital gain).

FAQs

  • Q: What is Small cap mutual fund meaning?
    A: Small cap mutual funds invest in small-cap stocks, i.e., companies ranked below 250 by market cap. These funds have higher growth potential but also higher risk.
  • Q: What are the main advantages of investing in Small Cap mutual funds?
    A: Higher returns, early-stage growth potential, and diversification.
  • Q: What is the major risk of investing in a small-cap fund?
    A: Higher volatility and market risk, as small cap stocks tend to fall and grow much faster than bluechips. The success rate is lower, and the risk of loss is higher.
  • Q: Who can invest in Small Cap mutual funds?
    A: Investors with a long-term horizon and a high risk tolerance level can choose Small Cap mutual funds.

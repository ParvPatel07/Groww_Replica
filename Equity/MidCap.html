What are Mid Cap Funds?

Mid Cap funds are equity mutual funds that invest in mid-sized companies of the Nifty Mid Cap companies. According to the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI), mid cap companies are those ranked between 101st and 250th in terms of market capitalization. These companies are considered to have higher growth potential than large caps, but are less risky than small caps.

Features of a Mid Cap Fund

  • Larger Investments: Mid cap funds invest in companies with solid fundamentals and robust management teams. A significant percentage of the portfolio is allocated to mid cap stocks, which have the potential to deliver higher returns than large cap stocks. However, midcap schemes are also riskier than large cap funds.
  • Fund Managers: The fund manager is the one who makes the decisions about buying/selling stocks from the mid cap universe. In most cases, the fund manager is an experienced professional who has a good understanding of the market and the companies in the mid cap segment. This helps in selecting the right stocks for the portfolio and managing risk in the pursuit of higher returns.
  • Sustainability: Mid cap funds are usually the first growth opportunity available to investors. However, compounding returns takes time and often the best results are seen only after a long investment horizon. Investors willing to wait are the best suited to benefit from the power of compounding in mid cap funds.

How Does a Mid Cap Mutual Fund Work?

Mid Cap Mutual Funds work by picking stocks that fall in the range of 101st to 250th in market cap. You put the fund's allocation of your assets into these companies, and the fund manager selects the best stocks for the portfolio. The fund manager's expertise and research play a crucial role in the performance of the fund. These funds are suitable for investors with moderate risk appetite, looking for higher returns than large cap funds, but with less risk than small cap funds.

Why Should You Invest in a Mid Cap Mutual Fund?

  • Returns: Mid cap funds usually offer better returns than large cap mutual funds. Belonging to the equity fund category, they are slightly riskier than large categories mutual funds, but they offer superior returns than the large-cap funds of almost all benchmarks in the long run.
  • Portfolio Balance: These funds add a balance to the portfolio. They offer the duality of bringing faster and risk-free growth of mid-cap mutual funds.

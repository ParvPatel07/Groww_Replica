PF Withdrawal Form

A PF withdrawal form, short for Provident Fund withdrawal form, is a document that allows concerned individuals to withdraw a certain amount from their PF corpus.

What Is the Purpose of the PF Claim Form?

During employment, both the employee and the employer are required to contribute equally to EPF or the Employees' Provident Fund Scheme. The employee's contribution is deducted as a percentage of their basic salary plus dearness allowance.

The purpose of EPF is large, and the scheme is specific to it encourages the accretion of adequate funds during an individual's working life and save it for their retirement needs. However, Provident Fund schemes and the tax rules also allow the accumulated corpus to cover specific financial emergencies. To withdraw these funds, one needs to fill out and submit the appropriate form. There are different types of PF claim forms depending on the purpose of the withdrawal.

Complete EPF Withdrawal

EPF can be withdrawn completely in the following circumstances:

  • When a person retires.
  • When a person is out of work for more than two months, individuals must obtain an attestation from a gazetted officer or manager confirming unemployment.

Individuals cannot withdraw their entire EPF balance while changing jobs. If they have not been unemployed for two months (which is generally the gap period between changing jobs).

Partial EPF Withdrawal

Only in emergency circumstances can a portion of an EPF balance be withdrawn.

EPF Form 10C

This form can be used by members to apply for withdrawal benefits or EPS certificate while obtaining membership under the Pension Fund. Retention of membership will allow individuals to enjoy the benefits of the Pension Fund later.

The following categories of members are eligible to withdraw funds from their Pension Fund using EPF Form 10C:

Category 1

  • Has not completed his/her job before completing 10 years of service.
  • Has not completed 10 years of service but attained the age of 58.

Category 2

  • Legal heir or nominee or family members of a deceased individual who did not complete 10 years of service but had attained the age of 58.

EPF Form 10D

This form is used by monthly pension benefits by individuals who have retired. It shall be filed by the legal claimant, i.e. the member or their legal heir/nominee in case of a deceased member.

Individuals need to complete 10 years of service in the formal sector before being eligible to receive monthly pension benefits.

It is crucial to note that individuals who have not attained 58 years of age are eligible to receive a pension only at a reduced rate, whereas, individuals who have reached 58 years can enjoy full pension benefits.

However, individuals who apply after reaching the age of 58 years are eligible to receive full pension benefits even if they have discontinued the service before completing 10 years.

EPF Form 19

The PF withdrawal form 19 is used to apply for the final settlement of the accumulated corpus in a member's EPF account. This form is relevant to individuals who wish to withdraw their entire PF balance. To apply for this form, a primary requisite is that such members shall at least be unemployed for 2 months.

Completion of form 5 (Registered employee for Employees' Provident Fund Scheme) is to be completed by the firm and submitted by the 10th of every month. A small portion, outside of employees' basic monthly wage, has been made mandatory under the EPFO scheme, or Employees' Provident Fund Scheme. EPF Form 15G

In case an individual withdraws funds from their EPF account before completing 5 years of service with an earning of more than Rs. 50,000 in their account, the earning will be subject to tax deducted at source (TDS).

Nevertheless, if such person's total income including the provident fund proceeds does not exceed the minimum tax slab for the year, then such an individual can file an EPF form 15G to escape the same, following which the organization shall not deduct any tax.

It can also be submitted by a member to avoid a TDS levy on interest earned from their EPF balance. If the member is below 60 years and expects their income to be below the taxable limit, they can submit Form 15G.

EPF Form 11

This new PF withdrawal form contains an employee's details such as bank account number, EPF and EPS account numbers etc. An employee shall file it through his/her existing employer and act as a declaration of their EPF membership.

When individuals change jobs, they can present this form to their new employer, thus enabling such new establishments to transfer their existing accounts. Additionally, in case their UAN is activated, their previous EPF and EPS accounts are transferred, and EPFO is notified of such individuals' change in employment status.

EPF Form 31

One can apply for the PF withdrawal form 31 online at the EPFO portal for withdrawal of funds from a member's EPF account. The essential eligibility for filling Form 31 is only applicable when the withdrawal is for:

  • An applicant has retired.
  • Such an applicant is unemployed for 2 months or more, which is to be certified by a gazetted officer.

EPF Form 14

This form is used to apply for a financing facility, wherein an individual's LIC policy premiums are paid from their member's EPF account.

Individuals applying for this facility shall attest this form with their employer and furnish it to the respective EPF commissioner.

EPF Form 19

Individuals who decide to close their existing EPF account shall use the EPF withdrawal form to apply for their accumulated corpus. It is crucial to note that such an application is only legitimate when the individual has remained unemployed for 2 months.

EPF Form 2

This form acts as a declaration and nomination certificate, wherein details of the nominated individual shall be furnished. The nominee shall be authorized as the legal claimant in the event of an employee's death.

EPF Form 20

In case of death of an employee, his/her nominee or legal heir shall use the PF withdrawal form to claim a final settlement of the deceased member's EPF account.

EPF Form 5(IF)

In the event of the death of an employee during service, his/her legal heir or nominee shall apply for insurance benefits under the Employees' Deposit Linked Insurance (EDLI) using EPF Form 5(IF). It shall be attested by the previous employer or a gazetted officer.

Some of these forms can only be filled in offline mode and the application must be made either physically or online. To apply physically, one shall download the complete claim form (Aadhaar or Non-Aadhaar), duly fill and submit it to the nearest EPFO office.

For cases where the member experiences an issue and wishes to file an appeal, one needs to hold an active UAN, which should be KYC compliant to make an application for PF withdrawal.

Process to Apply for EPF Withdrawal Online

Step 1: Navigate to the UAN portal.

Step 2: Enter your UAN and password. Fill in the captcha.

Step 3: Go to the 'Manage' page and click 'KYC' to see if your KYC details, such as Aadhaar, PAN, and bank details, have been uploaded.

Step 4: Once the KYC information has been verified, navigate to the 'Online Services' tab and click 'Claim (Form - 31, 19, & 10C)' from the drop-down menu.

Step 5: The following screen will reveal the member's information, KYC information, and other service information. Double-check that all the information displayed is correct.

Step 6: Click 'Yes' to sign the undertaking certificate and then proceed.

Step 7: Finally, click 'Proceed for Online Claim'.

Step 8: In the claim form, under the heading 'I Want To Apply For', select the claim you desire, i.e., full EPF settlement, EPF part withdrawal (loan/advance), or pension withdrawal. If a member is ineligible for any of the services, such services will not be visible in the drop-down menu. Ensure that the form suits the reason for your withdrawal. You may need to submit the form through your previous employer if your current employer does not have your digital signature registered with EPFO.

Step 9: Then, to withdraw your funds, select 'PF Advance'. Include the purpose of the advance, the amount required, and the employee's address.

Step 10: Submit your application by clicking on the certificate. You may be required to submit scanned documents for the purpose for which you filled out the form. Only you will receive money in your bank account if your employer has approved your withdrawal. The withdrawal shall take 5-10 days if the form is filled correctly and the bank account is linked with your UAN.

How to Check EPF Claim Status

EPF withdrawals are possible via the UAN member portal. The member must first activate his UAN before logging into the portal to make an online withdrawal. The portal can also be utilized to transfer money from his old PF account to his new one. The portal is also essential for an EPF account holder to check KYC details, such as updates, and so on.

The EPF member portal allows you to check the status of your EPF withdrawal online. You must log in to the portal using your UAN and password, then navigate to the 'Online Services' tab and select 'Track Claim Status' from the drop-down menu. You'll need to input a reference number to verify the status. It'll be displayed on the screen immediately.

Eligible Conditions for Withdrawal

The prerequisites that an employee must meet in order to be eligible for EPF withdrawal are as follows:

  • Only after retirement may the complete sum from the EPF account be withdrawn. EPFO considers early retirement to be withdrawal after the age of 54.
  • Partially withdrawing EPF funds is only permissible in the event of a medical emergency, home acquisition or renovation, marriage, or debt.
  • EPFO permits a withdrawal of 90% of the whole amount one year before retirement.
  • If an employee is laid off or discontinues his/her job for his/her own accord, he/she is eligible for EPF withdrawal.
  • According to the new rule, only 75% of the corpus can be withdrawn after one month of unemployment. After two months of unemployment, the employee is eligible to withdraw the remaining 25%.
  • Employees are not required to obtain permission from their employer before withdrawing their EPF. They can receive permission online by attaching their UAN and Aadhaar to their EPF account.

To initiate the claim online, you must have:

  • An active UAN
  • Bank details that are linked with UAN
  • PAN and Aadhaar details

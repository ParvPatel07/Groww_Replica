Kisan Vikas Patra (KVP)

Offered by the Government of India, Kisan Vikas Patra (KVP) is a savings scheme available in post offices across India. It is a safe and secure investment option for individuals looking to double their investment over a fixed tenure. The scheme offers attractive interest rates and comes with various benefits, making it a popular choice among investors.

KVP is a fixed-tenure savings instrument that guarantees a fixed return on investment. The amount invested doubles upon maturity, which is currently set at 124 months (10 years and 4 months). The interest rate is determined by the Ministry of Finance and is subject to change quarterly. The current interest rate is 7.5% per annum, compounded annually.

KVP Interest Rate

While high KVP interest rate ensures the return generated is sizeable, there are also kinks in investment options which make them popular among Indians. Initially launched by India Post in 1988, this scheme had been revised in 2014 and re-launched in 2015 to improve its security and lure investors at that time. The scheme is also suitable for urban residents with average to low income.

KVP plans are available in 2 types which an investor can opt for. While the KVP interest rate remains the same for both, one can either opt for a single certificate or a joint certificate with another individual, depending on their preference.

  • Single Holder: These are issued to a single investor or for an adult who is applying for a minor.
  • Joint A: These certificates are issued to two investors and are payable to both the investors jointly.
  • Joint B: Last of the types of KVP, these certificates can be obtained by two investors jointly too, though the amount is payable to either of the investors.

KVP Interest Rates

The Finance Ministry of India decides the Kisan Vikas Patra interest rate which can vary over different quarters in a financial year. Once an individual invests his/her savings in a specific quarter, the prevailing interest rate is applicable for the entire tenure.

Consequently, the applicable KVP interest rate also affects the maturity tenure for the investment in a specific quarter. The prevailing interest rate on investments in KVP stands at 7.5%. Resultantly, these policies have a maturity tenure of 123 months.

While it is crucial to know the Kisan Vikas Patra interest rate 2023 along with its investment tenure, it is also essential for investors to understand where they can apply for these certificates. Investors can apply for these certificates at any post office branch across India.

Interest Applicability on Premature Withdrawal of KVP

Premature withdrawal of investments made in KVP is not allowed within 30 months of investment. Beyond that period, premature withdrawal is permitted, but the interest earned is at a lower rate than the rate at which the investment would have matured. The exact interest rate depends on the period for which the investment was held. There is no depreciation in the KVP interest rates.

Benefits of KVP

While the KVP current interest rate makes it an interesting option for investment, individuals need to understand the numerous benefits which come with this policy.

  • Flexibility in Investment amount: The certificates which are offered by post offices have values of Rs. 1,000, Rs. 5,000, Rs. 10,000 and Rs. 50,000 which investors can opt for. This ensures flexibility for small as well as large investors. One can also invest any amount by purchasing multiple certificates.
  • Easy Application: Application procedure for KVP certificates is quite easy explained in detail later. Investors can apply for KVPs from any post office or authorized financial institution. The application forms are readily available at these locations or can be downloaded online.
  • Date of premature withdrawal: After a minimum tenure of 2 years and 6 months has been completed from the date of investment, premature withdrawal is allowed under this investment scheme without any penalty charges.
  • Fixed return: Return on this investment scheme is guaranteed under government regulations. Even if the Finance Ministry changes the prevailing rate of interest in future quarters, it will not affect the interest rate on your KVP. Unlike market-linked investments, the returns are fixed and predictable, providing security to investors.
  • Ease of transfer: A major advantage of investing in these certificates is that they can be transferred easily. It is quite simple to shift ownership from one post office to another, as well as transfer to another individual. This makes it convenient for investors who may need to relocate or gift the certificates.
  • Loan options: Investors can use KVP certificates as collateral while availing loans form specific financial institutions. With these certificates serving as collateral, secured loans are available at affordable interest rates and with favorable terms. This provides investors with access to funds when needed without having to prematurely withdraw their investment.
  • Tax benefits: While KVP interest rate offers substantial returns, the Government of India does not provide any tax benefit on these investments under Section 80C of Income Tax Act. However, the interest is not subject to TDS (Tax Deducted at Source).

Another benefit of investing in these certificates is that if the money is not withdrawn after the completion of tenure maturity, post offices offer additional interest on the existing corpus. Though this interest rate is not as high as KVP interest rates, it is the same as that of savings account at the post office.

Eligibility Criteria and Document Requirements for KVP

The eligibility criteria which are vital to be able to invest in KVP are:

  • Applicant should be a resident citizen of India.
  • He/She should be at least 18 years of age.

However, NRIs, PIOs, and OCIs are not eligible to invest in this scheme. Additionally, Hindu Undivided Families (HUF) cannot avail the benefits of this scheme either.

The documents necessary to purchase Kisan Vikas Patra and avail the benefits of KVP current interest rate are enumerated below:

  • KVP documents for identity and address proof. It includes any among Aadhaar card, Voter ID card, Passport, Driving Licence, etc.
  • Passport-sized photograph.
  • Fully filled-in application form for KVP. It can be collected from a post office or downloaded from the official website of India Post.
  • Birth Certificate.

Application Process for KVP

The application procedure for KVP investment is quite easy. Once an investor has checked the KVP interest rate and is ready to invest, they can follow the simple steps enumerated below and get started:

  • The form for KVP should be collected and filled in correctly.
  • The different documents mentioned above should also be submitted along with the form.
  • After submission of all forms and documents, payment of the amount to be invested should be completed too.
  • Once payment is complete, post offices or the concerned financial institution immediately issue the certificates. In case they do not have a sufficient number of certificates, a receipt is issued instead, which can be exchanged later for the certificates.

Applicants for KVP certificates with authorized financial institutions also requires you to follow a similar procedure.

This investment is applicable from the day of payment and investors must keep their KVP certificates safe. This certificate is necessary to withdraw the total amount at the time of maturity.

Who Should Consider Investing in KVP?

Designed specifically for the semi-written and rural population of India, any investor who has disposable money, with a minimum of Rs. 1,000 can buy a KVP. As noted in the previous sections, KVP interest rates which are offered at the various blocks are quite high, and the return is assured. This makes it a good saving option for any individual who does not prefer to invest in instruments exposed to market risks.

Consequently, it can also be a good investment avenue for the urban population, especially for low-income groups who do not have access to pension benefits. Kisan Vikas Patra interest rate 2023 offers to double the investment within 112 months, making it a safe savings option.

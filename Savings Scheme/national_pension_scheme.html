National Pension Scheme (NPS)

NPS full form stands for National Pension Scheme. NPS is an initiative undertaken by the Government of India with the goal of encouraging all residents to save towards retirement. NPS aims to inculcate the habit of saving for retirement amongst the residents.

Here, we will cover the objectives of NPS, types of NPS accounts, interest rates and benefits. Before that, let us first look at the purpose of the NPS scheme in India.

What is the purpose of NPS Scheme India?

The purpose of the NPS scheme is to provide financial security to individuals after retirement. It is a long-term investment plan that encourages individuals to save regularly throughout their working life to build a substantial corpus for their retirement.

What is National Pension Scheme?

Regulated and administered by the PFRDA or Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority under the PFRDA Act 2013, NPS is a voluntary, defined contribution scheme that is market linked and managed by professional fund managers.

Under NPS, individual savings are pooled in a pension fund which is invested by PFRDA registered professional fund managers into diversified portfolios comprising Government Bonds, G-Secs, Corporate Debentures, and Shares.

Contributions made by individual subscribers to the National Pension Scheme under this system are accumulated until retirement. A portion of the accumulated corpus can be withdrawn as a lump sum payment at the time of retirement or opting for superannuation. However, this scheme ensures that a part of savings is utilized to purchase an annuity plan for regular pension post retirement.

Thus, at retirement, or at superannuation, at least 40% of the contribution is utilized for the procurement of a lifetime pension via the purchase of an annuity. The remaining funds are paid to the subscriber in a lump sum.

Objectives of National Pension System

Now that you know what is NPS scheme, let us understand its objectives:

  • A retirement corpus creation for one's retirement phase is an essential aspect for long term financial planning.
  • Not only does it allow investors to meet their expenditure requirements but also allows them to fulfill their post-retirement life with their loved ones.
  • To address the concerns of the growing elderly urban demography in the country, the Indian Government has introduced schemes like the National Pension System or NPS.
  • The scheme allows for a systemic saving during one's working years, thus inculcating a financial discipline amongst individuals to save for the future.

Features of National Pension Scheme

Following are the key features of National Pension Scheme:

Liquidity and Flexibility via Two Different Account Types

The National Pension System allows individuals to make systematic investments, available in two account types:

Pensions and Investment options via two Different options

Subscribers can opt for either of the following two investment options, that providing the flexibility of choice:

  • Auto Choice

    • This is a default option for subscribers. As per the system, fund investments under this option are managed automatically by an appointed fund manager as per an investor's age profile.

  • Active Choice

    • Under this option, individuals can have to decide among suitable asset classes in which to invest their funds. Also, individuals would have to choose amongst the following three asset classes Equity or Asset Class E, Corporate Debt or Asset Class C, and Government Debt or Asset Class G. Other Asset Classes include Asset Class I - Alternative Investments.

Types of NPS Accounts

Tier I account: This is a mandatory account for all NPS subscribers. Contributions to this account are eligible for tax benefits. Withdrawals from this account are restricted until retirement.

Tier II account: This is an optional account that can be opened by a Tier I account holder. It offers more flexibility in terms of withdrawals compared to the Tier I account. However, contributions to this account do not provide any tax benefits.

Returns of National Pension Scheme

The interest rate on NPS is determined by the performance of the assets. As a result, the amount of return received upon retirement cannot be predicted in advance.

NPS is a market-linked product that allows you to invest in a variety of assets, such as stock, government debt, corporate debt, and alternative assets.

Once the asset mix and fund manager are determined, the money is invested in specific schemes that invest in these four asset classes.

How to Apply for NPS Account

Here's how to open a National Pension Scheme online and offline:

How to Open NPS Account Online

  • Individuals can register and obtain a subscription to the National Pension System through the online platform of NPS. Eligible for the scheme can be done at the following steps:
  • Step 1 - Go to the NPS website and select the 'Online Subscriber Registration' option.
  • Step 2 - Choose your subscriber type from the available options: 'Individual Subscriber' and 'Corporate Subscriber'.
  • Step 3 - Choose your residential status: 'Indian Citizen' or 'NRI'.
  • Step 4 - Opt for either Tier I account type or both accounts, as the former is mandatory for long-term savings.
  • Step 5 - Enter your PAN details and select a suitable bank or PoP. It is ideal to choose a PoP with whom you have an existing relationship, such as a savings/current/demat account for KYC verification, as the chosen PoP will do it.
  • Step 6 - Upload the scanned copy of your PAN card along with a cancelled cheque. The image format should be in .jpeg or .jpg and should not exceed 2 MB.
  • Step 7 - Next, upload your scanned photograph and signature in the same format and size as above.
  • Step 8 - Once routed to the payment gateway, proceed to pay the required charges via Net Banking.
  • Step 9 - With the completion of payment, your Permanent Retirement Account Number will be generated.

How to Apply for NPS Account Offline

To open an NPS account offline or manually, you must first locate a PoP - Point of Presence (which might be a bank), register yourself, and fulfil KYC requirements. You will then receive a PRAN - Permanent Retirement Account Number - once you make the initial contribution (not less than Rs. 500), Rs.1000 monthly, or Rs.1,000 annually).

This number, along with the password in your sealed welcome kit, will assist you in operating your account. This information also helps in tracking the account online.

How to Log in to the NPS Account

After your NPS account is created and PRAN is allocated, log in to your E-NPS account through various mediums, such as the NSDL NPS portal, the KFintech portal or net banking account.

Let's look at the steps to log in to your NPS account:

Through NSDL NPS Portal

  • Visit the official portal of NSDL NPS at https://enps.nsdl.com/nsdl/national-pension-system.xhtml.
  • Click on "Login with PRAN/IPIN".
  • Log in as "Subscriber".
  • Enter PRAN and password, enter the captcha, and click on Submit to access your respective E-NPS account.

If you are logging in for the first time, you will have to create a new password using the following steps:

  • Open the official portal of NSDL NPS at https://enps.nsdl.com/nsdl/national-pension-system.xhtml.
  • Click on "Login with PRAN/IPIN".
  • Log in as "Subscriber".
  • Click on "Reset Password" to generate a new password.
  • You will be asked to enter your PRAN, date of birth, and new password. Confirm the password and enter the CAPTCHA. Finally, click on Submit.
  • A confirmation message will be displayed. Enter the OTP on the screen to confirm your new password. Now you will be able to log in to your E-NPS account using your PRAN and new password.

Through KFintech NPS Portal

  • Visit the KFintech NPS portal at https://www.kfintech.com/.
  • Click on "NPS Login".
  • On the login screen, enter your PRAN, password and Captcha code to login.

If you are logging in for the first time, follow the steps mentioned below:

  • Visit the KFintech NPS portal at https://www.kfintech.com/.
  • Click on "NPS Login".
  • Click on "Generate Password".
  • Enter your PRAN and date of birth.
  • You will receive an OTP on your registered mobile number and email ID.
  • Enter the OTP and set a new password.
  • Now you will be able to log in to your NPS account using your PRAN and new password.

Tax Benefits on National Pension Scheme

Tax benefits for National Pension Scheme investments are available under the following sections:

Applicable Sections under the Income Tax Act 1961 Tax Benefits Allowed
U/S 80CCD (1) Own contribution of a subscriber towards Tier I investments is tax deductible within the total ceiling of Rs. 1.5 lakh u/s 80C.
U/S 80CCD (1B) In addition to deductions under section 80CCD (1), subscribers are allowed up to Rs. 50,000 as deductions towards Tier I contributions.
U/S 80CCD (2) Contribution of an employee towards Tier I investments is eligible for deduction up to 10% for central government contributions and up to 14% for others. This deduction is over and above the deduction limit applicable u/s 80C.

The National Pension Scheme Details has other tax benefits on NPS Tier I investments including -

  • Up to 20% of Tier I contributions withdrawn by a subscriber are exempt from tax.
  • Annuity purchase from the National Pension Scheme corpus is tax-exempt. However, income generated from such an annuity in the following years is taxable.
  • Lump-sum withdrawal of up to 60% of an NPS corpus after a subscriber turns 60 is exempt from tax.

Thus, after 60 years of age, if the total corpus created through the National Pension System amounts to Rs. 20 Lakh, a lump sum withdrawal of 60%, i.e., Rs.12 Lakh, will not attract any tax.

Further, if you utilise the remaining 40% of funds for annuity purchase, the entire corpus will be tax-free. Only the income generated from the annuity will be taxable.

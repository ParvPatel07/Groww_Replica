Post Office Monthly Income Scheme (POMIS)

Post office monthly income scheme (POMIS), an official scheme such as Post Office Savings Account, Post Office Recurring Deposit, Post Office Time Deposit, is one of the highest-ranking schemes with an interest rate of 7.4%.

This scheme is run by the Government of India. Currently, this scheme, like other post office schemes, is compulsory and enforced by The Ministry of Finance.

The Post Office Monthly Income Scheme Account was made effective from October 1, 2023. The interest rate was revised and is now 7.4% per annum, payable monthly.

Features of POMIS

  • Individuals can open the Post Office Monthly Income Scheme account with a minimum deposit of Rs. 1,000 and in multiples of Rs. 1000. There is no upper limit to the deposit amount in case of single account holders.
  • Individuals can open the Post Office Monthly Income Scheme account with a minimum deposit of Rs. 1,000 and in multiples of Rs. 1000. The maximum limit for a single account is Rs. 9 Lakh and for a joint account is Rs. 15 Lakh.
  • Joint accounts can be opened by two or three adults. Each joint account holder will have an equal share in the investment.
  • Multiple accounts can be opened by an individual or jointly, but the total deposit in all accounts should not exceed the maximum limit.
  • Minors above 10 years can open an account in their own name. For minors below 10 years, the account can be opened by a guardian. The minor can operate the account after attaining the age of 18 years.
  • Nomination facility is available.
  • The tenure of the scheme is 5 years.
  • Interest is paid monthly to the investor's post office savings account. If the monthly interest is not claimed by the investor, it does not earn additional interest.
  • Auto-withdrawal: You can opt to withdraw the monthly interest amount to your savings account automatically. This requires linking your POMIS account to your post office savings account.
  • The monthly interest amount can be directly credited to your post office savings account or can be withdrawn in cash or through post-dated cheques.
  • Tax benefits: The interest amount does not attract Tax Deduction at Source (TDS). However, it is taxable in the hands of the investor.

Documentation Required

  • Identity Proof: Copy of government-issued ID such as Passport / Voter ID card / Driving License/Aadhaar, etc.
  • Address Proof: Government-issued ID or recent utility bills.
  • Photographs: Two passport-size photographs.

Current Interest Rates on Post Office Monthly Income Scheme

The interest rate on Post Office Monthly Income Scheme is fixed by the Central Government and varies based on the prevailing economic conditions. The interest rates are revised quarterly and are declared by the Department of Posts. The interest rates offered are often revised every year depending on the returns generated by government securities of similar tenure.

As of October 1, 2023, the interest rate for Post Office Monthly Income Scheme will be 7.4% per annum, payable monthly.

How to Open a POMIS Account

Opening a Post Office Monthly Income Scheme Account (MIS) is easy and hassle-free. However, to invest in the scheme, you need to have a Post Office Savings Account. After opening a savings account with the Post Office, you can visit a post office and follow the steps below for opening a POMIS account:

  1. Step 1: Visit the nearest post office branch and get the POMIS application form.
  2. Step 2: Fill in the form with the required details, such as personal information, nominee details, and investment amount.
  3. Step 3: Submit the filled form along with necessary documents, such as identity proof, address proof, and two passport-sized photographs.
  4. Step 4: You will need to show the original documents mentioned above for verification purposes.
  5. Step 5: Deposit the investment amount in cash or by cheque. If paying by cheque, the date of realization of the cheque will be considered as the account opening date.
  6. Step 6: The interest earned on the investment will be credited to your post office savings account monthly.

This beneficiary can also be nominated after opening the Post Office Monthly Income Scheme in India account.

Eligibility Criteria to Open a POMIS Account

Here are the criteria for eligibility for MIS Scheme in Post Office:-

  • A POMIS account can be opened by a resident Indian citizen.
  • This scheme does not apply to Non-resident Indians.
  • Anyone above the age of 18 can open an account.
  • You can open an account on behalf of a minor who is 10 years old or older. When he/she reaches the age of 18, they will become the account holder.
  • After reaching the age of majority, a minor should apply for conversion of the account in his name.

Early Withdrawal Penalty

Here are the penalty regulations applicable to MIS premature closure:-

  • Before the completion of one year - Zero penalty
  • Between 1st and 3rd year - The entire deposit is refunded with a 2% penalty
  • Between 3rd and 5th year - The entire corpus is refunded with a 1% penalty

Benefits of Post Office Monthly Income Scheme (MIS)

The Post Office Monthly Income Scheme (MIS) is a market-linked investment where the returns are declared upfront and are guaranteed by the Government. It is a good option for monthly income with a low-risk appetite.

These benefits are:-

  • Steady Returns: You would avail a steady flow of income every month on your investment corpus, irrespective of market fluctuations. An interest rate of 7.4% p.a. is fixed by the post office.
  • Reinvestment: You can decide to invest the interest earned into high-yield yielding securities, such as equity shares, equity funds, however, these investment options also entail much higher risk.

Hybrid investment strategies that include fixed income and other market-linked options can help to engage in market-linked investments with potentially higher returns and take lower risk compared to equity shares and funds.

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY) Scheme

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY) is one of the prominent National Savings Schemes offered by the Indian Government. This small deposit scheme has been designed exclusively for securing a girl child's future.

What is Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)?

The Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana is a Government of India backed savings scheme created with the intention to benefit girl children under the 'Beti Bachao Beti Padhao' campaign.

This scheme allows parents or a legal guardian to open a savings account for a girl child below the age of 10 years. The deposits made under this scheme earn a fixed interest rate declared by the government annually.

Eligibility Criteria for Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana

Here are the key Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana details about the eligibility criteria:

  • The account can be opened by a parent or legal guardian of the girl's child.
  • The girl child must be below the age of 10 years at the time of account opening.
  • Only one account is permitted per girl child, and a maximum of two accounts per family are allowed (with an exception for twins or triplets).
  • The girl child must be an Indian resident.

Benefits of Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana Scheme

Here are the primary Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana benefits:

  • Economic SSY is an affordable option when it comes to saving up money for your girl child. Only a minimum deposit of Rs. 250 is required to start the account.
  • Good Interest Rates: The scheme offers high interest rates when compared to other schemes offered by the post office.
  • Tax Benefits: One can claim tax deductions under Section 80C up to Rs 1,50,000. Also, the interest accrued on SSY account and the amount withdrawn at maturity are tax-exempt.
  • Assured Benefits: The returns on the scheme are guaranteed by the Government.

How to Open SSY Account Online?

Investors can apply for Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana through the post office by participating public and private banks. Investors need to submit a few documents and follow the process listed below:

  1. Step 1: Visit the official website of the participating bank or India Post.
  2. Step 2: Download the SSY account opening form.
  3. Step 3: Fill in the form with all the required details of the girl child and the parent/guardian.
  4. Step 4: Attach necessary documents like the girl child's birth certificate and the parent/guardian's ID and address proof.
  5. Step 5: Deposit the initial investment amount (minimum Rs. 250).
  6. Step 6: Submit the form and documents at the nearest bank branch or post office.

How to Apply for Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana

Download the application form from either the RBI website, the Indian Post website, or participating public sector and private banks, and submit it with:

  • Birth certificate of the girl child
  • Identity and address proof of the parent or legal guardian

Following are the key mandatory fields to be filled in Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana scheme form:

  • Primary Account Holder - Name of Girl Child
  • Joint Holder - Name of a parent or legal guardian
  • Other Information
  • Date of birth and Date for the initial deposit
  • Name of the girl child along with Birth Certificate details
  • Details of the parent/guardian like Aadhaar Number, PAN Card, etc.
  • Present and permanent address (As per the ID document of the parent or legal guardian)
  • Details of other KYC proofs such as PAN card, ID card, etc.

How to Open SSY Account Offline

A Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY) account can be opened at any participating bank or Post Office branch. To open an account offline, follow these steps:

  1. Step 1: Go to the bank or Post Office where you want to open the account.
  2. Step 2: Ask for the SSY account opening form.
  3. Step 3: Fill out the application form with the necessary information and attach any supporting papers.
  4. Step 4: Submit the filled application form and documents to the bank or Post Office staff.
  5. Step 5: Your application and payment will be processed by the bank or the Post Office.
  6. Step 6: After processing, your SSY account will be activated. A passbook will be supplied for this account to maintain all the transaction details.

You can check the List of Banks Offering SSY here.

How to Open the SSY Account Online

To make online investment in your SSY account, you must first download the IPPB app to your smartphone. You can use this app to make regular deposits into your SSY account after linking it to your IPPB savings account. Here's how to make payments for Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana online:

  • Step 1: Transfer money from your bank account to the IPPB account.
  • Step 2: Navigate to DOP Products in the IPPB app and choose Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana.
  • Step 3: Enter your SSY account number and the DOP customer ID.
  • Step 4: Choose the installment amount and the payment frequency.
  • Step 5: The app will tell you when the payment has been successfully set up.
  • Step 6: You will receive a notification confirming the successful transaction.

You can also track the account details and transactions using the app.

Tax Benefits of Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana

The Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana offers attractive tax benefits under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, 1961. The amount deposited, the interest earned, and the amount withdrawn at maturity are all tax-exempt.

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana Withdrawal Rules

The SSY withdrawal rules are as follows:

  • After the SSY account tenure is completed, the entire corpus (including the interest) can be withdrawn by the account holder.
  • Partial withdrawal is allowed after the girl child attains the age of 18 years or has passed 10th standard, whichever is earlier. The withdrawal amount is limited to 50% of the balance available at the end of the preceding financial year.
  • The documents required for the Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana account withdrawal are as follows:
    • Application form for the withdrawal of the amount.
    • ID and Address proof.
    • Passbook of the SSY account.
    • All the documents/fees receipt required for the submission while taking admission will be required for the withdrawal.
  • The withdrawal is permitted for the higher education purpose if the girl has attained the age of 18 and has also passed the 10th standard. The withdrawal amount is limited to 50% of the balance in the account.
  • The maximum amount available for withdrawal is 50% of the amount that is available in the previous year. Note that this amount is available for investment in 5 installments or even in a lump sum.

Rules for Premature Withdrawal from SSY Account

The rules applicable for premature closure of the account are as follows:

  • When the girl attains 18 years of age and is about to get married, the premature withdrawal of SSY is permitted. Although, one application will be required to be presented at least 1 month prior to the wedding and 3 months past the wedding to receive the benefit.
  • If the girl becomes a non-resident/Non-citizen, the SSY account will be deemed closed. Note in case of any such status change, the guardian or the girl child will have to inform within one month of the change in status.
  • If, unfortunately, the girl child passes away, the balance available in the SSY account can be withdrawn by the guardian by submitting the death certificate.
  • One can be allowed to close the account for other reasons, too, though the interest earned from the contributions will be the same as the interest rates that are offered by post offices.

Key Pointers Related to Sukanya Samriddhi Account Scheme

  1. The account matures after 21 years of opening the account or in the event of the marriage of the girl child after she gains the age of 18 years.
  2. A premature withdrawal of up to 50% of investment is allowed after the child gains the age of 18 years even if she is not getting married.
  3. Duration of investment: 21 years.
  4. Minimum Investment: Rs 1,000 per annum
  5. Maximum Investment: Rs 1.5 lakh per annum
  6. On maturity of the account, the balance which is principal and interest earned is paid to the girl child on submitting an application along with proof of citizenship, residency and identity.

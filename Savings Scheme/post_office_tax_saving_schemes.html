Post Office Tax Saving Schemes

Post office tax saving schemes are reliable and risk-free investment tools that assure investors of a secure return. These schemes help in wealth creation and also provide tax benefits under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, 1961. These tax saving schemes offer income tax benefits on per different sections of the income tax Act, 1961.

Types of Post Office Tax Saving Schemes for Tax Benefits

The popular post office saving schemes that offer tax benefits are Public Provident Fund, Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana, National Savings Certificate, Senior Citizen Savings Scheme (SCSS), Post Office Time Deposit (POTD) and 5-year Post Office Time Deposit.

Public Provident Fund (PPF)

Individuals can open only one PPF account in their name, and can provide for a joint account there not exist. Nomination facilities are available, and account holders can transfer their accounts from one post office to another.

Key Features:

  • It comes with a 15-year tenure.
  • Investors have to make a minimum deposit of Rs 500 per year. Failing to which the account is discontinued. The minimum limit is Rs 1.5 Lakh per year, and deposits are eligible for tax benefits.
  • The maturity period for the scheme is 15 years and investors can avail a further 5-year lock-in.
  • Funds can be withdrawn partially after 7 years, except during times of medical emergency, funding higher education and change of residency. However, individuals can make a partial withdrawal after completing 5 years of the scheme for specific purposes.
  • Deposits made under this scheme provide tax benefits under section 80C of the Income Tax Act. Additionally, the interest earned is tax-exempted.
  • Investors should remember that interest payout facilities are not available for this account.
  • The current post office PPF interest rate is 7.1% annually.

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana is a popular tax-saving option. This is a scheme offered by the post office for saving schemes under 80C. A legal guardian can open this account for his/her girl child and only one account is permitted for one child with a maximum of two accounts per family.

Key Features:

  • This account can be opened for girls below 10 years.
  • This scheme has a tenure of 21 years, irrespective of the girl's age at the time of account opening. The investors, if the account is opened before the girl reaches the age of 10, can withdraw funds after the girl reaches 18 years of age.
  • Individuals have to make deposits for 15 years starting from the date of account opening. They can renew the account after maturity for a further 6 years.
  • The minimum amount of yearly deposit here is Rs. 250 and the maximum is Rs. 1.5 Lakh.
  • Premature closure is allowed at the occasion of account holder's marriage, but she has to reach the minimum 18 years of age. Similarly, funds can be withdrawn during times of medical emergency/terminal illness and higher education (50%). However, on the occasion of a medical emergency, premature withdrawal is allowed provided the account has completed 5 years from the date of opening.
  • Investments made under this Post office tax saving scheme qualify for tax benefits under Section 80C.
  • The current interest rate offered under Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana is 7.6% annually.

National Savings Certificate (NSC)

Investing in this post office saving scheme for tax benefits inspires individuals to pick this active investment amount at once. They will receive NSC more copier, i.e. principal plus interest at the time of maturity. Other features are:

  • The scheme has a tenure of 5 years.
  • The minimum investment amount is Rs. 1000 and further in multiples of Rs. 100.
  • This certificate can be purchased by an adult, jointly by 2 adults, minors above 10 years, an adult on behalf of a minor, or a guardian on behalf of a person of unsound mind.
  • The total interest accumulated at the maturity period is taxable. However, the interest accumulated in the first 4 years is deemed to be reinvested and is therefore eligible for a tax deduction under section 80C. The interest accumulated in the 5th year is taxable.
  • Individuals can pledge the NSC as collateral for a loan, but they can do so only once during the entire tenure.
  • The interest is earned at the rate of 6.8% annually under this post office tax saving scheme.

Senior Citizen Savings Scheme (SCSS)

Individuals over the age of 60 are eligible to invest in SCSS. Also, individuals over the age of 55 years and under 60 years can open SCSS if they have taken voluntary retirement scheme (VRS) or superannuation.

Key Features:

  • The tenure of this investment is 5 years.
  • Depositors can opt for a joint account with their spouse.
  • Individuals can open multiple accounts, but the total amount should not go above the maximum capped investment of Rs. 15 Lakh.
  • Interest is paid quarterly and is taxable. In case the interest earned in a financial year exceeds Rs. 50,000, TDS is deducted. However, investors can submit Form 15G/15H to avoid TDS.
  • Account holders can close the account prematurely. However, if closed before one year, 1.5% of the deposit amount is deducted as a penalty. If closed after one year but before two years, 1% is deducted.
  • Investors can extend this post office tax saving scheme's tenure for 3 years.
  • The scheme qualifies for tax deduction under section 80C of the Income Tax Act. However, TDS is applicable if the interest payout exceeds Rs 50,000.
  • The investors can earn interest at the rate of 7.4% per annum under this scheme.

Post Office Time Deposit (POTD)

Post office time deposit is one of the prominent post office schemes for tax exemption. This plan is a lot like a bank fixed deposit. Investors can make deposits of different tenures like 1, 2, 3 and 5 years. Other features are:

Key Features:

  • The maximum tenure here is 5 years, and any number of such deposits can be made in one or more post offices.
  • The minimum investment amount in time deposit is Rs. 1,000, and there is no upper limit to it. However, the tax benefit is limited to Rs. 1.5 Lakh.
  • Interest earned on this deposit is taxable under section 80C only if it is a 5-year time deposit. Otherwise, it is taxable.
  • Investors cannot encash their TD before 6 months. For premature withdrawals between 6 and 12 months, Post Office Savings Scheme interest rates are applicable.

The applicable interest rates on time deposit options are as follows:

Period of Investment Interest Rate Applicable
1 year 5.5%
2 year 5.5%
3 year 5.5%
5 year 6.7%

What are the Overall Advantages of these Schemes?

  • Easy Investment: India Post has branches all around the country, which makes it easier for people even in remote areas of the country to make investments.
  • Secured return: These post office tax saving schemes are free from market risks and safe to invest in.
  • Attractive interest rates: The interest rates on these schemes range from 4% to 8%.
  • Simple application procedure: The application or enrollment procedures of these schemes are simple and easy to follow.
  • Minimum deposit: Post office schemes allow individuals to start an investment with as low as Rs.20. Plus, most of these schemes come with no upward limit to investments.

How to Apply for Tax Saving Schemes in the Post Office?

Application forms for most of the post office tax saving schemes are available online at India Post's official website. Download the respective application form from the India Post website, or you can visit your nearest post office to avail a form. Duly fill-up the form, submit it with KYC documents and deposit the required amount to the post office.

Who Should Apply for the Post Office Tax Saving Schemes?

Individuals looking for investment options that meet the following requirements find these post office tax saving schemes as their ideal choice for corpus growth.

  • Offer assured return at the time of maturity.
  • Are not susceptible to market fluctuations.
  • Help them save substantially on income tax.
  • Are not subject to market risks.
  • Are not exposed to principal amount invested.

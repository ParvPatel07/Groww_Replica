Employees' Provident Fund or EPF

The Employees' Provident Fund or EPF is a popular savings scheme that has been introduced by the EPFO under the supervision of the Government of India.

This scheme provides an employee with an allocation (12% of an employee's basic salary and dearness allowance towards EPF). The amount can be claimed at the time of retirement, job change, or for specific needs.

The accumulated amount in the EPF is tax-free and can be withdrawn without paying tax on the return. Employees' fund is a long-term savings tool for salaried individuals in India.

Basically, every employee in India, who works in an organization and falls under the EPF Act, is eligible for EPF. The scheme is a great way to build a solid retirement fund and also offers tax benefits for employees, efficient, and hassle-free.

EPFO – Employee Provident Fund Organization

EPFO or Employees' Provident Fund Organization is a non-contributory body that promotes employee's long-term savings and retirement benefits.

EPFO was established in 1952 and is governed by the Ministry of Labour and Employment. It further administers trust funds and schemes for employees.

Schemes Offered Under EPFO

  • Employees' Provident Fund Scheme (EPF)
  • Employees' Pension Scheme (EPS)
  • Employees' Deposit Linked Insurance Scheme (EDLI)

Objectives of EPFO

  • To provide financial security and long-term savings via an EPF account.
  • To ensure employees' savings are managed and regulated.
  • To accumulate retirement corpus for employees.
  • To ensure employees' savings are protected by law and regulations regularly.
  • To facilitate easy transfer and withdrawal of funds for employees.

UAN and EPFO Portal

EPFO launched the Universal Account Number (UAN) to make account operations such as withdrawal and transfer easier. UAN is a 12-digit number that acts as a single umbrella for all EPF accounts of an employee, even if the employee changes employers. The UAN remains the same.

The UAN provides access to the EPFO member portal, where a member can log in, check the number of Member IDs, update KYC, and transfer or withdraw funds. Employees must, however, activate their UAN to use these services.

EPF Eligibility

  • The Employee Provident Fund is for employees shown on the Public and Private Sectors, which means all employees can join and maintain an EPF fund.
  • Any organization that employs 20 or more salaried individuals is added to the benefits of EPF, as per the law.
  • After becoming an authorized member of the EPF scheme, the employee is obliged to add as well all relevant Employees' Provident Fund benefits, including nomination and transfer of funds, etc.

EPF Interest

For the Financial Year 2023-24, the declared rate of interest offered by the EPF scheme is 8.15%.

The accrued interest amount is credited to investments in an EPF online account at the year's end.

EPF Calculation

The EPF calculator is an online tool that lets you check exactly what will be in your EPF account when you retire. You can use the employer's formula to get an amount, which includes both your contribution and the employer's contribution, as well as the total interest accumulated.

For example:

  • If the salary is ₹20,000, then 12% of this amount would be ₹2,400 (12% x 20,000 = 2,400).
  • 12% of the salary is deducted every month and added to the EPF account.
  • The employer also contributes 12% of the salary, of which 8.33% is contributed to the employee's EPS account, and the remaining 3.67% is added to the EPF account.
  • The combination towards the EPF account would be: 3.67% (employer) + 12% (employee) = 15.67% of the basic salary.
  • The total contribution is calculated as: (15.67% x salary) + interest, which is calculated monthly and added at the end of the year.

How to Transfer EPF Money

  1. To transfer the amount on a job change, EPF can be transferred using the same Universal Account Number (UAN).
  2. Go to the official EPF member portal and fill out the registration form.
  3. Once you have entered the details (login credentials), login.
  4. Click on the Online Transfer Claim Portal and request an EPF transfer with the unique login information as before.
  5. Fill in the required details and transfer claim online. After you log in, submit all information as directed.
  6. After submitting your employer information, you will receive a PIN on your mobile device.
  7. Track your application using the tracking ID that is issued to you.

EPF Benefits

  • Capital Appreciation: The EPF scheme allows the principal amount to be deposited with the EPF scheme. Additionally, accrued interest and monthly returns make principal funds higher and lead to consistent capital appreciation.
  • Corpus for Retirement: A regular and monthly contribution is directed towards the Employees' Pension Scheme. In the long run, the amount builds up a sizeable retirement fund to help build a healthy corpus for retirement.
  • Emergency Corpus: Uncertainties can put stress on the finances, making liquidity prepared for such an eventuality critical. In this kind of situation, you can use your EPF account for emergencies.
  • Tax-saving: The EPF is eligible for tax rebate under Section 80C. As an employment contribution towards the EPF account is eligible for a tax deduction, it helps in tax savings. Employer's share is exempted from tax. Such exemption can be availed up to a specific limit.
  • Easy Premature Withdrawal: Members of EPF can initiate a withdrawal for benefits or partial withdrawal. Various situations, like education, marriage, or illness, allow for partial withdrawal. In case the EPF account remains inactive for a period higher than the specified duration, the balance can be withdrawn as per the rules.

EPF Withdrawal Rules

Individuals may not enter partial or complete withdrawal of EPF. But, such withdrawals can be made only under specific circumstances.

Here is a list of a few withdrawal situations under which individuals can withdraw EPF completely:

  • When unemployment period exceeds more than two months.
  • When an individual wants to switch to or be self-employed. (But the duration without a job should be more than two months.)

Here is a list of a few such circumstances under which individuals can withdraw EPF partially:

  • For education
  • For marriage
  • For purchasing or constructing a house
  • Repayment of home loan
  • Requirement of illness

EPF Taxation

If you withdraw your EPF before 5 years of employment, you will be taxed. EPF withdrawals are not taxed otherwise.

Interest on the EPF is also subject to 10% TDS if the amount is greater than Rs. 50,000 and you have not completed 5 years of service. This is in accordance with Section 192A of the Income Tax Act. If you submit Form 15G/15H, TDS is not deducted.

Withdrawals from EPF are taxable as 'Income from other sources' in case of paying the account. TDS is also deducted from the withdrawal amount. However, if you withdraw after 5 years of continuous service, the amount is not taxed.

Withdrawal Process of EPF

EPF club members can utilize the EPF by submitting a withdrawal application either offline or through EPF online services.

Process of EPF Withdrawal Offline
Step 1Individual must fill up a 'new composite claim form' or a 'composite claim form' and submit it to the EPFO office under their jurisdiction.
Step 2A composite claim form needs to be attested by their employer.
Process of EPF Withdrawal Online
Step 1Individual must have an active Universal Account Number (UAN).
Step 2The mobile number used to activate the UAN should be active as well.
Step 3UAN should be linked with Aadhaar. They would then be able to log in to the EPFO portal and check details with their pass code.
Step 4After submitting all required information, the individual must verify their KYC details and proceed per instructions.

EPFO Information for Grievance

Employees who want to register a grievance can do so using the EPFO's member portal, where they can fill out a grievance registration form and submit it online.

  1. Go to the EPFO Grievance Redressal System website.
  2. Click on 'Register Grievance' where the form will be displayed.
  3. Fill in your information (Name, Employer, employee, EPF assistance).
  4. Enter your UAN and other details.
  5. State your grievance, upload any supporting documents, and submit.
  6. After you have submitted your grievance, you will get an email and SMS notification with a grievance reference number, as well as status updates.
  7. You can also track the status of your grievance using the grievance registration reference number.

EPF Form 20

EPF Form 20 is used to claim the Provident Fund (PF) amount by the nominee or legal heir in case of the death of an Employees' Provident Fund (EPF) member while in service.

What is EPF Form 20?

EPF Form 20 is the application form for claiming the accumulated PF balance in the event of the death of an EPF member. This form is to be filled and submitted by the nominee(s) or legal heir(s) of the deceased member to the concerned EPFO office.

Purpose of EPF Form 20

The main purpose of EPF Form 20 is to enable the nominee(s) or legal heir(s) of a deceased EPF member to claim the full accumulated balance in the member's PF account. This provides financial support to the family in case of the unfortunate demise of the earning member.

Who Should Fill EPF Form 20?

EPF Form 20 should be filled and submitted by the registered nominee(s) of the deceased EPF member. In case there is no nomination, or the nominee is also deceased, the legal heir(s) of the deceased member can claim the PF amount by submitting this form along with the necessary legal documents.

Details to be Filled in EPF Form 20

EPF Form 20 requires the following details to be filled in by the applicant (nominee/legal heir):

  1. Name of the applicant
  2. Relationship with the deceased member
  3. Date of birth of the applicant
  4. Address of the applicant
  5. Name of the deceased member
  6. EPF Account Number of the deceased member
  7. Date of death of the member
  8. Name and Address of the establishment where the member was employed
  9. Date of joining and leaving the establishment (if applicable)
  10. Bank Account Details of the applicant (Account Number, Bank Name, IFSC Code)
  11. Details of other nominees or legal heirs (if applicable)

How to Fill and Submit EPF Form 20

EPF Form 20 can be obtained from the EPFO website or the concerned EPFO office. The applicant needs to fill in all the required details accurately and sign the form. The form should be submitted to the concerned EPFO office along with the necessary documents.

The employer of the deceased member also needs to certify the form and provide relevant details about the member's employment and contributions. The form can be submitted either physically or online through the employer.

Documents Required for EPF Form 20

The following documents are generally required for submitting EPF Form 20:

  • Death Certificate of the member
  • Proof of Identity of the applicant (Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID, Passport)
  • Proof of Address of the applicant (Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, Passport, Driving License, Utility Bills)
  • Bank Passbook or Cancelled Cheque of the applicant (showing Account Number and IFSC Code)
  • EPF Account details of the deceased member
  • Nomination Form (if nomination was made)
  • Legal Heir Certificate (in case of claim by legal heir)
  • Guardian Certificate (in case of claim by a guardian on behalf of a minor)

Processing Time for EPF Form 20

The processing time for EPF Form 20 can vary depending on the EPFO office and the completeness of the application and supporting documents. Generally, it takes around 30 days to process the claim and disburse the PF amount to the applicant's bank account.

Importance of EPF Form 20

EPF Form 20 is a vital form that allows the family members or legal heirs of a deceased EPF member to claim the accumulated provident fund amount. It provides crucial financial assistance to the family during a difficult time and helps them to meet their financial needs.

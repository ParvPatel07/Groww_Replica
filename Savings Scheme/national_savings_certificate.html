National Savings Certificate (NSC)

National Savings Certificate (NSC) is a fixed-income investment scheme that is part of the Indian Government's postal savings system. It is a popular choice for individuals looking for a safe investment option with tax benefits.

What is National Savings Certificate

National Savings Certificate is a savings bond scheme that encourages subscribers, primarily small to mid income investors, to save while saving up on income tax under Section 80C.

Features and benefits of NSC include tax benefits, fixed interest rates, and safety of principal. These certificates can be purchased from any post office in India.

Features & Benefits of NSC

Here are some of the key features of NSC scheme:

  • Interest Rates: The certificates earn a fixed interest rate, which is revised every quarter by the government. The current interest rate is 7.7% annually, compounded annually but payable at maturity.
  • Maturity Period: The scheme offers two distinct maturity periods: 5 years and 10 years.
  • Tax Saving: As a government-backed tax-saving scheme, the principal invested in NSC qualifies for tax savings under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, 1961, up to a maximum of Rs. 1.5 Lakh per annum. The interest earned is taxable in the year of maturity.
  • Minimum Investment: You can invest in the scheme with as low as Rs. 100 and in multiples of Rs. 100, with no upper limit on the investment amount.
  • Accessibility: One can easily buy or pledge NSC from any post office in India. It is easy to transfer the certificate from one PO to another or even from one person to another without implying the interest accumulation of the original certificate.
  • Loan Against NSC: NSC can be used as collateral for securing loans from banks and NBFCs. In such a case, a transfer stamp is put on the certificate and transferred to the bank while disbursing loans.
  • Power of Compounding: Interest earned gets compounded annually and reinvested by default but will be payable only at maturity.
  • Nomination: The investor can nominate any family member (even a minor) so that they can inherit the investment in case of the investor's demise.
  • Encash on Maturity: The investor will receive the entire corpus at maturity. As there is no TDS on NSC payouts, the subscriber should pay the applicable tax on it while filing his income tax returns or paying the advance tax on the same.
  • Premature Withdrawal: Generally, one cannot exit the scheme early except on the death of an investor, or a court order, or on forfeiture by a pledgee who is a Gazetted Government Officer for it.

Who Should Invest in NSC?

The NSC scheme is suitable for individuals seeking a low-risk investment option that provides assured returns and tax benefits. It is particularly beneficial for:

  • Risk-averse investors who prioritize capital safety over high returns.
  • Individuals in lower tax brackets who can benefit from the Section 80C deduction.
  • Those looking for a fixed-income instrument with a predictable return.

Typically, individuals and Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs) can invest in NSC. Trusts and private limited companies are not eligible to invest in NSC. Non-resident Indians (NRIs) are also not allowed to invest in this savings scheme.

Eligibility Criteria for NSC

Below is the list of NSC eligibility criteria that are required to invest in NSC:

  • Resident Indian individuals are eligible to invest.
  • Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs), Trusts, Private and public limited companies are not eligible to invest in NSC.
  • The individual must be an Indian citizen. Non-resident Indians (NRIs) are not eligible to invest in NSC.
  • There is no age limit for individuals to purchase a certificate.

How to Invest in NSC?

You can invest in NSC through offline and online modes. Here is the procedure:

Steps to Invest in NSC Offline:

  1. Step 1: Visit the nearest post office.
  2. Step 2: Collect the NSC application form or at any post office.
  3. Step 3: Fill out the form with name, details.
  4. Step 4: Submit the form with self-attested copies of the required KYC documents.
  5. Step 5: Take the original documents for verification and pay the amount you want to invest.
  6. Step 6: Upon approval, collect the NSC of your application.

Steps to Apply for NSC Online:

  1. Step 1: Open Department of Posts (DOP) net banking and log in.
  2. Step 2: Under 'General Services', select 'Service Requests'.
  3. Step 3: Select 'New Requests' and then choose 'NSC Account - Open an NSC Account (For NSC)'.
  4. Step 4: Enter the deposit amount and select the linked post office savings account (PO Savings Account).
  5. Step 5: Choose 'Click Here' to read through the terms and conditions. Accept them once done.
  6. Step 6: Enter the transaction password and click on 'Submit'.
  7. Step 7: The deposit will be generated. Click 'OK' to download.
  8. Step 8: Login and click on 'Accounts' to view the details of your NSC account.

Documents Required to Apply for NSC

Below are the documents required to be submitted in order to invest in an NSC:

  • The NSC application form.
  • Investors need to provide an original identification proof such as Passport / Voter ID / Driving License / Aadhaar.
  • Permanent Account Number (PAN Card)
  • Passport-size photographs.
  • Address proof like electricity bill, Passport, telephone bill, bank statement along with a cheque.

NSC Tax Benefits

Though there is no maximum limit on the purchase of NSCs, only investments of up to Rs 1.5 lakh annually can earn the subscriber tax savings under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, 1961.

Additionally, the interest earned on NSC annually, for the first 4 years are deemed to be reinvested (i.e. added back to the initial investment) and hence, also eligible for a tax break, subject to the overall annual limit of Rs 1.5 lakh. However, the interest earned in the 5th year is not re-invested hence taxable as per the investor's applicable slab rate.

Currently, the NSC interest rate is 7.7%, which is applicable for the current quarter.

Interest Applicability on Premature Withdrawal of NSC

The interest earned on NSC applications are mentioned in the table below.

Time of Withdrawal Interest Applicability
Withdrawal before the completion of 1 year No interest
Withdrawal after the completion of 1 year Interest offered at the rate applicable then.

Further, the specific scenarios under which punishment in NSC can be withdrawn are listed below. During premature withdrawal, the return is calculated as per the National Saving Certificate interest rate though the tenor duration is not considered.

  • The demise of the owner.
  • If the certificate is forfeited.
  • In case a court of law orders the withdrawal of the invested amount.

Every applicant needs to note that while the NSC interest rate remains unchanged, the premature withdrawal of NSC applications are only permitted in the conditions above.

Application Procedure for NSC

The application procedure for NSC is quite simple. Interested applicants can simply apply for the certificate at their nearest post office along with the required documentation. Also, they can make the payment either by cash or cheque.

Duplicate National Savings Certificates Issue

If your original NSC certificate is lost, stolen, destroyed, damaged, or mutilated, you can request a replica.

Simply fill out the Duplicate Savings Certificates form and return it to the post office that issued the NSC that needs to be replaced.

The form's key fields are as follows:

  • Details about the certificate(s), such as serial numbers, denominations, NSC issue, and so on.
  • The date the certificates were purchased.

Along with other information, the purpose for applying for a duplicate certificate must be stated.

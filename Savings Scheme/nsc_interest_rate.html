NSC Interest Rate 2025

National Savings Certificate (NSC), in short, is a secured investment scheme offered by the Government of India. Primarily a savings bond, NSC is provided to individuals through different post offices. The scheme mainly aims to provide easy savings options to average and low-income earners.

Details About NSC Interest Rates 2025

NSC interest rates vary depending on their NSC's maturity. These schemes introduced by the Government of India are available with two tenor variants for maturity. The rate of interest provided as per the maturity periods are listed below, along with their tenure and issue numbers.

The latest NSC interest rate for the current quarter is 8.2%. The interest rate is revised every quarter by the Indian government.

Interest Applicability on Premature Withdrawal of NSC

The interest earned on NSC applications are mentioned in the table below.

Time of Withdrawal Interest Applicability
Withdrawal before the completion of 1 year No interest
Withdrawal after the completion of 1 year Interest offered at the rate applicable then.

Further, the specific scenarios under which investment in NSC can be withdrawn are listed below. During premature withdrawal, the return is calculated as per the National Saving Certificate interest rate though the tenor duration is not considered.

  • The demise of the owner.
  • If the certificate is forfeited.
  • In case a court of law orders the withdrawal of the invested amount.

Every applicant needs to note that while the NSC interest rate remains unchanged, the premature withdrawal of NSC applications requires specific documents which are mentioned above.

Application Procedure for NSC

The application procedure for NSC is quite simple. Interested applicants can simply apply for the certificate at their nearest post office along with the required documentation. Also, they can make the payment either by cash or cheque.

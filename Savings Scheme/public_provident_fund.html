Public Provident Fund (PPF)

PPF - Public Provident Fund, it is a popular investment scheme among investors, courtesy of its multiple investor-friendly features and associated benefits.

What is PPF?

PPF account can be simply coined as a long-term investment scheme popular among individuals who wish to earn risk-free returns while retaining the safety of the principal amount. It is to be noted that the principal amount invested in a provident fund scheme and their aggregate income, however, is not quite beneficial in terms of tax savings.

When a PPF account is opened, the PPF account is scheduled for the applicant where the money is deposited every month depending on their income sources.

Importance of a PPF Account

A Public provident fund scheme is ideal for individuals with a low-risk appetite. Since this plan is mandated by the government, it is backed up with guaranteed returns to protect the financial needs of the investors and their families. Further, investors can also avail income tax benefits under the Section 80C of the Income Tax Act.

Investors can also undertake the public provident fund regime to diversify their financial and investment portfolio. At times of downsizing of the business cycle, PPF accounts can provide stable returns on investment annually.

Features of a PPF Account

This key characteristic of a public provident fund scheme can be listed as follows:

Particulars Details
Interest Rate 7.1% per annum
Tax Benefit Up to Rs 1.5 Lakh under Section 80C
Risk Profile Offers guaranteed, risk-free returns
Minimum Investment Amount Rs 500
Maximum Investment Amount Rs 1.5 Lakh per annum
Tenure 15 years

  • Investment Tenure

    A PPF account has a lock-in period of 15 years on investment, before which funds cannot be withdrawn completely. An investor can choose to extend the tenure by 5 years after the PPF lock-in period is over if required.

  • Principal Amount

    A minimum of Rs. 500 and a maximum of Rs. 1.5 Lakh can be invested in a provident fund scheme annually. This scheme offers flexibility to investors to make lump sum investments. However, an individual is eligible for only 12 yearly instalment payments into a PPF account.

    Investment in a PPF account has to be made every year to ensure that the account remains active.

  • Loan against Investment

    Public provident funds provide the benefit of availing loans against the investment amount. However, the loan will only be available starting at any time from the beginning of any year till the end of the 5th year from the date of opening of the account.

    The maximum tenure of such loans against PPF is 36 months. Only 25% or less of the total amount available in the account can be availed for this purpose.

  • Eligibility Criteria

    Indian citizens residing in the country are eligible to open a PPF account in his/her name. Minors are also allowed to open a PPF account with a legal guardian. Notably, joint accounts are not permitted under PPF.

    Non-resident Indians are not permitted to open a new PPF account. However, any existing account in their name remains active till the completion of tenure. These accounts cannot be extended for 5 years. A benefit available to resident Indians.

  • Interest on a PPF Account

    The interest payable on public provident fund schemes is determined by the Central Government of India. It aims to provide higher interest than regular accounts maintained by various commercial banks in the country.

    Interest rates currently payable on such accounts stand at 7.1% and are subject to quarterly updates at the discretion of the government.

How to Open a PPF Account?

Both offline and online procedures are available for an individual provident fund scheme. Mostly, people partake in the online eligibility criteria. Activating PPF online can be done by visiting the portal of a chosen bank or post office.

Documents Required to Apply for PPF

The following documents have to be presented at the time of activation of a public provident fund account:

  • KYC documents verifying the identity of an individual, such as Aadhaar, Voter ID, Driver's License, etc.
  • PAN card
  • Residential address proof
  • Nomination Form and application
  • Passport sized photographs

PPF Tax Benefits

Income tax exemptions are applicable on the principal amount invested in a PPF account. The entire value of investment and the interest earned at the time of maturity are exempt from the purview of the Income Tax Act of India, 1961. It should be kept in mind that the total principal that can be invested in one financial year should not exceed Rs. 1.5 Lakh.

The total interest accrued on PPF investment is also exempt from any tax calculations.

Therefore, the entire amount redeemed from a PPF account upon completion of maturity is not subject to taxation. This policy makes the public provident fund scheme attractive to many investors in India.

Withdrawal

There are multiple clauses that an individual must adhere to in case he/she wants to withdraw funds from the PPF account.

Mandatory lock-in of 15 years is imposed on the principal amount invested in such plans. In case of emergencies, investors can make partial withdrawals from the PPF account. Notably, they can only withdraw funds after the completion of 5 years of activation of the account. Up to 50% of the total balance can be withdrawn in one transaction each financial year succeeding the 4th year.

Investors should note that funds invested in a PPF account cannot be liquidated before the completion of the mandatory lock-in period, except during certain risks. Also, investment options providing stable policies can easily opt for this government-backed instrument.

Loan Against PPF Scheme

  • Between the 3rd and 5th years of your PPF account, you can take out a loan.
  • The loan amount can be no more than 25% of the second year immediately preceding the loan application year.
  • If the first loan is entirely repaid, a second loan can be taken out before the 6th year.

Procedure to Withdraw PPF Money

The procedure for withdrawing from a PPF:

If you want to withdraw some or all of the money from your PPF account, you can do so.

Step 1: Complete the application form (Form C) with the necessary information.

Step 2: Submit the application to the bank branch where your PPF account is located.

What is Form C

Section 1

It is a declaration section in which you must submit your PPF account number as well as the amount of money you wish to withdraw. Along with that, you must state how many years have passed since the account was first opened.

Section 2

It is about office use and includes information such as:

  • The date on which the PPF account was opened.
  • The balance in the PPF account.
  • The date on which the partial withdrawal was granted.
  • The total amount of withdrawals available in the account.
  • The amount of money that has been authorized for withdrawal.
  • The signature and date of the person in charge - generally the service manager.

Section 3

It requests information about the bank where the funds are to be credited directly or the bank in whose favour the cheque or demand draught is to be issued. It is also required to include a copy of the PPF passbook with this application.

Blue Chip Stocks

Blue-chip companies are the big, sound and stable companies that have remarkable stature in the market. Many traders and Investors choose to opt for blue chip stocks because of their stability and rising dividends.

Blue chip stocks are often considered a good option for investment due to their dependable returns.

What are Blue Chip Stocks?

Blue chip stocks are highly priced market stocks that have emerged as a preferred investment option in recent years. The companies that issue Blue-chip stocks are highly established in the stock exchange market and tend to have a stable financial record and credibility.

Despite their repute, the fact that such companies extend attractive dividend pay-outs can be credited for the growing popularity of the said stock. However, before investing in Blue-chip stocks, individuals should equip themselves with a few essential pointers.

Shares that are issued by Blue-chip companies, i.e. companies with a large market capitalisation, are termed Blue-chip companies. Investors who seek stable returns for a long period and also enjoy capital appreciation from their shares, the shares issued by them are highly valued in the market.

Individuals can invest in Blue-chip stocks in India either directly or through Mutual Funds.

Features of Blue-chip Stocks

The features of Blue-chip stocks are listed below -

  • Assured Returns: Blue-chip stocks generate returns quarterly in the form of dividends. The fact that companies which are well-established also serve as a safe investment avenue for most investors. With this safety comes the assurance of earning steady but guaranteed returns.
  • Credit-worthiness: Blue-chip companies have enough capital to clear their financial dues and obligations easily. This, in turn, makes the shares issued by such companies high in creditworthiness.
  • Risk Factor: Since big companies with stable financial performance issue these stocks, the risk factors associated with blue-chip stocks are comparatively low. Investors can further reduce the burden of risk associated with blue-chip shares by diversifying their investment portfolio.
  • Investment Horizon: Blue-chip companies offer an investment horizon of over 7 years. Such assured term makes Blue-chip stocks suitable for achieving long-term financial goals owing to their long investment horizon.
  • Growth Prospect: Blue-chip companies are those large companies that have reached their maximum growth potential. This influences the Blue-chip shares in India, which undergo slow but steady growth over time.
  • Taxation: The gains generated through blue-chip shares in India are treated as Income under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act. The short-term capital gains are subjected to taxation at the rate of 15%. However, the long-term capital gains exceeding Rs. 1 Lakh are subjected to Taxation at the rate of 10%.

Reasons to Invest in Blue-Chip Stocks

Blue-chip stocks in India are considered to be a desirable investment option for achieving long-term financial goals. Here are a few reasons to invest in Blue-chip shares -

  • Offers higher returns that are paid quarterly.
  • Facilitates corpus building.
  • Facilitates portfolio diversification.
  • Cushions the impact of the recession, inflation and economic stagnation.

However, owing to their higher price, blue-chip shares may not be suitable for small investors.

Major Advantages of Investing in Blue-Chip Stocks

The strong financial standing, excellent market situation, and creditworthiness of Blue-chip stocks act in favour of their Investors and extend multiple benefits to them.

Blue-chip stock Investors would avail the following advantages -

  • Stable and Regular Dividends: Irrespective of the market conditions, Blue-chip stock Investors avail stable returns on their Investments. Such returns are generated in the form of dividends, which are extended to them quarterly.
  • Opportunity to Achieve Financial Goals: Owing to a long term Investment option that comes with an investment horizon of over 7 years, it gives Investors ample time to achieve their long term financial goals and their respective financial plans.
  • Advantage of Portfolio Diversification: Blue-chip companies are high-profit business organisations that do not generate revenue from just one channel. It allows them to cushion their losses against operational setbacks and allows their Investors to spread out the consequences of Investable shares.
  • Liquidity: Blue-chip companies enjoy market goodwill and high creditworthiness. It directly enhances the market value of blue-chip shares. Hence, the shares can be liquidated easily with minimum loss. This liquidity then increases the production and sale of such stocks, making them liquid in return.

Disadvantages of Investing in Blue-Chip Stocks

Blue-chip stock Investors might encounter the following disadvantages -

  • Slow Growth Rate: Blue chip companies generally have a slow growth rate. This means that an Investor would have to wait patiently to gain the expected returns fast.
  • Low Dividend Yields: Since Blue chip stocks have a slow growth rate, they might provide low dividends.
  • Comparatively Expensive: Since Blue chip stocks have reliability, low-risk and less volatility, they are usually in high demand. This makes them expensive in the market when compared to other kinds of similar size.

Alternative Investment Options

Owing to their expensive price, investing in Blue-chip shares in India come with a low risk factor. The low-risk factor can further make them suitable for risk averse and conservative Investors.

However, Investors who want to earn greater returns with limited exposure to market risks can opt for these alternative Investment options:

  • Real Estate: Even though the growth of the real estate market in India has slowed down a little, it is still one of the most profitable Investment avenues in the country. Investors can purchase, sell, or rent constructed and unconsolidated properties and generate income in due course.
  • Exchange-Traded Funds: ETFs are a collection of securities that are traded on exchanges that are considered as a low-risk investment option. ETFs include a basket of stocks, bonds, commodities and other investment instruments and come with a low expense ratio and tax efficiency.
  • Fixed Deposits: Investors who want to generate returns and tax benefits at the same can invest in Fixed deposit schemes instead of blue-cap stocks.
  • Government Bonds: Government bonds are low-risk investment options that are issued by the government of India and endorsed by the SEBI.

Blue-chip stocks in India are an attractive Investment option for Individuals who want to generate steady returns. Investing in Blue-chip shares can always enable Individuals to achieve Investment goals and enhance the profitability of their investment portfolio.

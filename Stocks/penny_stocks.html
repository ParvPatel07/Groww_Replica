Penny Stocks

For most of the penny stocks, very little information is available in the market, learning a few larger companies that can give significant returns. However, you should monitor their performance closely as they are low on prices, there are chances that they may become multi-baggers in the future.

Get the essential details about Penny Stocks here.

What are Penny Stocks?

Penny stocks are shares that are priced very low, mostly under Rs 20 per share, and such companies have low market capitalization, as well as a company's market cap is the value of outstanding shares trading in the market.

Features of Penny stocks

The features of penny stocks are listed below:

  • Illiquid

Penny stocks are illiquid in nature. This means that they are traded in much less of a quantity as compared to other stocks in the market. This makes it difficult to buy or sell these stocks quickly without significantly affecting their price.

  • Return

This is a tricky one. Most often, penny stocks give very high returns. It would be wrong to assume this.

While it is true that since the stocks are priced so low, there is a high potential of the stock price to go up by a couple of 1,000% and give you higher and faster returns when compared to companies that have already reached that stage. But it is highly dependent on the fate of the company. There is a high chance that stocks may become multibaggers.

Knowing at that nascent stage that the stock will give you humongous returns is like taking a shot in the dark. Because mostly there won't be much information available.

  • Multibagger

Some of these Multibagger stocks have the potential to evolve into multi-baggers. It means shares which yield in excess of a 100% return over a period of time are called multibaggers. If you invest in a penny stock that is a potential multibagger, and if it returns ten times its investment value, it is considered a ten bagger.

Including them in your portfolio could exponentially increase your return prospects and might outperform the large cap stocks. But it is important to remember that not all penny stocks will become multibaggers. Careful research and analysis are required to identify potential multibaggers.

Example:

  • If I invested Rs. 5000 in penny stocks of G Ltd., a start-up. Each unit costs Rs. 5.

The firm did well in the market and their penny stock value stood at Rs. 50 at the end of the year. My Rs. 5 investment became Rs. 50, thus gaining ten times the value. The stock is considered a Ten Bagger.

  • Unpredictable Pricing

Penny stocks might not attract adequate pricing due to the less quantity traded in the market compared to other shares. This means that the price of penny stocks can be highly volatile and unpredictable.

Similarly, these stocks could also attract a price significantly higher than your cost. Therefore, resulting in a considerable profit.

Pros and Cons of Penny Stocks

Investing in Penny Stocks comes with a lot of advantages as well as risks. Have a look at the following pros and cons of penny stocks before making your investment.

Advantages of Penny Stocks

  • Penny stocks are extremely volatile, yet they have the potential to give investors extraordinary gains. They have the potential to deliver high growth and profit from these investments, often in a very short period of time.
  • With a very small amount of money, investors can make significant returns.
  • The bulk of penny stock traders starts with small amounts, so individuals with Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 10,000 can easily make a lot of money on the shares of a new company. They can buy a large quantity of penny stocks for the same amount of money.

Disadvantages of Penny Stocks

  • Due to a lack of liquidity, traders find it difficult to cash out. Penny stocks are frequently traded in low volumes, which makes it challenging to sell them quickly without affecting the price.
  • Artificially increasing share prices may result in false representations about the company's position, a sort of fraud or manipulation, commonly known as the "pump and dump" approach. This involves artificially inflating the price of a stock through false and misleading positive statements, in order to sell the dearly held stock at a higher price.
  • The risk of losing your entire investment is higher with penny stocks compared to established companies. Many penny stock companies fail, and their stock can become worthless.

Conclusion

Investing in penny stocks can be exciting due to the potential for high returns, but it is also highly risky. It is essential to conduct thorough research, understand the risks involved, and only invest what you can afford to lose. Diversification is also crucial when investing in penny stocks to mitigate potential losses.

Multibagger Stocks

Multibagger stocks are equity shares of a company that generate returns multiple times higher than its associated cost of acquisition. These stocks were first invested by Peter Lynch, published in his book 'One up on Wall Street'.

What are Multibagger Stocks?

Multibagger shares are issued by companies having tremendous growth potential, demonstrating outstanding performance, and are expected to expand significantly in the future. They are called Multibagger stocks of a company, allowing this product to generate high demand in the market.

However, in some instances, Multibagger stocks might reflect an economic bubble developing in a country as well, especially in instances of low interest rates prevailing in the economy, or other external factors.

What Characteristics Should a Company Possess to Generate Multibagger Shares?

Multibagger stocks are associated with manifold returns on investment. Such profits can only be realized if investors carefully review the company and the stock market before investing.

  • Advanced Research and Development Skills

Robust growth of a company is associated with a massive volume of sales of its product in the market. To achieve this, quality products have to be delivered by such companies, providing immense customer satisfaction.

Considerable investment in research and development of a product has to be undertaken by companies to enlist its product for future services that generate great profits.

Start-up companies launching products having tremendous customer usage scope and no close substitutes are likely to generate massive demand in the market. These companies can increase their paid-up capital by issuing Multibagger shares.

Companies acting as a monopoly or duopoly in the market can also be classified as issuers of Multibagger shares. Such companies, along with entry restrictions, can help companies increase their total revenue generation.

  • High Growth

You can easily identify Multibagger stocks by looking at the performance of an issuing company. Businesses demonstrating high profit generation and balanced debt and equity ratios are likely Multibagger stocks.

These companies tend to have a high Return on Equity (ROE), indicating strong financial management skills. A high ROE indicates that a company is efficiently using shareholder funds to generate profits. Investors should look for companies with a consistently high ROE over a period of time, as this indicates strong financial performance and the potential for future growth.

  • Excellent Management Skills

Multibagger stocks are issued by companies having trained and experienced managers with sufficient technical expertise to guide the production process. Competent management can significantly impact a company's performance and its ability to generate profits. Investors should look for companies with experienced and skilled management teams, as this can be an indicator of the company's potential for growth and success.

Why Should You Invest in Multibagger Stocks?

Multibagger stocks are known to increase investor wealth manifold, as the returns on such investments are tremendous. For example, you can invest in such shares for Rs. 100, and realize profits amounting to Rs. 1000 (an increase of 10 times the initial investment).

However, investment in Multibagger shares has to be kept for a minimum amount of time to ensure extensive capital gain through the conversion of funds to final products sold in the market. Funds obtained from issuing shares in the market are used for production purposes, and the profits generated from the sale of the final product are realized through high profits on massive sales volume.

What is The Risk Associated With Multibagger Shares?

Multibagger shares are subject to the prevailing market trends and external factors of an economy. Therefore, investment in such shares involves high risk as the returns generated from it can be negative in a bearish market.

Many investors purchasing Multibagger shares can get caught up in an economic bubble or value trap. Companies issuing such shares might experience a loss in profit generation at a later time, when the market demand for its product has decreased or the price of its raw material has increased.

Thus, Multibagger shares are a high-risk investment and investors should carefully consider the risks involved before investing in such shares.

This would lead to massive losses incurred by an individual when the bubble pops and the asset value spirals.

Similarly, value traps are a rising possibility when it comes to Multibagger stocks. Products manufactured by a company issuing such shares might not generate sufficient revenue in the market at a later time. This is especially true if the company's products are not in high demand or are easily substituted by other products in the market. Investors should carefully analyze the demand and supply of a company's products before investing in its Multibagger shares.

Thus, investors need to carefully analyze the financial statements of a company and the prevailing situation in the stock market before investing in Multibagger stocks.

Alternative Investment Options Available To An Individual?

Besides investing in Multibagger stocks, individuals can choose from a wide range of investment options available in the market, such as:

  • Debt funds

This corpus of Mutual Funds primarily comprises debt securities issued by a company. Debt financing is a way for companies to raise money by issuing bonds or other debt instruments. Debt funds invest in these debt instruments, providing investors with a fixed income stream. Debt funds are generally considered less risky than equity funds, as they invest in fixed-income securities.

  • Hybrid Funds

Also known as balanced advantage funds, these hybrid funds aim to achieve optimal balance between risk and return. Hybrid funds invest in a mix of equity and debt securities, providing investors with diversification and potentially higher returns than debt funds alone.

  • Large-cap funds

Multibagger shares are generally issued by companies just launching in the market, thus, increasing the risk level of investment. Large-cap funds, on the other hand, choose to invest in equity securities of companies having a market capitalization of higher than Rs. 20,000 Crore.

These companies have a renowned reputation and proven financial strength, thereby reducing the chances of generating low returns. Large-cap companies have adequate financial resources to withstand any stock market downturn as well, thus ensuring the capital preservation of investors.

Multibagger stocks in India are ideal for investors seeking to increase their wealth by a substantial amount through capital appreciation of respective securities. As these stocks have an incremental value multiple times the cost of acquisition, capital gain profits earned are immense. However, investors should be prepared to assume associated risks as well.

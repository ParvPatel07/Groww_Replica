Equity Share Capital

Equity share capital requires substantial working capital to keep their business smooth and running. Such capital proves effective at times when the company is faced with financial restrictions to keep its regular operations active. What are Equity Shares?

Equity Share Capital Meaning - To understand equity share capital, individuals need to familiarise themselves with equity shares.

Equity shares or ordinary shares that represent ownership in a company. Shares sold by a company function as a source of Investment for the company as well.

Also, Individuals can easily transfer equity shares to avail fractional ownership of a company.

Also, benefits of Investing in Equity Shares are listed below -

  • Fair liquidity: Equity shares prices are directly proportional to fluctuations in the market or to the company's revenue generation. They may even be affected on both.
  • Participation in company operations: Apart from the capital appreciation feature of equity shares that also earn regular dividends on their Investments.

An Investor having substantial Investments with a significant per cent of shareholding can Influence a company's management significantly.

What is Equity Share Capital?

The capital of a company raised by affiliating shares is known as equity share capital or share capital. It is the money collected by a company from its shareholders in return for shares. They are paid in the event of liquidation of the company after all of their creditors.

Why Companies Issue Equity Shares?

Companies tend to Invite the general public to acquire Its shares as a means to avail fractional ownership of the same. Through such ownership, shareholders are entitled to earn returns in the form of dividends.

Usually, it largely privately-owned companies Issue shares to trade publicly in a stock exchange.

Also, to avail the equity capital, there are primarily two reasons - Investments from large companies and small companies. Investment from large companies is the primary reason why both small and large companies issue shares to the general public in the first place.

Thus, this equity share capital then raised through equity shares Is used for developing the business venture of the company.

Additionally, a larger capital base helps them to enhance their creditworthiness in the market.

When a company Issues share for Investors to acquire, they also avail an opportunity to earn a share of Its profits in return for Investment through dividends.

Types of Equity Share Capital?

Several types of equity shares help companies generate equity share capital.

The following highlights types of equity share capital -

  • Authorised share capital: The maximum amount of capital that can be Issued by a particular company is known as authorised share capital. This capital amount can be increased later with the permission of the shareholders after they have availed permission from respective authority and have paid the required fees.
  • Issued share capital: The part of authorized share capital that has been issued as Issued share capital.
  • Subscribed share capital: It comprises of the part of Issued share capital, which the Investors agree upon and acquire.
  • Right shares: The shares that are Issued to Individuals after they have Invested in equity shares are known as rights shares. They are offered in proportion to their existing shareholding.
  • Sweat equity shares: As an appreciation for a Job, expertise, companies award their employees or directors with shares. Such shares are known as sweat equity shares.
  • Preference shares: Preference shares are also part of capital of the company Invested In their business.
  • Bonus shares: These shares are Issued to the Investors in the form of a dividend.

Companies make new shares available for the public via Initial Public Offering (IPO) through the Use of Book Building Method.

Risks Associated with Equity share capital?

The drawbacks of equity shares tend to magnify the risks that are associated with equity share capital.

Risks while trying to Generate Equity Share Capital -

  • No takers

Generally, equity experts want to ensure that equity shares Investors these days have a better understanding of how the Investment market functions and which activities would prove more effective for them. They collect substantial data before making any Investment commitments and are aware of future market trends.

If the shares issued by a company do not match the Investors requirements or expectations, they would not be willing to Invest in them. If there are no buyers in the stock market, the company will fail to generate equity shares capital.

  • Inefficient Capital Generation

Usually, it largely privately-owned companies Issue shares to trade publicly in a stock exchange.

Given the uncertainty of how the company would perform as a listed company, Investors would Invest their capital and expect a higher dividend and market value appreciation than what they would receive from Invested shares. Therefore, if the company does not perform as expected after getting listed, it would not be able to generate equity shares capital efficiently.

There are numerous equity share options available to the stock exchange market for Investors to choose from. The availability of a number of Investment options also limits the chance of generating sufficient equity share capital for companies seeking Investments.

  • Higher Liabilities

When companies Issued a huge number of shares at low face values, they run the chance of gaining a larger number of shareholders in the company.

Having a large shareholder base proves effective only in the case when the number of shareholders is within a manageable range. When the number becomes unmanageable, it adds on to the company's liability as well as they have to pay the shareholders out of their profits. Also, it poses the risk of the shareholders interfering with the company's management.

Companies with large shareholder base may also lose control over the company in the event of hostile takeovers. Thus, companies have to incur substantial losses when equity shares are diluted beyond a certain limit.

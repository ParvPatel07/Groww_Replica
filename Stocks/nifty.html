What is NIFTY?

NIFTY, also known as NIFTY 50, is the flagship index of the National Stock Exchange (NSE) in India. It represents the weighted average of 50 of the largest Indian companies listed on the NSE.

Understanding NIFTY

NIFTY is a market capitalization-weighted index that tracks the performance of 50 large-cap companies across 13 sectors of the Indian economy. It is one of the two main stock indices used in India, the other being the SENSEX of the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE).

The name "NIFTY" is derived from the combination of "National" and "Fifty", indicating that it represents the 50 largest companies listed on the NSE.

How is NIFTY Calculated?

NIFTY is calculated using the free-float market capitalization weighted method. This means that:

  • Only the shares that are available for trading in the market (free-float) are considered
  • The weight of each stock in the index is proportional to its free-float market capitalization
  • The base value of NIFTY is 1000, and the base date is November 3, 1995

Importance of NIFTY

NIFTY serves several important purposes in the Indian financial market:

  • Market Indicator: It provides a snapshot of the overall market performance and investor sentiment.
  • Benchmark: It serves as a benchmark for portfolio performance evaluation.
  • Investment Vehicle: It is used as an underlying for various financial products like index funds, ETFs, and derivatives.
  • Economic Indicator: It reflects the health of the Indian economy and corporate sector.

How to Invest in NIFTY?

There are several ways to invest in NIFTY:

  • Index Funds: These are mutual funds that replicate the NIFTY 50 index.
  • Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs): These are funds that track the NIFTY 50 and trade like stocks on the exchange.
  • Futures and Options: These are derivative contracts based on the NIFTY 50 index.
  • Direct Stock Investment: Investors can buy shares of individual companies that are part of the NIFTY 50.

NIFTY vs SENSEX

While both NIFTY and SENSEX are important market indices in India, they have some key differences:

  • Number of Companies: NIFTY tracks 50 companies, while SENSEX tracks 30 companies.
  • Exchange: NIFTY is based on the National Stock Exchange (NSE), while SENSEX is based on the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE).
  • Base Year: NIFTY's base year is 1995, while SENSEX's base year is 1978-79.
  • Calculation Method: Both use the free-float market capitalization method, but their specific methodologies may differ slightly.

Eligibility Criteria for NIFTY Index Listing

The eligibility criteria for getting listed on the NIFTY index are mentioned below -

  • The company must be a domicile of India and registered with the National Stock Exchange.
  • Stocks must possess high liquidity, which is measured by their average impact cost. It is the cost of executing a transaction in the stock. The impact cost of transacting 10 Lakh worth of securities must be less than 0.50% or lower than that for a period of 6 months while 90% of the observations are made on a portfolio of Rs. 10 Crore.
  • The company should have a trading frequency of 100% during the previous six months.
  • It should have an average free-floating market capitalization, which is 1.5 times higher than the smallest constituent in the index.
  • Shares which have Differential Voting Rights or DVR are also eligible for the index.

About SBI Bank

SBI, short for State Bank of India, is the largest Indian bank with an extensive network of about 22,405 branches, over 62,600 ATMs, and 71,705 BC outlets. SBI offers a wide range of banking and financial services to individuals, businesses, and government entities. SBI FDs are a popular investment option, offering competitive interest rates, flexible tenures, and a high degree of safety. The bank provides various FD schemes, including tax-saving FDs, reinvestment plans, and special schemes for senior citizens. SBI is rated AAA by CRISIL. This implies that the FD offered by SBI is one of the safest investment options in India.

How to Invest in SBI FD?

You can now invest in State Bank of India (SBI) FDs through both online methods such as internet banking and mobile app and offline as well. Let's look at how to open an SBI FD as follows:

1) Internet Banking

  • An SBI FD account can be easily opened with internet banking by following these steps:
  • Log in to the SBI NetBanking account (or use the SBI Yono/NetBanking account)
  • Click on the "Fixed Deposit" option and select the deposit scheme as required
  • Fill out all the required details, nominee information, and instructions for maturity
  • Finally, select the tenure and submit the application. The SBI fixed deposit application is complete.

2) Mobile Application

  • An SBI fixed deposit account can be opened through the SBI Mobile app as well:
  • Download the SBI Yono app and login
  • Click on the "Fixed Deposit" option
  • Fill out all the required details and open the Term Deposit option as required
  • Check nominee details and submit the deposit opening process

3) Open SBI FDs Offline

To open an SBI Bank fixed deposit account through the offline method, one needs to visit their nearest SBI Bank branch, get the SBI FD application form and submit the duly filled form with the deposit amount. Once it's successfully processed, the application will be provided with an FD receipt.

Eligibility to Open an SBI FD Account

Investors must check that their eligibility to invest in SBI Bank FD. The eligibility criteria is shown in SBI Bank Fixed Deposit:

  • Indian Resident Individuals
  • Partnership Firms
  • Proprietorship Firms
  • Public/Private Limited companies
  • Trusts
  • Clubs
  • Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) and PIOs (Persons of Indian Origin)

Documents Required to Open State Bank of India FD

The list of documents required to open an FD in State Bank of India is as follows:

  • Duly filled SBI FD application form
  • Recent passport photograph
  • Proof of identity (Aadhaar Card, Passport, PAN card, Voter ID card, Driving License or Passport)
  • Proof of address (Aadhaar Card, Passport, telephone bill, bank statement with cheque, Driving License)
  • Form 60 (in case of no PAN card)
  • Other documents as required by the SBI branch

Tax on SBI Fixed Deposit

SBI offers the SBI Tax Saving Scheme, 2023 in which the minimum deposit amount required is Rs 1,000 and in multiples of Rs 1,000, up to Rs 1,50,000 per financial year. The bank provides tax benefits under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, 1961. Also, note that the interest income from the SBI Fixed Deposit is taxable according to the slab rates of income tax. Also, the interest income is subject to a TDS of 10% if the income from SBI FD exceeds Rs. 40,000 p.a. For senior citizens, the limit is Rs. 50,000 p.a. Also, if the investor fails to provide PAN card details to the bank, the TDS rate will be 20%.

Loan Against SBI FD

SBI provides the facility of obtaining a loan against the FD account. Users can avail up to 90% of the value of their deposit.

Premature Closure of SBI Fixed Deposit

SBI Fixed Deposit Account holders will be charged a nominal fee upon premature withdrawal for long term deposits. For FDs above 5 years, the premature withdrawal penalty is 0.50% for senior citizens and 1.00% for fixed deposits above Rs. 5 lakh, as per the prevailing SBI rules.

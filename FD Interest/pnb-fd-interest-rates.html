About Punjab National Bank

Punjab National Bank is an Indian banking company and state called India's first Swadeshi Bank. It has a network of 10,528 branches, 13,514 ATMs, more than 180 million customers in India.

Punjab National Bank provides multiple fixed deposit options to its customers with interest rates in the range of 3.50% to 6.80%. The depositor is allowed to request an auto-renewal against their term at the time of opening the account. The bank also provides a nomination facility and loan against PNB FD rates along with pre-closure benefits. The account is also provided with an educational account number to track and operate the account.

Features and Benefits of PNB Fixed Deposit

  • Tenure ranges from 7 days to 10 years
  • Interest is compounded quarterly
  • Minimum deposit amount is ₹1,000
  • Loan/overdraft facility available up to 90% of deposit
  • Premature withdrawal facility (with penalty)
  • Nomination facility available
  • Special rates for senior citizens

Types of PNB FD Schemes

Regular Fixed Deposit

  • Standard deposit scheme for all customers, tenure up to 10 years
  • Minimum deposit: ₹1,000
  • Loan/overdraft facility available
  • Premature withdrawal permitted (penalty may apply)
  • Special rates for senior citizens

PNB Scheme for Road Accident Victims

  • Special scheme for compensation funds for providing financial relief to accident victims and accident claimants.
  • Tenure: 7 days to 10 years
  • Loan/overdraft facility available
  • Premature withdrawal permitted (penalty may apply)

PNB Sugam Term Deposit Scheme

  • Special deposit scheme providing flexibility in the amount of deposit along with a higher rate of interest. Under this scheme, the depositor can withdraw in multiples of ₹1,000.
  • Tenure: 7 days to 10 years
  • Loan/overdraft facility available
  • Premature withdrawal permitted (penalty may apply)

PNB Anupam Term Deposit Scheme

  • Special deposit scheme for recurring deposits, with a unique prepayment facility.
  • Tenure: 6 months to 10 years
  • Loan/overdraft facility available
  • Premature withdrawal permitted (penalty may apply)

PNB Balika Shiksha Fixed Deposit Scheme

  • For female students, this fixed deposit scheme is mainly designed for girls who passed the 8th grade.
  • Tenure: 1 year to 10 years
  • Loan/overdraft facility available
  • Premature withdrawal permitted (penalty may apply)

PNB Multi-benefit Deposit Scheme

  • Special deposit scheme, which allows the depositor, along with higher rates of interest, to get part of their deposit amount withdrawn in multiples of ₹1,000.
  • Tenure: 6 months to 10 years
  • Loan/overdraft facility available
  • Premature withdrawal permitted (penalty may apply)

PNB Sanchetan Zinn Vayal Fixed FD Scheme

  • Special deposit scheme for senior citizens, along with prepayment facility. Tenure for this scheme is 1 year to 10 years.
  • Loan/overdraft facility available
  • Premature withdrawal permitted (penalty may apply)

PNB Varishtha Any Deposit Scheme

  • Special deposit scheme for senior citizens, with prepayment facility and special rates.
  • Tenure: 1 year to 10 years
  • Loan/overdraft facility available
  • Premature withdrawal permitted (penalty may apply)

PNB Tax Saver Deposit Scheme

  • 5-year lock-in period, eligible for tax deduction under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act.
  • Maximum investment: ₹1.5 lakh per annum
  • No premature withdrawal allowed
  • Interest paid quarterly or on maturity

Prospective Senior Citizen Term Deposit Scheme

  • Special deposit scheme for senior citizens above 60 years of age, higher rates of interest as compared to normal accounts.
  • Tenure: 1 year to 10 years
  • Loan/overdraft facility available
  • Premature withdrawal permitted (penalty may apply)

PNB Minor Linked Notice Deposit Scheme

  • Special deposit scheme for children, the deposit is opened jointly with a parent, and payable to a Minor, allowed under the scheme.
  • Tenure: 1 year to 10 years
  • Loan/overdraft facility available
  • Premature withdrawal permitted (penalty may apply)

Bulk Fixed Deposit Scheme

  • Special deposit scheme for bulk deposits of ₹2 crore and above, 1 year to 10 years of age under special FD rates, and allowed withdrawal in multiples of ₹1,000.
  • Loan/overdraft facility available
  • Premature withdrawal permitted (penalty may apply)

Term-wise PNB Fixed Deposit Rates

Short-term Deposits

PNB offers short-term deposits ranging from 7 days to 12 months, the interest rate offered is from 3.50% to 5.80% p.a. on deposits. These deposits are best for investors looking for lower tenure FDs and liquidity.

Medium-term Deposits

Medium-term deposits have tenures ranging from 1 year to less than 5 years. These FDs offer higher rates. 1 year PNB FD rate is 6.75%.

Long-term Deposits

These deposits have tenures ranging from 5 years to 10 years and offer a rate of 6.25%.

How to Invest in PNB FD?

A Punjab National Bank FD account can be opened through different modes like Mobile App, Internet Banking, and offline.

1) Internet Banking

  • Sign in to the PNB NetBanking account
  • Navigate to the Fixed Deposit section
  • Fill out all the necessary details, nominee information, and maturity instructions
  • After completing all the details, click 'Open Deposit' and click Save to proceed, and fill all the necessary details along with savings information.
  • After saving, the account is created. Confirmation via validation of the application form and KYC documents. The FD receipt, and other information will be sent to the registered email address. A physical slip will also be sent to the registered address.

2) Mobile App – PNB Bank App

  • Download the PNB Bank app and sign in
  • Click on the 'Fixed Deposit' option
  • Fill out all the required details, nominee, details and type of deposit, details and type of deposit, and KYC details
  • After successful authentication, the FD account will open. An acknowledgement slip will be available for download and record.

3) Offline PNB FD Opening

For opening a PNB FD account via offline mode, one has to visit the nearest PNB branch. Fill out the required PNB FD application form and submit all the necessary documents with the deposit amount. On successful processing of the application, an FD receipt will be provided by the Punjab National Bank.

Eligibility to Open a PNB FD Account

Individuals or entities satisfying any of the following criteria can apply for a fixed deposit scheme from PNB –

  • Individuals and residents
  • Minors, including guardians on behalf of minors
  • Partnership firms
  • Limited companies, trusts, and private limited companies
  • Societies and associations

Documents Required to Open PNB FD

Documents to be submitted to open a Punjab National Bank Fixed Deposit are:

  • Identity proof – Aadhaar card, Voter ID, Card, or driver's license
  • Address proof – any (Passport, telephone/bill, bank statement with a utility bill such as electricity bill and gas bill)
  • Bank account statements

Tax on Punjab National Bank Fixed Deposit

An investment in Punjab National Bank Tax Saving FD entitles to tax saving up to Rs. 1,50,000 p.a. No other Punjab National Bank FDs entitle to tax saving under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act.

Also, note that the interest income from the Punjab National Bank Fixed Deposit is taxable according to the slab rates of income tax. Also, the interest income is subject to a TDS of 10% if the income from Punjab National Bank FD exceeds Rs. 40,000 p.a.

For senior citizens, the limit is Rs. 50,000 p.a. Also, if the investor fails to provide PAN Card details to the bank, the TDS rate will be 20%.

Loan Against Punjab National Bank FD

PNB provides loans against their Fixed Deposits. Investors can get up to 90% of the deposit amount as a loan against PNB Fixed Deposit. The rate of interest will be between 1.5% - 2.0% above the FD rate.

Premature Closure of Punjab National Bank Fixed Deposit

All Punjab National Bank Fixed Deposits can be withdrawn prematurely, excluding the tax-saving Fixed Deposit. Also, penalty is levied for deposits below Rs. 5 lakhs if kept for 12 months. For fixed deposits greater than Rs. 5 lakhs or without that period, the penalty is subject to modification according to the terms and conditions of Punjab National Bank.

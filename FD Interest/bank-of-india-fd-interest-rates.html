About Bank of India

Bank of India (BOI) is one of the oldest and largest public sector banks in India, established in 1906. The bank offers a wide range of banking products and financial services to corporate and retail customers through a network of branches across India and overseas.

Bank of India's Fixed Deposits are a secure investment option that offers competitive interest rates and flexible tenures. The bank provides various FD schemes catering to different customer needs, including special rates for senior citizens and tax-saving options.

Features and Benefits of Bank of India FD

  • Senior citizens can enjoy an additional interest rate of 0.50% p.a. over regular rates
  • The minimum deposit required to open an FD with Bank of India is Rs 1,000
  • Nomination facility is provided by the bank
  • Loan up to 90% of the deposited amount is available
  • Interest is paid quarterly
  • BOI provides multiple deposit schemes to its customers, including Regular FD, Tax Saving FD, Senior Citizen FD, and more

Types of Bank of India FD Schemes

Regular Fixed Deposit

  • Available for all customers
  • Tenure from 7 days to 10 years
  • Minimum deposit of ₹1,000
  • Interest rates up to 7.25%
  • Quarterly interest payout option
  • Loan facility available up to 90% of deposit amount

Tax Saving Fixed Deposit

  • Lock-in period of 5 years
  • Eligible for tax deduction under Section 80C
  • Maximum investment limit of ₹1.5 lakhs per year
  • No premature withdrawal allowed
  • Interest rates as per regular FD
  • Quarterly compounding of interest

Senior Citizen Fixed Deposit

  • Additional 0.50% interest rate
  • Available for customers above 60 years
  • Same features as regular FD
  • Special rates for super senior citizens (above 80 years)
  • Flexible interest payout options
  • Higher TDS threshold of ₹50,000

How to Invest In Bank of India FD

A Bank of India FD account can be opened through different modes like Mobile App, Internet Banking, and offline.

1) Internet Banking

Follow the below-mentioned steps for opening a Bank of India FD account via internet banking-

  • Sign in to the Bank of India NetBanking account
  • Navigate to the Fixed Deposit section
  • Fill out all the necessary details, nominee information and furnish the maturity instructions
  • On successful submission of all the information, the FD account will get booked

2) Mobile App - Bank of India Mobile Banking

Follow the steps mentioned ahead to open an FD account via Bank of India Mobile Banking-

  • Download the Bank of India Mobile Banking App and sign in
  • Click on the 'Fixed Deposit' option
  • Fill out all the required details, nominee information, and provide maturity instructions
  • On successful submission of all the information, the FD account will get booked

3) Offline Bank of India FD Opening

For opening a Bank of India FD account via offline mode, one has to visit the nearest Bank of India branch. Fill out the required Bank of India FD application form and submit all the necessary documents. Next, deposit the required funds to open the fixed deposit. On successful processing of the application, an FD receipt will be provided by the Bank of India.

Eligibility to Open Bank of India FD Account

Consumers can start FD investments in the bank from as low as Rs. 1,000. There is no upper limit for these investments.

The following people or groups are eligible for opening Bank of India fixed deposit accounts -

  • Individuals
  • Partnership firms
  • Minors
  • Visually-challenged Individuals
  • Senior citizens
  • Sole proprietary companies
  • Religious, charitable and educational institutions
  • Hindu Undivided Families
  • Trusts
  • Clubs, societies, associations, etc.
  • Joint account holder comprising of two or more members
  • Panchayati Raj Institutions
  • Limited companies

Tax on Bank of India Fixed Deposit

An investment in Bank of India Tax Saving FD entitles to tax saving up to Rs. 1,50,000 p.a. No other Bank of India FDs entitle to tax saving under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act.

Also, note that the interest income from the Bank of India Fixed Deposit is taxable according to the slab rates of income tax. Also, the interest income is subject to a TDS of 10% if the income from Bank of India FD exceeds Rs. 40,000 p.a.

For senior citizens, the limit is Rs. 50,000 p.a. Also, if the investor fails to provide PAN card details to the bank, the TDS rate will be 20%.

Loan Against Bank of India FD

Bank of India provides loans against their Fixed Deposits. Investors can get up to 90% of the deposit amount as a loan against Bank of India Fixed Deposit. The rate of interest will be between 1-2% higher than the FD rate. However, for senior citizens, the differential rate is 1% above the FD rate.

Premature Closure of Bank of India FD

All Bank of India Fixed Deposits can be withdrawn prematurely except for the tax-saving Fixed Deposit. Also, no penalty is levied for deposits below Rs. 5 lakhs if kept for 12 months. However, there are premature withdrawal penalties for all deposits higher than Rs. 5 lakhs.

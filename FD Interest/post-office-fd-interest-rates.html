About Post Office FD

Post Office FDs are one of the major options in the village postal network. It provides depositors with a safe and secure investment option. The Post Office Fixed Deposit is also called the Post Office Time Deposit (POTD) and is available at all post offices across India. The Post Office FDs are available for tenures of 1, 2, 3, and 5 years. The interest rates are revised quarterly by the government of India. The minimum deposit amount is ₹200, and there is no maximum limit. The account can be opened by cash or cheque, and the interest is paid annually but calculated quarterly.

Features and Benefits of Post Office FD

  • The post office fixed deposit tenures range from 1 to 5 years.
  • Minimum deposit of ₹200, no maximum limit.
  • Account can be opened by cash or cheque at any post office branch across India.
  • Interest is paid annually but calculated quarterly. For 5-year FDs, the interest rate is 7.50% (as of 2023).
  • Transferable from one post office branch to another.
  • Nomination facility is available for deposit holders. Joint or single account holders and two adults can open joint accounts.
  • Premature withdrawal is allowed after 6 months, subject to penalty.
  • Tax benefit under Section 80C for 5-year deposits.
  • Account can be opened in the name of a minor (above 10 years), with a guardian facility. The minimum age for opening is 10 years.
  • Multiple accounts can be opened at any post office branch in India. Post office FDs are not tradable or transferable except on the death of the account holder.
  • The account can be opened in cash or cheque, and a passbook is given. All deposits in post office deposit accounts are backed by the government of India, so they are considered safe and secure.

How to Invest in Post Office FD?

A Post Office Fixed Deposit can be opened at any post office across India using online and offline methods:

1) Online Method

  • The Post Office Fixed Deposit account can be opened online by using internet banking.
  • One needs to visit the official India Post portal for the post office banking/logged-in portal.
  • Click on the "Fixed Deposit" option and proceed as per the instructions.
  • After successful submission, the FD account will be opened and the FD receipt will be available for download and record.

2) Offline Method

  • One can also visit the nearest Post Office branch and collect the form for opening a new Post Office Fixed Deposit account.
  • After submitting the KYC and the necessary documents along with the required savings forms, the post office administrator will guide you through the account creation, and the FD receipt will be provided.

Eligibility to Open a Post Office FD Account

The following people are eligible to open a Post Office deposit:

  • Indian residents as managing account holders, either individually or jointly.
  • Minor children (10 years and above) fixed deposit accounts can be opened in trust, managed by their legal guardian.

HUFs, trusts, companies, and other organizations, however, are not allowed to avail fixed deposit investments through Post Office.

Documents Required to Open Post Office FD

Investors must furnish the following documents for opening a deposit in the POTD scheme -

  • Proof of Address
  • Telephone bill
  • Bank statement
  • Passbook
  • Cancelled cheque
  • Post office issued card or certificate
  • Proof of identity
  • PAN card
  • Voter ID card
  • Aadhaar card
  • Passport

Minor applicants must provide details of a nominee for the FD. A witness must be present when the holder signs. No telecommunication is required.

Tax on Post Office FD

The 5-year Post Office FD as National Savings Term Deposit qualifies for tax deduction under section 80C. It offers tax benefits up to ₹1,50,000 per financial year.

Note that the post office fixed deposit interest paid will be taxable for all depositors. The interest is taxable for those above the exemption limit. As of 2023, interest up to ₹50,000 is entirely exempt from tax for senior citizens.

Loan Against Post Office Fixed Deposit

Loan against FD in Post Office provides the loan up to 95-90% of the Fixed Deposit amount. The interest charged is 2% above the FD interest rate. The maximum tenure for such a loan is the maturity term of the FD.

Premature Closure of Post Office Fixed Deposit

Post Office Fixed Deposits can be withdrawn prematurely after the deposit payment, and withdrawals can be made. After 6 months, premature withdrawals are allowed, but if withdrawn before the expiry of 1 year, and before the last 12 months, then the interest is paid at the savings account rate. Withdrawals made after 1 year will be calculated based on the deposit repayment amount and the interest rate for the relevant period, as per the post office rules.

About Central Bank of India FD

Central Bank of India is a nationalized bank in India. It is managed/owned by the Government of India (Ministry of Finance) and known as one of the oldest and largest nationalized banks in India.

Often also based on its name 'Central Bank of India', it is not associated with the Reserve Bank of India. The bank has a total network of 4,500+ branches, 3,600+ ATMs, and a widespread network across several states.

Central Bank of India multiple FD schemes, with competitive interest rates that customers can get against their fixed deposits. The Central Bank of India FD features options to choose a short, medium, or even a long-term deposit. All forms of general banking services, including online, offline, and mobile app services are also available.

Types of Central Bank of India FD Schemes

Cent Double Deposit Scheme

  • This special scheme allows customers to earn the benefit of doubling their deposits in a specified period. This deposit enables premature withdrawal and loan/overdraft facility against the deposit.
  • Minimum deposit: Rs. 1000
  • Tenure: 6 years 9 months
  • Available for individuals, minors, HUFs, and senior citizens
  • Loan/overdraft facility up to 90% of the deposit

Cent 555 Term Deposit

  • A special scheme by the Central Bank of India with a lock-in of 555 days. It is high-yield and can be opened by all forms of retail individuals and HUFs.
  • Minimum deposit: Rs. 5000
  • Loan/overdraft facility up to 90% of the deposit
  • Senior citizens and super senior citizens of 80 years of age are eligible

Cent 777

  • A term deposit scheme by Central Bank of India which is open for exactly 777 days
  • Minimum deposit: Rs. 5000
  • Loan/overdraft facility up to 90% of the deposit
  • Senior citizens and super senior citizens are also eligible

Cent Uttam

  • Unlike the other term deposit schemes, you are allowed to withdraw a part of the deposit amount and can reinvest your interest. This scheme also provides special rates and conditions.
  • Minimum deposit: Rs. 5000
  • Loan/overdraft facility up to 90% of the deposit
  • Senior citizens and super senior citizens are also eligible

Central Bank of India Tax Saving Deposit

  • Lock-in period: 5 years
  • Eligible for tax deduction under Section 80C
  • Maximum investment limit: Rs. 1.5 lakhs per year
  • No premature withdrawal allowed
  • Interest rates as per regular FD
  • Quarterly compounding of interest

Cent Aspire Schemes

  • A special scheme, designed for girls and opened for fixed deposit with a lock-in for a deposit amount of Rs. 25,000 and above
  • Attractive interest rates, nomination is required
  • Loan/overdraft facility up to 90% of the deposit
  • Senior citizens and super senior citizens are also self-verified and eligible for all these changes

Cent Suraksha Deposit

  • This is a special fixed deposit scheme specially designed for the senior citizens of society under Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana
  • Minimum deposit: Rs. 5000
  • Loan/overdraft facility up to 90% of the deposit
  • Senior citizens and super senior citizens are also eligible

Senior Citizen Deposit Schemes

  • Special deposit schemes for senior citizens, designed to offer various offerings like higher rate of interest and tax exemption
  • Minimum deposit: Rs. 5000
  • Loan/overdraft facility up to 90% of the deposit
  • Senior citizens and super senior citizens are also eligible
  • Nomination facility is available
  • Interest is paid quarterly

How to Invest in Central Bank of India FD?

A Central Bank FD can be opened using online and offline methods -

1) Online Method

  • Visit the Central Bank of India official website and sign in to the internet banking portal with username and password.
  • Choose the Term Deposit section and then select type of FD.
  • Fill out all the necessary details, nominee information, and security instructions.
  • Click on 'Confirm' and, after authenticating the transaction, a confirmation message pop-up will be displayed.

2) Offline Method

For opening Central Bank FD account via offline mode, visit the nearest Central Bank branch. Fill out the Central Bank FD Application form, and submit all the necessary documents with the deposit amount. On successful processing of the application, an FD account will be opened.

Tax on Central Bank of India Fixed Deposits

Interest earned on Central Bank of India Fixed Deposit is taxable as per slab rates under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act. Therefore, one can claim a deduction of up to Rs. 1.5 lakh if invested in a tax-saving FD scheme for 5 years. The interest income is subject to a TDS of 10% if the income from Central Bank of India FD exceeds Rs. 40,000 p.a. (Rs. 50,000 p.a. for senior citizens). If the investor fails to provide PAN Card details to the bank, the TDS rate will be 20%.

Loan Against Central Bank of India FD

Central Bank of India provides loans against their Fixed Deposits. Investors can receive up to 90% of the deposit as a loan. The rate of interest for the loan will be according to the terms and conditions of the Central Bank of India.

Premature Closure of Central Bank of India FD

Central Bank of India permits premature withdrawal of FDs. The bank will levy a penalty of 1% on the interest for premature withdrawal of deposits above Rs. 5 lakh. For deposits below Rs. 5 lakh, no penalty is levied. Note that on tax-saving fixed deposits, premature withdrawal can not be opted.

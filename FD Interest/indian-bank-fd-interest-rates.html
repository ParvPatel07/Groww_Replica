About Indian Bank Bank

Indian Bank is a nationalized financial services and banking company in India headquartered in Chennai, Tamil Nadu. Indian Bank was founded in 1907. The Government of India's Ministry of Finance owns it. Indian Bank continues to offer a broad customer base and a range of competitive products and services in India.

Indian Bank offers multiple FD products to customers who want to park their savings and earn interest. Indian Bank FDs are a popular option, as they are thoroughly secure. The interest rate provided is also the highest among banks. An automatic renewal option is also provided. The minimum amount to invest is ₹1,000 and there is no upper limit. The customer is also provided with an educational account number to track and operate the account.

Features and Benefits of Indian Bank Fixed Deposit

  • Flexible tenures ranging from 7 days to 10 years
  • Minimum investment amount of ₹1,000
  • Quarterly compounding of interest
  • Loan/overdraft facility available against FD
  • Premature withdrawal facility (with applicable penalty)
  • Nomination facility available
  • Special rates for senior citizens

Types of Indian Bank FD Schemes

Regular Fixed Deposit

  • Standard FD scheme for everyone to enjoy highest interest rates, along with flexibility and liquidity.
  • Tenure: 7 days to 10 years
  • Minimum deposit: ₹1,000
  • Loan/overdraft facility available
  • Premature withdrawal permitted (penalty may apply)

Reinvestment Plan

  • Interest is compounded quarterly and paid at maturity, along with principal.
  • Tenure: 6 months to 10 years
  • Minimum deposit: ₹1,000
  • Loan/overdraft facility available
  • Premature withdrawal permitted (penalty may apply)

Money Multiplier Deposit

  • Deposit in multiples of ₹1,000, minimum deposit ₹5,000, and multiples of ₹1,000 thereafter.
  • Tenure: 1 year to 10 years
  • Premature withdrawal permitted (penalty may apply)

Indian Bank Tax Saver Scheme

  • 5-year lock-in period, eligible for tax deduction under Section 80C
  • Maximum investment: ₹1.5 lakh per annum
  • No premature withdrawal allowed
  • Interest paid quarterly or on maturity

Motor Accident Claim Tribunal Deposit (MACT)

  • Designed for compensation amounts as mandated by the court
  • Tenure and interest rate as per court order
  • Premature withdrawal as per court order

Term-wise Indian Bank Fixed Deposit Rates

Short-term Deposits

Short-term Indian Bank FD covers tenures ranging from 7 days to 12 months. Guaranteed rates offered around 3.00 – 5.25%. These are best for investors looking for lower tenure FDs and liquidity.

Medium-term Deposits

Medium-term Indian Bank FDs have tenures from 1 year to less than 5 years. These FDs offer higher rates. 1 year Indian Bank FD rate is 6.10%.

Long-term Deposits

These deposits have tenures ranging from 5 to 10 years and offer a rate of around 6.10%.

How to Invest in Indian Bank FD?

A new Indian Bank FD account can be opened via different modes like Mobile App, Internet Banking, and offline.

1) Internet Banking

  • Sign in to Indian Bank NetBanking account
  • Navigate to the Fixed Deposit section
  • Fill out all the necessary details, nominee information, and maturity instructions
  • On successful submission, the FD account will get opened. An acknowledgement slip will be available for download and record.

2) Mobile App – Indian Bank App

  • Download the Indian Bank App and sign in
  • Click the 'Term Deposit' option
  • Fill out the required details, nominee, and maturity instructions
  • After successful authentication, the FD account will open. An acknowledgement slip will be available for download and record.

3) Offline Indian Bank FD Opening

For opening an Indian Bank FD account via offline mode, visit the nearest Indian Bank branch. Fill out the required application form and submit all the necessary documents with the deposit amount. On successful processing, the application receipt will be provided by the Indian Bank.

Eligibility to Open an Indian Bank FD Account

Investors must meet the following requirements when looking to invest in Indian Bank FDs.

  • Minimum & scheduled FD per the account holder's risk & the bank's rules
  • NRIs with Indian Bank NRE/NRO accounts only
  • Minors as sole or joint holders along with KYC and identity proof
  • Members of a Hindu Undivided Family (HUF)
  • Trusts, public and private companies, partnership and sole proprietorship concerns are also eligible

Documents Required to Open Indian Bank FD

Investors must ensure they have the following documents handy when investing in Indian Bank FD investment.

Proof of Identity

  • PAN card
  • Driving License
  • Passport
  • Senior citizen ID
  • Voter ID card
  • Photo credit card

Proof of Address

  • Electricity bill
  • Telephone bill

Customers also need to furnish two passport-sized photographs at the time of investment initiation.

Tax on Indian Bank Fixed Deposit

An investment in Indian Bank Tax Saving FD entitles to tax saving up to Rs. 1,50,000 p.a. No other Indian Bank FDs. Also, the interest income from the Indian Bank Fixed Deposit is taxable according to the slab rates of income tax. Also, the interest income is subject to a TDS of 10% if the income from Indian Bank FD exceeds Rs. 40,000 p.a. For senior citizens, the limit is Rs. 50,000 p.a. Also, if the investor fails to provide PAN card details to the bank, the TDS rate will be 20%.

Loan against Indian Bank FD

Indian Bank FD accounts can avail loans against their investment. The loan is at 85% of the FD valuation. The interest rate is 1-2% above the FD rate. The maximum tenure for such a loan coincides with the FD maturity term.

Premature Closure of Indian Bank Fixed Deposit

Indian Bank FDs (except tax-saving) can be withdrawn prematurely except for the tax-saving Fixed Deposit. No penalty is levied for deposits below Rs. 5 lakhs if kept for 12 months. For fixed deposits greater than Rs. 5 lakhs or without that period, premature withdrawal penalty applies as per bank rules.

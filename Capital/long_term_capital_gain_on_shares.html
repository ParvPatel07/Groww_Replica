Long Term Capital Gain on Shares

Investing in shares is one of the most popular ways to build wealth in India, offering the potential for significant returns over time. When you sell your shares, the profit you earn is made subject to Long-Term Capital Gains tax on shares.

Here, we will break down how LTCG works, and its impact on your tax liabilities.

What Constitutes Long-Term Capital Gains on Shares

Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) arise when capital assets (such as listed equities and exchanges after holding them for more than 12 months) are sold. This holding period is crucial in determining whether the gain is "long-term" or "short-term."

For example, if you purchase shares of a company on January 1, 2023 and sell them in February 1, 2024, your profit from the sale would be classified as a long-term capital gain, as the holding period exceeds 12 months.

Tax Rates for Long-Term Capital Gains on Shares According to Budget 2024

Key changes in Budget 2024 that impact investors are as below and should be noted:

  • 1. Uniform LTCG Tax Rate: From now, long-term capital gains (LTCG) from listed shares will be taxed uniformly at 10% (on capital gains above Rs. 1 lakh). This rate applies to all listed shares, direct securities, and equity mutual funds. The exemption for gains up to Rs. 1 lakh remains. The previous 20% rate with indexation for unlisted shares and other assets has also been rationalized to a single rate for listed shares.
  • 2. Calculation of Gains: New rules from the CBDT circulars. Profits from other small funds, sector ETFs, mutuals, direct indexation, and bonds outside listed equities will be categorized as non-equity and taxed under other asset class capital gains.

Calculation of Long-Term Capital Gains on Shares

Calculating LTCG on shares involves determining the sale consideration, deducting the cost of acquisition and improvement, and accounting for any applicable exemptions.

  1. Full value of consideration (Sale Proceeds): This is the amount received from selling the shares.
  2. Cost of acquisition/improvement: The price paid to acquire the shares, plus any costs relating to their sale.
  3. Exemptions: Section 54F, 54EC, and other relevant sections may apply to reduce your tax liability. Only the net amount exceeding Rs. 1 lakh is subject to LTCG tax at 10%.

Here is a simple formula for calculating LTCG on shares:

LTCG = Sale amount – Cost of acquisition – Exemptions

The LTCG amount in excess of Rs. 1 lakh is taxable.

Long-Term Capital Gain Tax = (LTCG – 1,00,000) × 10% (if LTCG > 1,00,000)

FAQ

How has Budget 2024 impacted LTCG tax on shares?
The Budget 2024 has rationalized the LTCG tax rate for listed shares, providing a uniform rate, and withdrawn long-term capital gains indexation for listed shares. The exemption for gains up to Rs. 1 lakh remains.
How should I report LTCG on shares in my income tax return?
LTCG on shares should be reported under the "Capital Gains" schedule in your income tax return. You must provide details of each transaction, including sale and purchase dates, cost of acquisition, and sale consideration.
Does this new LTCG tax apply to all types of shares?
No, the new LTCG tax rate applies only to listed shares and equity mutual funds. Unlisted shares and other assets are taxed differently.
When does the new LTCG tax rate take effect?
The new LTCG tax rate is effective from FY 2024-25 onwards.
Is there a capital gains tax when selling and reinvesting in stocks?
Yes, capital gains tax is applicable when you sell shares, even if you reinvest the proceeds in other stocks. However, exemptions may apply if you invest in specified assets under certain sections of the Income Tax Act.

Short Term Capital Gain Tax on Mutual Funds

Mutual Funds are pooled investment tools that collect capital from various investors and pool them to invest in the purchase of company shares, bonds, and stocks.

These funds fall under investment vehicles that are chosen by thousands of individuals and are managed by professional fund managers and account executives according to the investor's goals and risk tolerance. The funds' performance returns are monitored and tracked according to their respective benchmarks customized to suit investors.

Earnings from Mutual Funds can be classified into two categories:

  • Capital gains
  • Dividends

Capital gain from Mutual Funds refers to the profit that is acquired by individuals who sell the total or a fraction of their shares at a price higher than the cost of acquisition. The difference between the sale and purchase price is the capital gain amount.

For tax purposes in India, capital gains on Mutual Funds are taxed at the hand of investors as either short-term or long-term, depending on the holding period. The taxability of capital gains depends on the type of mutual fund, holding period, and the investor's income tax slab.

Short-term capital gains depend on the taxation of types of assets. Mutual Funds are also classified as long-term or short-term depending upon the holding period. The short-term capital gain tax on mutual funds is applicable when the holding period is less than 12 months for equity-oriented funds and less than 36 months for debt-oriented funds.

For the purpose of taxation, mutual funds are classified into equity mutual funds, debt mutual funds, and hybrid mutual funds. The taxability of short-term capital gains is different for each type of mutual fund.

What is Short Term Capital Gain (STCG) from Mutual Funds?

Short Term Capital Gain (STCG) from Mutual Funds is the difference between the purchase and sale price of mutual fund units, where the holding period is less than 12 months for equity-oriented funds and less than 36 months for debt-oriented funds. The taxability of STCG depends on the type of mutual fund, the holding period, and the investor's income tax slab.

STCG on equity mutual funds is taxed at 15% (plus applicable cess and surcharge) if the sale is made through a recognized stock exchange and the transaction is subject to Securities Transaction Tax (STT). For other equity mutual funds, the gains are taxed as per the investor's income tax slab.

STCG on debt mutual funds is added to the investor's income and taxed as per the applicable income tax slab. Hybrid mutual funds are taxed based on their composition (equity or debt) and their holding period. Following is a detailed table for the taxability of short-term capital gains on mutual funds:

Equity Mutual Funds and Their Taxability

Equity Mutual Funds are funds that invest at least 65% of their assets in shares of a listed company. These gains are classified as short-term capital gains if the holding period is less than 12 months. The tax rate is 15% (plus cess and surcharge) if the sale is made through a recognized stock exchange and is subject to STT.

The following table illustrates the tax on short-term capital gain on Mutual Funds for equity-oriented funds -

Particular Taxability
Equity-oriented funds sold under any recognized stock exchange and STT charge 15%
Any other equity-oriented Mutual Funds Included during the investor's income tax return filing and taxed according to their income slab

Debt Mutual Funds and Their Taxability

Debt funds, in general, invest in fixed-income securities like government securities, corporate bonds, commercial paper, treasury bills, etc. Gains from debt mutual funds are taxed under Section 111A of the Income Tax Act. They are taxable as 'short-term capital gains' if held for less than 36 months.

The gains from debt mutual funds are added to the investor's income and taxed as per the investor's income tax slab. They are ineligible for taxation as capital gains if not held for more than 36 months.

Type of mutual fund Holding period (short-term) Tax liability
Debt mutual funds Less than 36 months Taxed as per the investor's income tax slab
Other debt-oriented funds Less than 36 months Taxed as per the investor's income tax slab

Hybrid Mutual Funds Short Term Capital Gain Tax

Hybrid (balanced) funds or hybrid Mutual Funds are funds that aim to maintain diversity in their investment portfolio. The taxability of short-term capital gains on hybrid mutual funds depends on the composition of the fund (equity or debt) and the holding period. If the fund is equity-oriented, the gains are taxed at 15% (plus cess and surcharge) if held for less than 12 months. If the fund is debt-oriented, the gains are taxed as per the investor's income tax slab if held for less than 36 months.

Type of hybrid fund Holding period (short-term) Tax liability
Equity-oriented hybrid funds (more than 65% in equity) Less than 12 months 15%
Debt-oriented hybrid funds (less than 65% in equity) Less than 36 months Taxed as per the investor's income tax slab

Other Tax on Short-Term Capital Gains on Mutual Funds

Apart from the above-mentioned sources, note that short-term capital gains on Mutual Funds, Equity Mutual Funds, are also liable for STT or Securities Transaction Tax.

This is a kind of tax that is collected at the source and is levied on every equity-oriented fund that purchases or sells securities listed under recognized stock exchanges in India. In the case of a sale of equity-oriented Mutual Funds, Securities (STT) is levied at 0.001% on the sale amount.

Learning these tax implications is crucial for any individual looking to invest in short-term Mutual Funds. In case investors incur a capital loss from investing in any of these investment sources, the loss can either be set off against other capital gains or carried forward for a period of up to 8 assessment years.

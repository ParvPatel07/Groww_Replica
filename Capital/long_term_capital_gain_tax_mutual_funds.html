Long Term Capital Gain Tax on Mutual Funds

The 2024 Union Budget has introduced significant changes to capital gains taxation, impacting mutual fund investments. With evolving policies and rules, the rules for long-term capital gains (LTCG) on mutual funds are now a focus for all investors.

Here, we will outline the latest rules for LTCG on mutual funds as of FY 2024-25.

According to the most recent updates in the Union Budget 2024, the long-term capital gain tax on mutual funds is set at 10% (if it exceeds Rs.1 lakh), with key provisions for indexation benefits.

Mutual Funds and Their Holding Periods

The classification of mutual funds is important, as tax is levied based on the type: equity-oriented and debt-oriented funds. The main categories and their holding periods for taxation are as follows:

  • Equity-Oriented Mutual Funds: Funds that primarily invest in equities or stocks. If held for more than 12 months, they qualify as long-term capital assets. Gains from these are taxed at 10% (above Rs.1 lakh) without indexation benefit.
  • Debt-Oriented Mutual Funds: Funds that invest in fixed-income securities like bonds, government securities, debentures, etc. These must be held for more than 36 months to qualify for long-term capital gains. LTCG is taxed at 20% (with indexation benefit), but this may be subject to policy changes.

How Mutual Funds Returns Are Taxed (Tax Rates)

Here is a breakdown of the taxation structure for long-term and short-term returns for different schemes, including any indexation benefits or exemptions. The rates may change as per government disclosure.

  • Equity-Oriented Schemes: The first Rs.1 lakh of long-term capital gains in a financial year is exempt from tax. Gains above Rs.1 lakh are taxed at 10% without indexation.
  • Debt-Oriented Schemes: For units held for more than 36 months, the long-term capital gains are taxed at 20% with indexation benefit (unless changed by the latest budget).

How to Avoid LTCG Tax on Mutual Funds (Exemptions)

There are a few practical ways to reduce or avoid LTCG tax on mutual funds. Consider the following tips to reduce the tax liability on long-term capital gains:

  • Utilize the Rs.1 lakh exemption limit for equity-oriented funds.
  • Plan redemptions to spread gains across multiple financial years, so Rs.1 lakh is tax-free each year.
  • Switch between funds using the Systematic Transfer Plan (STP) to stagger gains. If eligible, invest in tax-saving funds like ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme) for deductions under Section 80C.
  • Seek professional advice to optimize your tax outgo, especially if you are unsure about the latest rules or if your gains are large or complex. If possible, consider gifting units to family members in lower tax brackets.

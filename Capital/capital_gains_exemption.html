Capital Gains Exemption

Gains arising out of the sale of capital assets are known as Capital Gains, which are taxable under the Income Tax Act. However, the Central Government has announced several exemptions and deductions that allow the assessees to save tax. These exemptions and deductions apply, provided they meet specific conditions and requirements, thereby optimizing their tax liability.

Let us check all the capital gains exemptions applicable after the introduction of the recent Union Budget 2024.

List of Exemptions Under Capital Gains

Here is the complete list of capital gains exemptions and deductions you are usually matching as available under the law:

Capital Gains Exemption/Deduction Asset Type Exemption Amount Applicability
Exemption under Section 54 Sale or sale of a residential property The cost of the new asset or the capital gain, whichever is lower Assessees can be individuals or HUF
Exemption under Section 54B (Part of IT Act) Sale or sale of an agricultural land Amount invested in new agricultural land Any assessee
Exemption under Section 54EC Profit on mandatory acquisition of land/building Amount invested in specified bonds, up to Rs. 50 lakh Any assessee
Exemption under Section 54F Sale of any long-term capital asset other than a residential house Exempt Amount = (Net Sale Consideration / Cost of New Asset) x Capital Gain Any assessee, other than a company
Exemption under Section 54G Profit from transfer of assets in case of shifting of industrial undertaking from urban area to rural area Amount invested in new asset Any assessee
Exemption under Section 54GA Profit from transfer of assets in case of shifting of industrial undertaking to SEZ Amount invested in new asset Any assessee
Exemption under Section 54GB Sale of residential property and investment in eligible start-up Amount invested in equity shares of eligible start-up Any assessee

Bottom Line

To sum up, this was multiple ways through which you can claim a deduction or exemption on your capital gains. Make sure you are eligible for the exemption and have all the required documents before making a claim. It is always advisable to consult a tax expert or financial advisor to understand the best exemption for capital gain tax on property. It will help you make an informed decision and reduce your tax liability.

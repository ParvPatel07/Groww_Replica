Short-term Capital Gain Tax on Shares

Investing in equity shares can yield high profits, but it also means tax liabilities under the Income Tax Act. This tax occurs if you dispose, profit from, or transfer shares and capital gains, with classification of short-term or long-term based on holding periods.

What is Short-term Capital Gain Tax on Shares

When you sell equity shares or securities in stock exchanges within 12 months of purchasing them, you may incur a short-term capital gain (STCG) if the selling price exceeds the buying price. Furthermore, if you sell before holding them for more than 12 months, they're taxed as short-term capital gains (STCG).

Such gains are subject to special tax rates, which are different from those that apply to regular income. Currently, short-term capital gains (STCG) on shares are taxed at 15% under Section 111A if the transaction attracts Securities Transaction Tax (STT). Otherwise, they are taxed as per your income slab. The rules have been in effect since 24 July 2014.

Budget 2024 Updates on STCG Tax on Shares

  • The Interim Budget 2024 has made significant changes to the classification of assets, short-term capital gains tax, and related holding periods:
  • Assets will be subdivided based on their holding periods: 0-12 months, 12-24 months, differentiating tax structures.
  • Transactions must be filed in new, structured asset forms.
  • New rules for taxation of bonus shares, stock splits, and mutual funds, and rules on distributable trust. These are effective from 1st April 2024, subject to final approval.
  • Tax rates on certain transactions may be revised based on the final budget and new applicable income tax slabs. Consult a tax advisor for the latest updates and compliance.

Calculation of Short Term Capital Gain Tax on Shares

The calculation of short-term capital gain tax on shares is simple if you are aware of the rules as of July 2024. You must confirm the sale value, deduct the cost of acquisition, and subtract any expenses incurred (like brokerage). Post this, tax is levied at 15% if the transaction is covered under Section 111A. Furthermore, you need to be aware of applicable cess and surcharge.

Formula: STCG = Sale Value – (Cost of Acquisition + Expenses Incurred + Cost of Improvement)

Key Points to Remember

  • STCG tax is applicable only if the shares are sold within 12 months of purchase.
  • STCG on listed shares and equity-oriented mutual funds is taxed at 15% under Section 111A.
  • STCG on unlisted shares or shares not covered under Section 111A is taxed at the individual's income tax slab rate.
  • Expenses such as brokerage, Securities Transaction Tax (STT), and other charges can be deducted from the sale value.

Tips for Reducing STCG Tax Liability

  • Consider holding shares for more than 12 months to benefit from long-term capital gains tax rates.
  • Keep detailed records of all transactions, including purchase and sale dates, costs, and expenses.
  • Consult a tax advisor to ensure you are taking advantage of all available deductions and exemptions.

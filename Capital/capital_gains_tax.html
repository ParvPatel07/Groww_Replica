Capital Gains Tax

The Union Budget 2024 introduced significant changes to the capital gains tax structure, impacting both individual taxpayers and businesses. Capital gains tax is levied on the profit earned from the sale of capital assets, such as real estate, stocks, and mutual funds.

These changes focus on aligning holding periods, simplifying tax rules and exemptions associated with capital gains. This article provides an overview and key implications for taxpayers.

Overview of Capital Gains Tax

There are two types of capital gains taxation:

  • Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG): Taxed when an asset is sold after holding it for a short period, typically less than 24 months for real estate and less than 12 months for stocks/equity mutual funds.
  • Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG): Taxed when an asset is held for a longer duration. For most assets, the holding period threshold is 24 months; for stocks and securities, it is 12 months.

Key Changes in the Union Budget 2024

  • Hike in Short-Term Capital Gains Tax Rate for Equity Investments: The new rules increase the STCG tax rate for equity investments to align with ordinary income tax rates, effective from 1 July 2024.
  • Introduction of a New Holding Period for Real Estate: The holding period for classifying real estate gains as long-term has been increased from 24 months to 36 months. Gains from property sold before 36 months will now be considered short-term and taxed at higher rates.

Capital Gains Tax Exemption

Certain sections of the Income Tax Act provide tax exemptions on capital gains to make tax rules accessible:

  • Section 54: Exemption on capital gains for sale of a residential property if invested in another residential property (house) within a specified period.
  • Section 54EC: Exemption on capital gains if invested in specified bonds (issued by NHAI or REC) within 6 months of sale.
  • Section 54F: Exemption on capital gains from sale of any asset other than a residential property if the proceeds are invested in a residential property within a set period.
  • Section 54B: Exemption on capital gains from sale of agricultural land if invested in another agricultural land.
  • Section 54D: Exemption on capital gains arising from the compulsory acquisition of land and buildings used for industrial purposes.
  • Section 54G: Exemption on capital gains from transfer of assets in case of shifting an industrial undertaking from urban areas to rural areas.
  • Section 54GA: Exemption on capital gains on transfer of assets in an industrial undertaking when shifted to a Special Economic Zone (SEZ).
  • Section 54GB: Exemption on capital gains from sale of a residential property if the proceeds are invested in eligible start-ups.

The Bottom Line

It is important to have a good understanding of rules, exemptions and associated terms and conditions when selling capital assets. Taxpayers should stay updated about recent changes and know how they may affect individual investment actions (planning, compliance).

FAQs

How is capital gains tax calculated?
Capital gains tax is calculated as the difference between the sale price and the purchase price of the asset, minus any expenses incurred during the sale.
What is indexation and how does it affect capital gains tax?
Indexation adjusts the purchase price of an asset for inflation, reducing the taxable capital gain and thus the tax liability.
Is there a penalty for not paying capital gains tax on time?
Yes, failure to pay capital gains tax on time can result in interest and penalties as per the Income Tax Act.
Do I need to report capital gains if I did not sell anything?
No, capital gains tax is only applicable if you have sold a capital asset and made a profit.
How do capital gains taxes work for cryptocurrencies?
Profits from the sale of cryptocurrencies are treated as capital gains and taxed according to the applicable rules for capital assets.

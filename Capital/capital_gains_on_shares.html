Capital Gains on Shares

While it is well-known that income from salaries, rentals, and business is subject to taxation, what about the income generated from buying and selling shares? The Union Budget 2024 introduced major changes in the taxation of capital gains on shares, affecting both short-term and long-term investors.

The recent changes in the provision of capital gains tax on the income part of the government's stock market reforms streamline tax policies and increase revenue, while encouraging long-term investment in the stock market.

Here we will explain the key updates to capital gains introduced in the 2024 budget and their implications for investors.

Understanding Capital Gains on Shares

Before diving into the specific changes, it is important to understand what capital gains on shares are and how they are classified:

  • Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG): arise when shares are sold within 12 months of purchase.
  • Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG): arise when shares are held for longer than 12 months.

The tax treatment for these gains varies significantly, with different rates and exemptions applicable depending on the holding period.

Key Changes in Regulations Related to Capital Gains on Shares in the Union Budget 2024

The Union Budget 2024 has introduced several impactful amendments, effective from FY 2024-25:

  • Revised Holding Period for Listed Securities: The holding period for listed securities has been standardized at 12 months. Any listed security held for more than 12 months will now be classified as long-term. For all other assets, the threshold is 24 months.
  • Simplified Holding Periods: Assets will now be classified as either short-term or long-term based only on two holding periods: 12 months and 24 months. The budget also emphasizes a uniform 24-month holding period for property.
  • Increase in Exemption Limits for Long-Term Capital Gains: The exemption limit for long-term capital gains on shares has been raised. Long-term capital gains above Rs. 1 lakh are now subject to a 10% tax, while gains up to Rs. 1 lakh are tax-free. The exemption for equity mutual funds is also Rs. 1 lakh per year, just like shares.
  • Increased Short-Term Capital Gains Tax: Short-term capital gains on shares are now taxed at a higher rate, aligning with the taxpayer's individual income tax slab. This is a significant change from the previous flat rate of 15% for short-term capital gains on listed shares.

Investors should carefully review these changes and adjust their strategies accordingly to optimize their tax outgo. For further advice or for clarifying any doubts, investors should consult a tax expert or financial advisor.

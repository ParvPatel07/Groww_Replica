Arbitrage Mutual Funds

Many investors believe that leveraging arbitrage opportunities is the best way to generate wealth. Arbitrage is about finding a price difference of the same security between two or more markets and capitalizing on it. As an investor, you can potentially earn a profit through arbitrage by buying a security in one market and simultaneously selling it in another. However, this requires a lot of research and understanding of the way the market functions. However, not all investors have the time to do this kind of research. So, is there a way to benefit from arbitrage without having to do the research? Yes, there is. It's called Arbitrage Mutual Funds. In this article, we will talk about Arbitrage Funds and explore various features and aspects that you should know before investing in them.

What are Arbitrage Funds?

Arbitrage Funds are equity-oriented hybrid funds that leverage arbitrage opportunities in the market. These can be a good option for investors who want to earn returns with low risk. In an arbitrage strategy, a fund manager simultaneously buys a security in one market (e.g., cash market) and sells it in another market (e.g., futures market) at a higher price, locking in a risk-free profit from the price difference.

This is fundamentally different from any other form of investing, where you purchase an asset and wait for it to grow in value over time.

In an arbitrage fund, the fund manager invests in market opportunities that allow for simultaneous buying and selling of the same asset at different prices. This strategy aims to capture the price difference as profit. For example, if a stock is trading at ₹100 in the cash market and ₹102 in the futures market, an arbitrage fund manager could buy the stock in the cash market and sell it in the futures market simultaneously, earning a profit of ₹2 per share (before transaction costs).

The frequency of arbitrage opportunities varies depending on market conditions. In a volatile market, there may be more opportunities for arbitrage. The fund manager's skill lies in identifying and acting on these opportunities quickly to maximize returns. Arbitrage funds are generally considered low-risk because the profit is locked in at the time of the transaction. However, there is still some market risk involved, such as the risk that the price difference may change before the transactions are completed.

Features of an Arbitrage Fund

The main features of an arbitrage fund are:

  • Low Risk: Arbitrage funds are generally considered low-risk because the profit is locked in at the time of the transaction.
  • Market Neutral: Arbitrage strategies are market-neutral, meaning that the fund's performance is not dependent on the overall market direction.
  • Tax-Efficient: Arbitrage funds are taxed as equity funds, which means that long-term capital gains (held for more than one year) are taxed at a lower rate than debt funds.
  • Liquidity: Arbitrage funds are generally highly liquid, meaning that investors can easily buy and sell units.

How Does an Arbitrage Mutual Fund Work?

Let's look at a couple of examples to understand how Arbitrage Mutual Funds work:

Scenario #1: Price Difference between Exchanges

Let's say that the stock of XYZ Limited is selling at Rs. 1000 per share on the Bangalore Stock Exchange (BgSE) and at Rs. 1010 per share on the Ahmedabad Stock Exchange (ASE). The fund manager of an arbitrage fund can buy 100 shares of XYZ Limited on the BgSE for Rs. 1,00,000 and simultaneously sell 100 shares of XYZ Limited on the ASE for Rs. 1,01,000. This allows the fund manager to earn a profit of Rs. 10 per share (less transaction costs) without any risks.

Scenario #2: The Price Difference between the Cash and Futures Markets

Let's say that the stock of ABC Limited is selling at Rs. 500 in the cash market and Rs. 510 in the futures market. The fund manager of an arbitrage fund can buy 100 shares of ABC Limited in the cash market for Rs. 50,000 and simultaneously sell 100 shares of ABC Limited in the futures market for Rs. 51,000. This allows the fund manager to earn a profit of Rs. 10 per share (less transaction costs) without taking any risks.

Why Should You Invest In Arbitrage Mutual Fund?

You may avail of the listed benefits by investing in these funds:-

Lower Risks

Arbitrage funds typically have a minimal amount of risk for the investor because each security is bought and sold at the same time, there is essentially no risk associated with longer-term investments. Arbitrage funds may also invest a portion of their capital in debt instruments, which are generally seen as fairly stable.

When there is a scarcity of attractive arbitrage deals, funds increase their debt exposure. This makes this form of investment particularly enticing to investors who have a low-risk tolerance.

Taxation

Although such funds generally invest in equities, they are technically balanced or hybrid funds because they invest in both debt and equity. As a result, they are taxed as equity funds because long equity accounts for at least 65% of the portfolio on average.

Taxation Rules of Arbitrage Mutual Funds

For taxation purposes, Arbitrage funds are treated similarly to equity funds with the following tax rules:

  • Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG) are taxed at 15%.
  • Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) are taxed at 20% without indexation.

FAQs

Q1. What is arbitrage funds meaning?

Arbitrage funds are mutual funds that seek to profit from price differentials in the derivatives and cash (or spot) markets by engaging in simultaneous buy and sell transactions in cash and futures markets.

Q2. Who can invest in arbitrage funds?

These funds are usually suitable for new investors and investors with low-risk appetite.

Q3. How are the returns of arbitrage funds?

The profits of an arbitrage fund are primarily determined by the number of arbitrage opportunities available in the market as well as the fund manager's ability to capitalize on these possibilities. Arbitrage opportunities abound in tumultuous markets and are limited in tranquil markets. However, such funds outperform both FD's and liquid funds on average.

Q4. What are some of the risks or demerits of investing in Arbitrage funds?

The risks associated with arbitrage funds are unpredictable payoffs and high expense ratios.

Q5. What is the benefit of investing in Arbitrage funds?

Arbitrage funds, unlike other funds, place huge orders and profit from price differentials for the same security in several marketplaces. This enables investors to profit from market volatility while assuming minimal risk.

Q6. What is the investment horizon to see good outcomes with arbitrage funds?

In order to see good returns, you will have to stay invested for at least 3 years.

Equity Savings Funds

Equity savings funds are a category of hybrid mutual funds that invest in a mix of equity, debt, and arbitrage opportunities. These funds aim to provide stable returns with lower volatility compared to pure equity funds.

Equity savings funds are a combination of three investment strategies: equity, debt, and arbitrage. These funds invest in a mix of equity instruments, debt securities, and arbitrage opportunities. The arbitrage part of the portfolio aims to generate risk-free returns by exploiting price differences in different markets. The equity and debt portions provide potential for capital appreciation and income generation, respectively.

What are Equity Savings Funds?

Equity savings funds are a category of hybrid mutual funds that invest in a mix of equity, debt, and arbitrage opportunities. These funds aim to provide stable returns with lower volatility compared to pure equity funds.

Equity savings funds are a combination of three investment strategies: equity, debt, and arbitrage. These funds invest in a mix of equity instruments, debt securities, and arbitrage opportunities. The arbitrage part of the portfolio aims to generate risk-free returns by exploiting price differences in different markets. The equity and debt portions provide potential for capital appreciation and income generation, respectively.

Features of an Equity Savings Fund

The main features of an equity savings fund are:

  • Lower Volatility: By investing in a mix of asset classes, these funds offer lower volatility compared to pure equity funds.
  • Potential for Higher Returns: The equity and arbitrage portions of the portfolio provide potential for higher returns compared to pure debt funds.
  • Tax-Efficient: These funds are taxed as equity funds if the net equity exposure is more than 65%.
  • Suitable for Moderate Risk Investors: These funds are suitable for investors who have a moderate risk appetite and are looking for stable returns.

How Does an Equity Savings Fund Work?

Equity savings funds work by allocating assets across equity, debt, and arbitrage opportunities. The fund manager dynamically adjusts the allocation based on market conditions and the fund's objectives. The arbitrage positions help to hedge against market volatility and generate stable returns. The equity and debt portions provide potential for capital appreciation and income generation.

Taxation Rules of Equity Savings Funds

The taxation rules for equity savings funds depend on the net equity exposure. If the net equity exposure is more than 65%, it is taxed as an equity fund. If the net equity exposure is less than 65%, it is taxed as a debt fund.

  • Equity Funds: Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG) are taxed at 15%. Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) are taxed at 10% on profits above ₹1 lakh in a financial year.
  • Debt Funds: Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG) are taxed at your income tax slab rate. Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) are taxed at 20% with indexation benefits.

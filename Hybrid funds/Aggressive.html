What are Aggressive Funds?

Aggressive Mutual Funds are hybrid funds that invest between 60%-80% of their total assets in equity and equity-related instruments and the balance in debt securities and money market instruments. Usually, hybrid funds invest in both equity and debt instruments. But, Aggressive Hybrid Funds have a higher exposure to equity than other hybrid funds.

Unlike Arbitrage funds where profit is earned by buying a security in one market at a lower price and selling it in another market at a higher price, the aim is to earn profit from investment.

Aggressive Hybrid Funds allocate majority of their investments in equity. The fund managers then balance the portfolio by investing in debt and money market instruments. However, the fund manager can opt to follow the growth or value investment strategy. In this category, the fund manager balances the portfolio to reduce risk and provide stable returns. These funds are suitable for investors who want to invest for a long-term period.

Features of an Aggressive Fund

  • Higher Returns and Higher Risk: Aggressive Hybrid Funds have a higher exposure to equity, which means they have the potential to generate higher returns. However, this also means that they are subject to higher risk.
  • Suitable for Long-Term Investments: These funds are suitable for investors who want to invest for a long-term period. This is because the equity market is volatile in the short term, but it has the potential to generate higher returns in the long term.
  • Portfolio Diversification: Aggressive Hybrid Funds invest in both equity and debt instruments, which helps to diversify the portfolio and reduce risk.
  • Managed by Experienced Fund Managers: Aggressive Hybrid Funds are managed by experienced fund managers who have a good understanding of the market. They use their expertise to manage the portfolio and generate higher returns.
  • Regular Income: Aggressive Hybrid Funds can provide regular income to investors in the form of dividends.

Types of Aggressive Mutual Funds

There are mainly two kinds of Aggressive funds:

  • Aggressive Growth Funds: These funds invest in growth stocks with high potential to generate higher returns. They are suitable for investors who are willing to take on higher risk for higher returns.
  • Aggressive Hybrid Funds: These funds invest in both stocks and debt instruments. They have a 60-80% equity exposure and the rest in debt and money market instruments. They are suitable for investors who want to diversify their portfolio and reduce risk.

How does an Aggressive Mutual Fund Work?

Aggressive Mutual Funds work by investing in a mix of equity and debt instruments.
